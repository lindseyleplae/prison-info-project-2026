The Georgia Department of Corrections (GDC) runs one of the country's largest state prison systems — about **34 state prisons**, **four private prisons** (run by CoreCivic and The GEO Group under state contract), and a separate tier of smaller facilities, holding roughly **47,000–50,000 people**. GDC's own materials mostly use the word **offender**; this site uses neutral terms. The agency also supervises probation and parole, and divides its prisons into three regions (North, Southeast, and Southwest).

Where a person is held turns first on **sentence length and who runs the facility**. A **felony state sentence** places a person in **GDC custody**; **pretrial detention and short local sentences** are served in a **county or city jail** run by a sheriff. Two further tiers hold or serve some state-sentenced people: about **21 county correctional institutions** (county-operated prisons) and a set of **transitional centers** (work-release/reentry) and **probation detention centers**. A newly sentenced person often waits in the **county jail** for weeks — already "in GDC custody" on paper — before being picked up.

**Newly received people are processed at a diagnostic center first** — men at the **Georgia Diagnostic and Classification State Prison (GDCP)** in Jackson, and women at **Lee Arrendale State Prison** in Alto — for about ten business days of medical, mental-health, and security screening before transfer to a permanent prison. GDC generally does **not allow visits during diagnostic status**, so families usually wait for the permanent-facility assignment before applying to visit. Georgia has an **active death penalty**: men under a death sentence and the **state execution chamber** are at GDCP, and women under a death sentence are held at Lee Arrendale. **Augusta State Medical Prison** is the system's statewide medical hub.

To find where someone is held, search **GDC's Find an Offender** tool by name or GDC ID number (records under the First Offender Act are not published). If a person is not listed, they may be in a **county or city jail** — search that county sheriff's jail roster.
