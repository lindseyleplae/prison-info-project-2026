The Mississippi Department of Corrections holds people in custody across three different kinds of facility, and which kind holds a person changes the rules that apply. **State prisons** — seven of them, including Mississippi State Penitentiary at Parchman, Central Mississippi Correctional Facility at Pearl, and South Mississippi Correctional Institution at Leakesville — run under MDOC's statewide rules. **Two privately operated prisons** (East Mississippi at Meridian and Wilkinson County at Woodville) and **fifteen county-run regional facilities** hold state-sentenced people too, but set their own visiting schedules, and some use different phone and money vendors. A sentence is served in state custody unless a court orders up to 24 months in an approved county jail when state space is unavailable.

Central Mississippi Correctional Facility in Pearl is the system's classification hub — the first stop for most people entering MDOC — and holds the state's women (administered as the Mississippi Correctional Institute for Women on the same campus) and people under 18. The MDOC inmate search on mdoc.ms.gov shows where a person is held; the facility's "type" — State, Private, or Regional — tells a family which rulebook applies.
