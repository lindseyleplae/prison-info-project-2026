:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**The dividing line is the offense.** A felony sentence to state prison — generally **more than a year** — places a person in **ODRC** custody; pretrial detention and short or misdemeanor sentences are served in a **county jail** run by a sheriff.

**New arrivals go through a reception center** for medical, mental-health, and security and program classification, then transfer to a permanent prison.

**Reception centers are sorted by county and sex** — men from most of Ohio go to the **Correctional Reception Center (CRC)** in **Orient**; men from **Stark, Summit, and Cuyahoga** counties go to **Lorain Correctional Institution** in **Grafton**; women go to the **Ohio Reformatory for Women** in **Marysville**.

**Find someone with the ODRC Offender Search** (by name or number); county-jail inmates are on the **sheriff's roster**, and federal prisoners are in the **Bureau of Prisons locator**.
:::

## State prison or county jail

Whether someone is in an **ODRC state prison** or a **county jail** depends on the case. A **felony sentence to state prison** — generally **more than a year** — places a person in **ODRC** custody. **Pretrial detention** and **short or misdemeanor sentences** are served in a **county jail** run by one of Ohio's **88 county sheriffs** — not ODRC, and not in the ODRC locator. Each jail sets its own visiting, mail, phone, and money rules; this guide and the Ohio guides cover the **ODRC state system**.

## Reception and classification

After **sentencing**, a person is taken from the **county jail** to an **ODRC reception center** for **medical, mental-health, and security and program classification**, then transfers to a **permanent prison**. Which reception center depends on **county and sex**:

- **Men, most counties:** the **Correctional Reception Center (CRC)** in **Orient**.
- **Men from Stark, Summit, and Cuyahoga counties:** **Lorain Correctional Institution** in **Grafton**.
- **Women:** the **Ohio Reformatory for Women** in **Marysville**.

:::reality-check
**Visiting is restricted during reception.** Under ODRC's visitation policy, reception visiting is limited to a **short list of immediate family and a support person**, and the person will **move to a different prison after classification**. The reception center is not where the person will stay, so a visit plan is built around the **permanent facility**, not the reception center.
:::

## Finding someone

State prisoners appear in the **ODRC Offender Search**, which searches by **name or number**. Ohio has **no statewide county-jail locator** — a person in a county jail is found through that **specific county sheriff's jail roster**, or by **calling that jail**. **Federal prisoners** are in the **Bureau of Prisons locator**.

## Transfers and current location

ODRC does **not generally notify families** when a person is **transferred between prisons**. Check the **Offender Search** for the current location. This also matters because the **visiting application and the visit reservation are tied to the specific facility**, so the current prison determines where the application goes and where the reservation is made.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Check the ODRC Offender Search first; if the person is not listed, they may be in a county jail — there is no statewide county-jail locator, so check the specific county sheriff's roster or call that jail, and check the Bureau of Prisons locator for federal custody. Expect a newly sentenced person to pass through a reception center — CRC in Orient for men from most counties, Lorain in Grafton for men from Stark, Summit, and Cuyahoga counties, and the Ohio Reformatory for Women in Marysville for women — before moving to a permanent prison, with visiting restricted during reception. Because ODRC does not generally notify families of transfers, confirm the current location on the Offender Search before traveling and before sending a visiting application or making a reservation, since both are tied to the specific facility.
:::
