:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**New arrivals go through a reception and classification (diagnostic) center first** — **men to the Bledsoe County Correctional Complex** in Pikeville and **women to the Debra K. Johnson Rehabilitation Center** in Nashville (formerly the Tennessee Prison for Women) — for medical and mental-health screening, a custody/security classification, and program assessment before assignment to a permanent prison.

**The listed prison can change after classification.** Because the first prison is the reception center, the facility shown in the locator can change once the person is classified and transferred.

**Find someone with the Felony Offender Information Lookup (FOIL)** at [foil.app.tn.gov/foil](https://foil.app.tn.gov/foil/), searching by **name or TDOC ID number**. FOIL covers people in **TDOC custody** (state-sentenced), not county-jail detainees.

**The four CoreCivic-operated prisons** — **Trousdale Turner, South Central, Hardeman County, and Whiteville** — hold TDOC-sentenced people and appear in FOIL the same way as state-run prisons.
:::

## Reception and classification

After **sentencing**, a newly committed person goes to a **reception and classification (diagnostic) center** for **medical and mental-health screening**, a **custody/security classification**, and a **program assessment**, then transfers to a **permanent prison**. Which center depends on **sex**:

- **Men:** the **Bledsoe County Correctional Complex** (Pikeville).
- **Women:** the **Debra K. Johnson Rehabilitation Center** (Nashville), formerly the Tennessee Prison for Women.

During **reception**, **visiting and other privileges are limited**, and the prison shown in the locator can **change** once the person is **classified and transferred** to a permanent facility.

:::reality-check
**The first prison is not where the person will stay.** Because the reception and classification center is the **first** facility, the prison shown in FOIL can **change** after the person is classified and moved to a permanent assignment. A [visit](/states/tennessee/guides/visiting) and a [mail](/states/tennessee/guides/mail) address are tied to the **current** facility, not the reception center.
:::

## State prison or county jail

**FOIL** covers people in **TDOC custody** — those serving **state-sentenced time** — including in the four **CoreCivic-operated prisons**: **Trousdale Turner**, **South Central**, **Hardeman County**, and **Whiteville**. These four hold TDOC-sentenced people and appear in **FOIL** the same way as **state-run prisons**.

A person sentenced to **state time** may still be **physically held in a county jail** awaiting transfer to a TDOC prison — known as a **"backup."** **County jails are a separate system** and are **not in FOIL**. To locate someone in a county jail, **search that county's roster**.

## Finding someone

To find where someone is held, use the **Felony Offender Information Lookup (FOIL)** at **[foil.app.tn.gov/foil](https://foil.app.tn.gov/foil/)**, searching by **name or TDOC ID number**. FOIL covers people in **TDOC custody** (**state-sentenced**), **not** county-jail detainees. A person sentenced to state time who is **not yet listed** in FOIL may still be a **backup** in a **county jail** awaiting transfer; in that case, **search the county's roster**.

## Transfers and current location

A person may be **transferred again** later in the sentence for **custody, medical, program, or disciplinary reasons**. Transfers are **TDOC decisions**, and **families are not always notified in advance**. Because the **location can change** with transfers, **confirm the current facility on FOIL** before traveling, scheduling a visit, or sending mail.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the current location and status on FOIL at foil.app.tn.gov/foil before traveling, scheduling a visit, or sending mail, searching by name or TDOC ID number. A newly committed person passes through a reception and classification center — the Bledsoe County Correctional Complex for men, the Debra K. Johnson Rehabilitation Center for women — before moving to a permanent prison, so the listed facility can change once the person is classified and transferred. TDOC may transfer a person again for custody, medical, program, or disciplinary reasons, and transfer timing and notification are set by TDOC. FOIL covers TDOC-sentenced people, including in the four CoreCivic-operated prisons (Trousdale Turner, South Central, Hardeman County, Whiteville), but not county-jail detainees; a person sentenced to state time may be held as a backup in a county jail awaiting transfer, so if they are not listed in FOIL, search that county's roster.
:::
