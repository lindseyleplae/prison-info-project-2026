:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Incoming personal mail goes to the prison, but the person receives a photocopy.** Each prison's own mailroom opens and **photocopies general correspondence in black and white**, then delivers the copy — the incarcerated person receives a copy, not the original. Indiana does **not** use an off-site mail vendor.

**Address mail to the person at the facility where they are held** — full name, IDOC number, and the prison's mailing address. Confirm the current location on the offender locator first.

**Legal mail is handled separately** — it goes to the facility and is opened and inspected in the incarcerated person's presence, not photocopied as general mail.

**Books and publications must be new and shipped directly** from a publisher, bookstore, or retailer (for example, an order must ship directly from Amazon, not a third-party marketplace seller).

**Digital messages and photos** can be sent through **GettingOut** (a ViaPath service) instead of paper mail.
:::

## How incoming mail works

Indiana handles incoming mail differently from states that use an off-site scanning vendor. **Each prison's own mailroom** opens and inspects incoming **general correspondence** — letters, cards, and photos — and **photocopies it in black and white**. The incarcerated person receives the **photocopy**, generally within about 48 hours, and the original is not delivered. There is **no off-site processing center**; mail goes to the prison where the person is held.

Address personal mail with the person's **full committed name and IDOC number** to the **mailing address of the facility where they are held**:

> [Incarcerated person's full name and IDOC number]
> [Facility name]
> [Facility mailing address]

Because the prison shown in the offender locator can change after a transfer, **confirm the person's current facility before sending**.

:::callout{variant="tip"}
Each facility page on this site lists that prison's mailing address. Always include the person's **IDOC number** so the mailroom can match the mail to the right person.
:::

## Legal mail

**Legal and privileged mail is handled separately** from general correspondence. It goes **directly to the facility** and is opened and inspected **in the incarcerated person's presence** rather than photocopied. Attorneys and courts should follow IDOC's legal-mail labeling requirements. Confirm the current legal-mail procedure with the specific prison before sending.

## Books and publications

**Books and publications must be new and undamaged** and must be **shipped directly from a publisher, bookstore, or approved retailer** — third-party fulfillment is not accepted, so an online order must ship **directly from the retailer** (for example, directly from Amazon, not a marketplace seller). Used or personally mailed books are generally not accepted, and incoming books are inspected for alterations. Confirm the current rules and any quantity limits with the facility.

## Digital messages and photos

Instead of paper mail, families can send **digital messages and photos** through **GettingOut** (a ViaPath service) — create an account, add the incarcerated person as a contact, and fund the account to send messages. Details are in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/indiana/guides/phone-video/).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the person's **current facility on the offender locator before sending**, since mail goes to the prison where they are held and the location can change after a transfer. Address **personal mail** with the person's **full name and IDOC number** to that **facility's mailing address** — the mailroom **photocopies general mail in black and white** and delivers the copy, so do not send originals you want returned. Send **legal mail** to the facility under IDOC's legal-mail rules, where it is opened in the person's presence. Order **books and publications new and shipped directly** from a publisher, bookstore, or retailer (no third-party sellers). Digital messages and photos can be sent through **GettingOut**.
:::
