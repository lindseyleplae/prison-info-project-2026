:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Money goes onto the prisoner's Offender Trust Account (OTA).** Alaska DOC runs this account itself — there is **no third-party deposit vendor** (no JPay, Access Corrections, or TouchPay).

**Two ways to deposit:**
- **In person at any Alaska DOC institution** — regardless of where the prisoner is held — with valid photo ID. **Cash is accepted in person.**
- **By mail**, sending a **cashier's check or money order** (not cash) to the prisoner at the prisoner's facility.

**Only people on the prisoner's approved visitor list may deposit funds.**

**Deposits are capped at $500 per month** (dividend and Native-corporation checks are excepted).

**Money is never handed over at a visit.**
:::

## Where money goes

Money for commissary and personal spending goes onto the prisoner's **Offender Trust Account (OTA)**. Alaska DOC handles these accounts directly. Unlike most states, Alaska uses **no outside deposit vendor** — there is no JPay, Access Corrections, or TouchPay account to set up, and no central lockbox to mail to. Deposits go either to a DOC institution in person or to the prisoner at the prisoner's own facility.

Only people on the prisoner's **approved visitor list** may deposit funds.

## Deposit in person

You can deposit money **in person at any Alaska DOC institution**, regardless of where the prisoner is held — the institution does not have to be the one where the prisoner is housed. Bring **valid photo identification**. **Cash is accepted for in-person deposits.**

## Deposit by mail

To deposit by mail, send a **cashier's check or money order** — **cash is not accepted by mail** — to the prisoner at the prisoner's facility. The check or money order must be:

- **Made out to the prisoner**, including the prisoner's **prisoner number**, and
- **Marked with the sender's full name.**

Because Alaska uses no central deposit vendor, there is **no separate P.O. box** for funds. A mailed deposit goes to the **prisoner at the facility's mailing address** — the same address used for personal mail. Find the current mailing address on the prisoner's **facility page**, or on the Alaska DOC institutions list, and confirm where the prisoner is currently held before sending.

## The monthly limit

Deposits to the trust account are **capped at $500 per month**. **Dividend and Native-corporation checks are excepted** from that limit.

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the two methods above. **Money is not accepted in person during a visit** — it is never handed over in the visiting room. To put money on the account, use an in-person deposit at an institution or mail a cashier's check or money order to the prisoner.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm that you are on the prisoner's approved visitor list before depositing — only approved visitors may add funds. Have the prisoner's full name and prisoner number ready, and verify the prisoner's current facility and its mailing address on the facility page before mailing. Send a cashier's check or money order made out to the prisoner (with the prisoner number) and showing your full name — not cash — to the prisoner at the facility; there is no third-party vendor and no separate deposit P.O. box. Cash is accepted only for in-person deposits, which can be made at any Alaska DOC institution with valid photo ID. Deposits are capped at $500 per month, and money is never handed over at a visit.
:::
