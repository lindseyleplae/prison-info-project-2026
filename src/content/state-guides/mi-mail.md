:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Address mail with the prisoner's name and MDOC number**, sent to their facility.

**You get a black-and-white photocopy; the original is shredded.** Michigan photocopies mail inside the facility — it does not use an off-site scanning company.

**Legal mail now uses a QR-code system.** As of January 2026, attorneys register with a vendor (TextBehind) and add a QR code; legal mail without one is returned.

**Books must be new, from an approved vendor** — and **hardcover books are no longer allowed** (since February 2026).
:::

## How to address it

Use the prisoner's name and **MDOC number**, then the facility name and address (look up the number on OTIS and the address in MDOC's facility directory). For example:

John Smith #123456
[Facility name]
[Facility street address]

## What happens to your letter

Incoming personal mail is opened, inspected, and **photocopied in black and white at the facility**; the prisoner receives the photocopy plus the front of the envelope, and the **original is shredded** (held about 14 days first). Michigan does **not** route personal mail to an outside scanning company — the copying is done in-house. If you want the prisoner to have a **color** copy, send it as a **JPay electronic message** instead of on paper. Limits: **one photo per page** (no collages), no nude photos, generally **no more than 12 pages**, and **postage stamps are not allowed** in the mail.

## Legal and confidential mail (new in 2026)

As of **January 2026**, legal mail (from an attorney) and "confidential" mail (from courts and certain agencies) are also **photocopied** — opened in the prisoner's presence, copied, the original shredded in front of them, and not read. Senders must **register with TextBehind and put a single-use QR code on the envelope**; legal mail without a valid QR code is **returned to the sender unopened**. Because even legal originals are not delivered, keep your own copies of important documents.

## Books, publications, and packages

Books and magazines must be **new** and shipped **directly from a publisher or an MDOC-approved vendor** (such as Amazon, Barnes & Noble, or Walmart) — used items and items mailed by family are not accepted, and prisoners cannot order publications themselves. **Hardcover books are no longer accepted** as of February 2026; softcover books from approved vendors still are. Family **care packages** go through the SecurePak program at michiganpackages.com — one order per calendar quarter, with a spending limit (currently $125).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Address mail with the MDOC number, and expect the prisoner to receive a black-and-white photocopy rather than your original. Michigan's mail rules changed recently — the 2026 QR-code system for legal mail and the hardcover-book ban are both new — so confirm the current procedure on MDOC's mail page before sending.
:::
