Mail to an incarcerated person in Nevada goes **physically to the institution**. Nevada does **not** route incoming personal mail through an off-site scanning vendor; under **Assembly Bill 121** (2023), NDOC is required to deliver **physical and original** copies of mail. The mail rules are set in NDOC **Administrative Regulation 750** (general correspondence and mail), with item rules also in **AR 711**.

## How to address personal mail

Mail goes to the person at the **institution where they are currently housed**. Address the envelope with:

- The person's **committed name** (the name under which they were sentenced).
- The person's **NDOC inmate (back) number**.
- The **institution's mailing address**.

Confirm the person's **current institution** on the NDOC inmate search before sending — a person may transfer, and a new commitment is processed at an intake center before assignment.

## What may be enclosed

Enclosure limits are set by NDOC regulation and enforced by each institution's mailroom. NDOC has published rules allowing, within limits:

- **Letters** and written correspondence.
- **Photographs** (limited in number and size; content restrictions apply — no nudity, sexual activity, or depictions of illegal activity). NDOC has stated a limit of up to ten photos no larger than 8"x10", with the person's name and NDOC number on the back.
- **Greeting cards** within size limits and without electronics (no musical or recorded cards).

:::reality-check{title="A 2026 proposal would tighten these rules — it is not yet in effect"}
In **2026**, NDOC **proposed** regulations to restrict physical general mail — including **greeting cards and photos** — and to deliver most non-legal correspondence electronically instead. That change has **not been adopted**, and advocates note NDOC has not released the study **AB 121** requires before such a change. Until any new rule takes effect, the **current** physical-mail and enclosure rules apply. Confirm the **current** enclosure rules with the specific institution before sending cards or photos.
:::

## Electronic messages

Family and friends can send **one-way electronic messages** to an incarcerated person through **Corrlinks** (the NDOC SecureMail service). Messages are sent for a per-message fee through a free Corrlinks account, then **printed in the mailroom at the person's facility and distributed at mail call**. Messages are **one-way** — the incarcerated person **cannot reply** by electronic message. (GettingOut is the separate NDOC tablet and video platform — see [Visiting in Nevada](/states/nevada/guides/visiting/).)

## Books and publications

Books, magazines, and calendars are accepted **with limits** and generally must come **directly from an approved source** (for example, a publisher or an approved online retailer), not from an individual. Items NDOC offers through its **Canteen** or the **AccessSecurePak** program may not be mailed in from outside. Confirm the current book, publication, and approved-vendor rules with the institution before ordering.

## Money is not enclosed in mail

**Funds for a person's account are not sent in the mail.** Money is sent through the NDOC vendor (**ConnectNetwork**) — see [Sending Money in Nevada](/states/nevada/guides/money/). Cash, checks, or money orders enclosed in a letter are not the accepted method.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm with the **specific institution** before sending anything. Incoming personal mail goes **physically to the institution** — Nevada uses **no off-site scanning vendor**, and state law (**AB 121**) requires delivery of original mail. Address mail with the person's **committed name, NDOC inmate number, and the institution's mailing address**, and confirm the person's **current institution** on the NDOC inmate search first. A **2026** proposal to limit cards and photos and move correspondence to digital delivery is **not yet in effect** — confirm the **current** enclosure rules before sending cards or photos. Send **electronic messages** through **Corrlinks**, send **books and publications** only from an **approved source**, and send **money** through the NDOC vendor (**ConnectNetwork**), never enclosed in mail.
:::
