:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Deposit to the resident's trust account** through **Access Corrections**, the KDOC money vendor.

**Several methods:** the Access Corrections app, online at accesscorrections.com, by phone, with cash at retail through CashPayToday, or by a mailed money order.

**Phone deposits** go to an Access Corrections line at **866-345-1884**, available 24 hours a day.

**Money orders** are mailed to **Centralized Resident Banking, P.O. Box 9101, Topeka, KS 66608**, with the sender's name and return address included.

**Service fees apply** to the app, online, phone, and retail methods, and the amounts vary — confirm before paying.
:::

## How to deposit to the trust account

Money for commissary and personal spending goes into the resident's **trust account**. KDOC handles these deposits through **Access Corrections**. There are several routes:

- **In the Access Corrections app**, available free from the App Store or Google Play, using a credit or debit card.
- **Online** at **accesscorrections.com**, or by phone to Access Corrections at **833-878-0120**, using a credit or debit card.
- **By phone**, on the Access Corrections automated line at **866-345-1884**, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
- **With cash at retail** through **CashPayToday**, at participating stores including Dollar General, Family Dollar, CVS, and 7-Eleven. CashPayToday support is **844-340-2274**.
- **By money order**, mailed to **Centralized Resident Banking, P.O. Box 9101, Topeka, KS 66608**. Include the sender's name and return address.

The **resident's full name and KDOC registration number** are needed for any deposit, so the funds reach the right account. A money order that arrives without the sender's name and return address may post differently than intended, so include both.

## Fees and limits

**Service fees apply to the app, online, phone, and retail methods, and the amounts vary.** Access Corrections charges a service fee that is shown before a transaction is completed, so confirm the current amount before paying. Fees can change over time.

## The phone and messaging account is separate

The trust account is not the same as the accounts that pay for calls and messages. Phone service runs through **ICSolutions**, and electronic messaging runs through **GettingOut** (ViaPath), each funded separately — money added to the trust account does not pay for phone time or messages. For how to set up and add money to the calling and messaging accounts, see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/kansas/guides/phone-video/).

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the methods above. Money is not accepted in person during a visit. A money order intended for the trust account goes to **Centralized Resident Banking** at the Topeka P.O. box — not to the prison where the resident is held. For account questions, Centralized Resident Banking can be reached at **785-746-7630**.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the resident's full name and KDOC registration number ready, and confirm the current service fee with Access Corrections before depositing. Mail money orders to Centralized Resident Banking at the Topeka P.O. box — not to the prison — and include the sender's name and return address. Keep trust deposits separate from the ICSolutions phone account and the GettingOut messaging account. Service fees apply to the app, online, phone, and retail methods and vary; confirm current amounts before paying.
:::
