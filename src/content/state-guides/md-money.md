:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Maryland uses Access Corrections for deposits.** Money goes into the person's banking account through **Access Corrections** — online at **accesscorrections.com**, by phone at **1-866-345-1884**, or by **mailing a money order** to the DPSCS lockbox.

**Funds are deposited to the person's account**, which is drawn on for **commissary** and other charges, including phone use.

**Money is not handed over at a visit.** Deposits go through Access Corrections, not in the visiting room.

**Money orders of $250 or more are held for 30 days.** Online and phone deposits use a Visa or MasterCard debit or credit card and carry a vendor fee.
:::

Money for an incarcerated person in Maryland is sent to a **banking account** managed for the Department of Public Safety and Correctional Services (DPSCS) through **Access Corrections**. There are two ways to deposit: **online or by phone** through Access Corrections, or by **mailing a money order** to the DPSCS lockbox. Funds are not accepted in the visiting room.

## Online and phone deposits (Access Corrections)

Access Corrections handles electronic deposits to a person's Maryland banking account:

- **Online:** at **accesscorrections.com**.
- **By phone:** **1-866-345-1884** (toll-free).

Both methods accept **Visa and MasterCard debit and credit cards** and charge a **vendor fee** that varies by deposit method and amount. To send a deposit, the sender identifies the **person and the facility**; confirm the current fee schedule and any per-deposit or daily limits with Access Corrections before sending.

## By mail (money order to the lockbox)

DPSCS processes mailed funds through a **lockbox**. The lockbox accepts **money orders** (not personal cash). As listed by DPSCS, the address is:

> **P.O. Box 17111, Baltimore, MD 21297-0382**

A money order must show, in **black or blue ink**:

- the **incarcerated person's legal name** (no nicknames or aliases),
- the **facility name or abbreviation**,
- the person's **State Identification number (SID)**, and
- the **sender's name and address**.

DPSCS reports that processing usually takes **2–7 business days** after the lockbox receives a correctly completed money order, and **up to 10 days** including mail delivery. Money orders of **$250 or more are placed on a 30-day hold**.

## What the account pays for

The deposited funds sit in the person's **banking (trust) account**, which is used to **purchase commissary items** and to cover other authorized charges. Commissary funds are commonly drawn on for **phone use** as well — for how phone and video calling are set up and paid for in Maryland, see [Phone and Video in Maryland](/states/maryland/guides/phone-video/).

:::reality-check{title="Confirm the vendor, address, and fees first"}
Deposit vendors, lockbox addresses, and fee schedules change. Before sending money, confirm the **current vendor** (Access Corrections), the **current lockbox P.O. box**, and the **current fees and limits** on the DPSCS banking-services page or with Access Corrections. Confirm the person's **current facility and SID** on the DPSCS Inmate Locator, since both are needed for a deposit to post.
:::

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the **current details before sending money**: that **Access Corrections** is still the vendor and the **lockbox address** (reported as **P.O. Box 17111, Baltimore, MD 21297-0382**) is current, the **fees and limits** for online, phone, and money-order deposits, and the person's **current facility and State Identification number (SID)** on the DPSCS Inmate Locator. Mailed funds must be a **money order** (no cash) made out to the person's **legal name**; money orders of **$250 or more are held for 30 days**. Money is sent through **Access Corrections**, never handed over at a visit.
:::
