## Finding someone

Idaho publishes an online locator called the **Resident/Client Search**, searchable at [idoc.idaho.gov](https://www.idoc.idaho.gov/content/prisons/resident-client-search). It can be searched by **last name** or by **IDOC number**, and it covers people who are incarcerated, on probation, or on parole under Idaho DOC jurisdiction. Checking it is the reliable way to confirm where a person is currently held.

A person held in a **county jail** before transfer to state prison may not appear the same way in the locator; Idaho also contracts county-jail beds (see below), so a search result may point to a county facility that runs its own rules.

## Reception and classification

Newly sentenced people are not sent directly to a long-term assignment. They first pass through **reception and diagnostic processing**, where they are evaluated and assigned a custody level before being moved to another facility. Where that happens depends on the person:

- **Men** enter through the **Reception and Diagnostic Unit (RDU)** at the **[Idaho State Correctional Institution](/states/idaho/facilities/idaho-state-correctional-institution/)** in Kuna, the entry point for all men entering the Idaho prison system.
- **Women** are received at the **[Pocatello Women's Correctional Center](/states/idaho/facilities/pocatello-womens-correctional-center/)**, which operates the Reception and Diagnostic Center for women statewide.

New residents must be classified before visiting applications are processed, so the facility shown on the locator during this early period may be the reception unit rather than the person's eventual assignment.

## Out-of-state transfers (a defining factor)

Idaho's prison system is **operating over capacity**, and to manage the population the Idaho DOC transfers a portion of its men out of state. As of 2026, **about 700 men are held outside Idaho**, all at two CoreCivic-run prisons in Arizona:

- the **[Saguaro Correctional Center](/states/idaho/facilities/saguaro-correctional-center/)** in Eloy, Arizona, a long-standing placement for Idaho men, and
- the **[Central Arizona Florence Correctional Complex](/states/idaho/facilities/central-arizona-florence-correctional-complex/)** in Florence, Arizona — a newer placement, with transfers to the Florence complex beginning in 2026.

This is the single most important context for many Idaho families: **a person searched on the Idaho locator may physically be in Arizona.** The Idaho DOC frames these transfers as a temporary, necessary step to manage capacity rather than a long-term solution, and says people are ideally sent out of state early in a sentence and returned to Idaho well before potential release dates.

## What changes when someone is moved out of state

For a person held at Saguaro or the Central Arizona Florence Correctional Complex, **the CoreCivic process governs visiting, mail, phone, and money** — not the standard Idaho DOC process used at the in-state prisons. The arrangements differ by facility, so the details are confirmed on the facility pages and through the relevant topic guides:

- **Visiting** at an out-of-state facility uses a different application path than the in-state prisons. See [Visiting in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/visiting/) and the facility pages for [Saguaro](/states/idaho/facilities/saguaro-correctional-center/) and the [Central Arizona Florence Correctional Complex](/states/idaho/facilities/central-arizona-florence-correctional-complex/).
- **Mail, phone, and money** also follow the CoreCivic process at those facilities. See [Mail in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/mail/), [Phone & Video in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/phone-video/), and [Sending Money in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/money/), along with the two facility pages, for the addresses and methods that apply.

## County-jail placements

Alongside out-of-state transfers, Idaho also **contracts county-jail beds** to help manage overcrowding, so a person under Idaho DOC jurisdiction may be held in a **county jail**. County jails run their own visiting, mail, and phone rules, which can differ from both the state prisons and the out-of-state facilities. The locator is again the way to confirm where a person actually is.

## How transfers affect tracking and visiting

The Idaho DOC moves people among its prisons, to out-of-state facilities, and to contracted county jails based on capacity, custody level, and programming. **The Idaho DOC does not necessarily notify families of a move,** so for anyone tracking where a person is held, re-checking the **Resident/Client Search** is the practical way to confirm a new location.

A transfer can also change visiting logistics. The days, hours, application path, and contact level **depend on the facility** — and an out-of-state move shifts those onto the CoreCivic process entirely — so visiting arrangements are confirmed for the facility where the person is currently housed. See [Visiting in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/visiting/).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Use the Resident/Client Search to confirm a person's current facility, and check it again after any move, since the Idaho DOC does not necessarily notify families in advance. A person shown on the locator may physically be held in Arizona or in a contracted county jail, where a different process applies — confirm visiting, mail, phone, and money arrangements for the facility where the person is currently housed. Reception assignments and facility rules change; confirm details with the specific facility. A new women's prison is under construction in Kuna (expected around 2027), which may change where women are received and housed.
:::
