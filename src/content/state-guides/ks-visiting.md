:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Get on the approved list first.** Every visitor must be on the resident's **pre-approved visitation list** before a visit can take place. Procedures are set in KDOC policy IMPP 10-113D.

**All visits are scheduled statewide through ICSolutions** at **icsolutions.com.** Reservations must be made **at least 72 hours before** that week's visitation begins at the facility.

**Each facility offers two slots per day** — one morning and one afternoon. A visitor may reserve one or two slots on a single day but **cannot book multiple days in the same weekend.**

**Up to four visitors** may visit a resident.

**Appropriate contact — a brief hug or embrace — is allowed** between residents and their visitors.
:::

## Getting on the approved visitor list

No one may visit until they are on the resident's **pre-approved visitation list.** The resident initiates the process from inside the facility, and each prospective visitor is reviewed under KDOC policy IMPP 10-113D. A visitor who is not on the list cannot be scheduled for a visit.

## Scheduling a visit

Kansas schedules visits **statewide through ICSolutions** rather than facility by facility. Every visit must be **reserved in advance.**

:::steps
1. Confirm the visitor is on the resident's pre-approved visitation list.
2. Create a visitor account and reserve a slot at **icsolutions.com.** Reservations must be made **at least 72 hours before** that week's visitation begins at the facility.
3. Select an available slot. Each facility offers **two slots per day** — one in the morning and one in the afternoon.
:::

A visitor without internet access can contact the **facility's visitation clerk** to arrange a reservation.

:::callout{variant="tip"}
The Kansas Adult Supervised Population Electronic Repository (KASPER) shows where a person is currently held. Confirm the facility before reserving a slot, since families are not notified of routine transfers.
:::

## Visiting slots and limits

Each adult facility offers **two visiting slots per day** — one morning slot and one afternoon slot. Within those slots:

- A visitor may reserve **one or two slots on a single day.**
- A visitor **cannot book multiple days in the same weekend.**
- The number of visitors per resident is **four.**

The exact days and hours are set by each facility within this statewide framework.

## Contact and non-contact visits

Kansas allows **appropriate contact** during a visit: a hug or brief embrace between a resident and their visitor is permitted. The specific arrangement at a given visit depends on the facility, the resident's housing, and the visiting area.

## ID and what visitors may bring

Visitors pass through facility security, and a dress code, identification requirement, and limits on what may be carried in apply at every facility. KDOC does not publish a single statewide list of approved attire or permitted items; the rules are set in policy IMPP 10-113D and at the facility level.

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Confirm the dress code, ID, and item rules with the facility"}
What visitors may wear, the identification they must present, and the items they may carry in vary and are not published as one statewide list. Confirm the current rules with the facility where the person is held before traveling.
:::

## Reception and where someone enters the system

Where a person first enters the Kansas prison system depends on their gender:

- **Men:** all men enter through the **Reception and Diagnostic Unit (RDU) at El Dorado Correctional Facility,** which processes new commitments before transfer to a permanent facility.
- **Women:** all women enter through the intake process at the **Topeka Correctional Facility,** the only women's prison in Kansas.

During reception, **family visits are not allowed** until the resident advances past intake. While a person is in the diagnostic unit, only **attorneys, clergy, and law enforcement** may visit. See [Intake and transfers in Kansas](/states/kansas/guides/transfers/).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm that the visitor is on the resident's pre-approved list and that a slot is reserved through ICSolutions at least 72 hours ahead before traveling — and confirm the facility's dress code, ID, and item rules, which are not published as one statewide list. Visiting rules and schedules change; the KDOC Visitation (In-Person) page and the facility's visitation clerk are the authoritative sources.
:::
