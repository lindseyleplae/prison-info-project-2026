:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Visiting requires an approved application.** Everyone who wants to visit (or even to be on the person's phone list) must submit the **Application to Visit an Inmate** and be approved first. A **background check** applies, supporting documents are due within about **30 days**, and processing can take up to about **60 days** — so apply well ahead.

**Do not apply while the person is still in intake** at the Alhambra (Phoenix) or Perryville reception unit.

**Visiting is organized by unit.** Each complex contains several units, and visiting days, hours, and contact vs. non-contact rules vary by unit and by the person's custody level — confirm them for the specific unit.

**Bring a government photo ID.** Money for the person is sent through Securus, not at the visit.
:::

Visiting an incarcerated person in Arizona involves getting approved on the person's visitor list and then visiting by appointment at the specific unit. Because each ADCRR complex is made up of several units with different security levels, the visiting schedule and rules are set **at the unit level** — so confirm the details for the unit where the person is actually held. These rules come from ADCRR Department Order 911.

## Step 1: Getting approved

Anyone who wants to visit must submit the **Application to Visit an Inmate** and be approved before visiting. The process includes a **background check**, and supporting documents must be received within about **30 days**; approval can take up to about **60 days**. A **non-refundable processing fee** applies — confirm the current amount with ADCRR.

:::callout{variant="warning"}
Apply well before any planned visit. A person in **intake** at the Alhambra (Phoenix) or Perryville reception unit cannot receive regular visits yet — do not apply until the person has been classified and assigned to a permanent unit.
:::

:::reality-check{title="People at a reception unit (intake)"}
A newly committed person is held at a **reception unit** — the **Alhambra unit at ASPC-Phoenix** for men, **ASPC-Perryville** for women — for assessment and classification before transfer to a permanent unit. Confirm the person's **current unit** on the ADCRR Inmate Data Search before applying or scheduling.
:::

## Step 2: Scheduling the visit

Visits are **by appointment**, and the days, hours, length, and frequency vary by **complex, unit, and the person's custody level** (visiting is often offered in weekend time blocks, with frequency tied to the person's program/phase level). Confirm the schedule and how to book with the **specific unit** before traveling. Some privately operated prisons (such as La Palma) schedule visits **online** rather than by phone.

## What to wear

The ADCRR dress code (Department Order 911) applies to all visitors:

- **Nothing orange** (it resembles inmate clothing).
- **No camouflage.**
- **No sheer, see-through, or open-net clothing,** and **no spandex-like material.**
- **No medical scrubs.**

Each unit may add rules, and dress standards are enforced at the entrance, so confirm the specific unit's current rules before traveling.

## Identification

Every **adult** visitor must show a **valid government-issued photo ID**. A minor must be approved on the visitor list and accompanied by an approved adult; confirm the unit's rules for bringing children.

## What you can bring

Items are tightly limited and vary by unit — generally a **photo ID** and, where permitted, a small amount of money for vending in a clear container. **Phones, bags, and gifts are not allowed.** **Money for the person's account is sent through Securus**, never handed over at a visit.

## Video visiting

Arizona offers **video visits through Securus**. The visitor must be on the approved visitor list, the same as for an in-person visit. Confirm the specific unit's video-visit availability and how to schedule.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm with the **specific unit** before relying on any plan: that the visitor is **approved** (submit the Application to Visit an Inmate well ahead — a background check and a non-refundable fee apply, and approval can take up to about 60 days), and the **unit's** visiting days, hours, and contact/non-contact rules (these vary by unit and custody level). A newly committed person is at a **reception unit** (Alhambra at ASPC-Phoenix for men, ASPC-Perryville for women) until transfer — do not apply during intake, and confirm the **current unit** on the ADCRR Inmate Data Search first. Bring a **government photo ID**, dress within the **Department Order 911** code (nothing orange, camouflage, sheer, or scrubs), and send money through **Securus**, not at the visit.
:::
