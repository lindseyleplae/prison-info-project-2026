## Who provides care

Medical, dental, and mental-health care inside MA DOC prisons is delivered through a **contracted health-services vendor** working with the department. As of this writing the contractor is **VitalCore Health Strategies**, which took over in **2024**, replacing the prior contractor (Wellpath).

:::reality-check
**The vendor can change.** VitalCore's takeover in 2024 is a **recent transition**, and the company providing care is not permanent. Confirm the **current provider** through the [Massachusetts DOC](https://www.mass.gov/orgs/massachusetts-department-of-correction).
:::

## Specialized treatment facilities

Two facilities are an exception to the general arrangement. Clinical services at **Bridgewater State Hospital** and at the **Massachusetts Alcohol and Substance Abuse Center (MASAC)** are provided **separately**, by a different contractor (**Recovery Solutions**), because those are specialized treatment facilities rather than general-population prisons.

## Requesting care and the services provided

A person accesses routine care by submitting a **sick-call / health-services request**, which is **triaged by health staff**. Available care includes:

- sick call
- chronic-care management
- emergency care
- medication management
- mental-health care

## Co-pay

A **co-pay may apply** to some health-services visits.

:::reality-check
**The current Massachusetts inmate medical co-pay is not confirmed.** Confirm **whether a co-pay applies**, and the **amount**, with the MA DOC or the facility before relying on it.
:::

## The grievance process and the Office of the Ombudsman

The **MA DOC has an inmate grievance process** for complaints about care. A person who believes a medical need was not addressed can raise it through that process. The specific steps and deadlines are set by MA DOC policy; confirm the current procedure with the facility or the MA DOC.

Separately, the **DOC Office of the Ombudsman** is an **independent health-and-wellness advisor** that reports to the state public-safety secretariat and **reviews concerns** about care. It operates apart from the standard grievance process.

## Emergencies and family contact

A **family member cannot authorize or arrange treatment**. In a **medical emergency**, the prison arranges care and, if needed, **transport to a community hospital**.

For an **urgent concern**, families typically contact the **facility** directly. Confirm the facility's current process for handling such contacts.

## Privacy

**Medical information is protected.** Staff may be **limited in what they can share with family** without the incarcerated person's authorization.
