:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Care is provided in-house by Oklahoma DOC Health Services** — the Medical Services Unit staffs medical, dental, and mental-health care across the prison system rather than contracting it to a single for-profit company.

**There is no named statewide private medical contractor.** Oklahoma DOC may contract for specific inpatient hospital and specialty services, but day-to-day care is delivered by the agency's own Health Services.

**Inpatient and specialty care is centralized** at specialty units, including Joseph Harp Correctional Center in Lexington and Mabel Bassett Correctional Center in McLoud.

**Health complaints go through the Oklahoma DOC grievance process,** starting with the facility's medical staff.
:::

## Health care in Oklahoma prisons

Unlike many states that hire a private prison-health company, **Oklahoma delivers care in-house through Oklahoma DOC Health Services**, the agency's Medical Services Unit. It provides medical, dental, and mental-health care across Oklahoma DOC facilities using its own staff. The agency may contract for general inpatient hospital services and outside specialty providers, but **there is no single named statewide private medical contractor**.

Routine care begins at each prison's on-site clinic, generally through a sick-call request submitted to the facility's medical staff. Confirm the current sick-call procedure, any charges or co-pay, and any exemptions with the facility's health services unit, because details change.

## Inpatient and specialty care

Routine and lower-acuity care is delivered at each prison's on-site clinic. Higher-acuity inpatient and specialty care is provided at designated specialty units, including:

- **Joseph Harp Correctional Center** in Lexington, a medium-security men's prison that houses a Mental Health Unit and provides inpatient medical care.
- **Mabel Bassett Correctional Center** in McLoud, the women's facility that serves as the women's medical and mental-health hub in addition to women's reception.

Mental-health and substance-use services are provided in-house as part of Oklahoma DOC Health Services, ranging from clinic-based care at individual prisons to the specialty units above.

## The grievance process

A health or treatment concern that cannot be resolved with the facility's medical staff can be raised through the **Oklahoma DOC grievance process**, the agency's formal procedure for inmate complaints. The procedure has internal review steps within the prison and the department.

Oklahoma does **not** have a separate independent corrections ombudsman; the agency is governed by the internal Board of Corrections. The grievance process is the route for review of a health or treatment concern. Confirm the current grievance steps and deadlines with the facility's medical staff.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the current sick-call procedure, any charges, and grievance deadlines with the facility's health services unit, because details change.
