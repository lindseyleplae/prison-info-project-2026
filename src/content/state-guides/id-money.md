:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Deposit to the resident's account** through **Access Corrections**, the Idaho DOC money vendor. It is the sole vendor for deposits.

**Several methods:** online at accesscorrections.com, the Access Corrections mobile app, by phone, with cash at retail stores, or by a mailed money order or check.

**Phone deposits** go to an Access Corrections line at **866-345-1884**.

**Mailed money orders and checks** go to the lockbox at **Secure Deposits — Idaho DOC, PO Box 12486, St. Louis, MO 63132**.

**Service fees apply to every method except the mailed lockbox.** The amounts are not published here — confirm the current fee before paying.
:::

## How to deposit through Access Corrections

Money for commissary and personal spending goes into the resident's account. Idaho DOC handles these deposits through **Access Corrections**, which is the only money vendor. There are several routes:

- **Online** at **accesscorrections.com**, using a credit or debit card.
- **In the Access Corrections mobile app**, using a credit or debit card.
- **By phone**, on the Access Corrections line at **866-345-1884**.
- **With cash at retail**, at participating stores including CVS, Dollar General, Family Dollar, 7-Eleven, and Walgreens, through cashpaytoday.com.
- **By money order or check**, mailed to the lockbox (see below).

## Mailing a money order or check

Mailed money orders and checks go to the Access Corrections lockbox, not to the prison:

**Secure Deposits — Idaho DOC, PO Box 12486, St. Louis, MO 63132**

Include the **resident's full committed name and IDOC number** so the funds reach the right account, along with the sender's name and return address. A money order intended for the resident's account goes to this St. Louis lockbox — not to the facility where the resident is held.

## Fees

**Service fees apply to every method except the mailed lockbox** — that is, fees apply to the online, app, phone, and retail cash methods. The mailed money order or check to the lockbox is the one route without an added service fee. Specific fee amounts are not published here, and fees can change over time, so confirm the current amount before paying.

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the Access Corrections methods above. **Money cannot be handed over in person during a visit.** A money order intended for the resident's account goes to the Secure Deposits lockbox in St. Louis, not to the prison.

## Residents held out of state in Arizona

Some Idaho residents are held in Arizona at facilities operated by CoreCivic. For a resident held out of state, the deposit method can differ from the standard Idaho DOC process. **Confirm the current way to send money with the facility** before depositing.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the resident's full committed name and IDOC number ready, and confirm the current service fee with Access Corrections before depositing. Mail money orders and checks to the Secure Deposits — Idaho DOC lockbox at the St. Louis P.O. box — not to the prison — and include the sender's name and return address. Service fees apply to the online, app, phone, and retail methods but not to the mailed lockbox; confirm current amounts before paying. For a resident held out of state in Arizona, confirm the current deposit method with the facility.
:::
