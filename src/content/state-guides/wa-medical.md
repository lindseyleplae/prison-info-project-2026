:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Care is delivered in-house by DOC Health Services** — Washington has no private statewide medical contractor for its prisons.

**The medical co-pay is $3.00 per visit** — never less than $3 — and is waived for emergencies, staff-initiated visits, chronic care, and serious health needs.

**Residential mental-health care for men is at the Sky River Treatment Center** at the Monroe Correctional Complex; women's acute mental-health care is at the Washington Corrections Center for Women.

**Two complaint routes exist** — the internal Offender Grievance Program and the independent Office of the Corrections Ombuds.
:::

## Health care in Washington prisons

Unlike many states that hire a private prison-health company, **Washington delivers care in-house through DOC Health Services**, which provides medical, mental-health, dental, and pharmacy services with its own staff. There is **no private statewide medical contractor**.

The standard charge for a medical visit is a **$3.00 co-pay** — the co-pay is never less than $3. It is **waived for emergencies, for visits initiated by staff, for chronic-care appointments, and for serious health needs**. For the current sick-call request process and any additional exemptions, confirm with the facility.

## Mental health and substance use

DOC Health Services provides mental-health care in-house. For men, the **Sky River Treatment Center** at the **Monroe Correctional Complex** is the residential mental-health facility. For women, **acute mental-health care** is provided at the **Washington Corrections Center for Women** in Gig Harbor. Other facilities provide on-site mental-health services; confirm the level of care available at a specific facility with that facility.

## Raising a concern

Washington has **two routes** for health-care concerns — the prison system's internal grievance process and an independent state office outside WA DOC.

- **The Offender Grievance Program** is WA DOC's internal process for an incarcerated person to file a complaint, including complaints about medical and mental-health care.
- **The Office of the Corrections Ombuds (OCO)** is an **independent** state office that takes complaints about WA DOC from **incarcerated people and from families**. It operates outside the department. More information and the complaint process are at [oco.wa.gov](https://oco.wa.gov).

A complaint about an immediate medical emergency should also go straight to the facility, but the Office of the Corrections Ombuds gives families a route that does not depend on the prison itself.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the current sick-call request process and any co-pay exemptions with the facility's health services unit. For a health or safety concern that cannot be resolved with the facility, the Office of the Corrections Ombuds is an independent office that accepts complaints from incarcerated people and families — contact information is at [oco.wa.gov](https://oco.wa.gov).
:::
