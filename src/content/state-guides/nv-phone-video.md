:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Phone calls run through ViaPath.** Calls and account billing are handled by ViaPath Technologies; families fund a prepaid account through **ConnectNetwork**.

**Tablets, messaging, and video visits run through GettingOut.** GettingOut (a ViaPath service) handles tablet electronic messaging, photos, short video clips, and video visits.

**The incarcerated person places the calls.** Phones and tablets are operated by the incarcerated person; family members fund the account and receive calls or messages.

**Confirm current rates and how accounts work** with ViaPath/NDOC before funding — per-minute rates and program details change.
:::

Nevada routes prison communications through **two linked ViaPath systems**. Telephone calls and account billing go through **ViaPath Technologies**, with prepaid funding processed by **ConnectNetwork**. Tablets, electronic messaging, and video visits go through **GettingOut**, a ViaPath service. This is a change from NDOC's earlier telephone vendor (Securus). The incarcerated person places the calls and uses the tablet; family members fund the accounts and receive contact.

## Phone calls and setting up an account

The incarcerated person places calls from institution phones; family members do not call in. To receive calls, a family member sets up and funds an account through **ViaPath / ConnectNetwork**:

- **Prepaid (AdvancePay) account** — created and funded by the family member through **ConnectNetwork** (online at ConnectNetwork.com, the automated phone line, or a customer-support representative). The balance pays for calls to that number.
- **Traditional collect** — calls billed to the receiving phone's bill, if the carrier permits.
- **Direct-bill accounts** — available for attorneys and bail agents through ViaPath.

ConnectNetwork accounts can also hold money for the incarcerated person's trust/commissary account; sending money is covered in [Sending Money in Nevada](/states/nevada/guides/money/). Per-minute calling rates are set by the ViaPath contract and have changed over time, so confirm the **current rate** with ViaPath/NDOC before funding an account.

## Tablets and electronic messaging

NDOC issues **tablets** through **GettingOut**. The tablets support **electronic messaging, photos, and short video clips**, alongside other content. A family member creates a **GettingOut account** (at GettingOut.com or through the GettingOut mobile app) to exchange messages and photos with the incarcerated person and to fund tablet services. Messaging and media are paid features, and the incarcerated person must have a tablet and account access to receive them. Tablet pricing has been the subject of public reporting in Nevada, so confirm **current per-message and per-item costs** with GettingOut before relying on the service.

## Video visits

Nevada offers **video visits through GettingOut** (the same platform as the tablets). A visitor **registers through GettingOut** — online or in the app — and schedules a session; availability and scheduling are set by the **institution**. Video visits are separate from in-person visiting, which is covered in [Visiting in Nevada](/states/nevada/guides/visiting/). Video-visit rates are set by the ViaPath/GettingOut contract, so confirm the **current rate** and the institution's video-visit availability before scheduling.

:::reality-check{title="Confirm current rates and how accounts work"}
Per-minute phone rates, video-visit rates, and tablet messaging prices are set by the **ViaPath contract** and have changed in Nevada over time — a single published figure can be out of date. There is no standing free-call program assumed here. Confirm the **current rates** and the steps to open and fund an account directly with **ViaPath / ConnectNetwork** (phones) and **GettingOut** (tablets, messaging, video) before sending money.
:::

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the current setup before funding an account. Phone calls and billing run through **ViaPath / ConnectNetwork**; tablets, electronic messaging, photos, and **video visits** run through **GettingOut**. The incarcerated person places the calls and uses the tablet — family members fund the accounts and receive contact. **Per-minute rates, video-visit rates, and messaging prices change**, and a single published figure may be out of date, so confirm the **current rates** and account steps directly with ViaPath/ConnectNetwork and GettingOut. Verify the institution's **video-visit availability** before scheduling, and send money for the person's account through the NDOC vendor — see [Sending Money in Nevada](/states/nevada/guides/money/).
:::
