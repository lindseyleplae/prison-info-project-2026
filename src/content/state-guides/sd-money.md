:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**There are two separate accounts.** The **phone account** pays for calls and tablet services; the **trust (commissary) account** pays for spending at the institution. Money added to one does not move to the other.

**Phone money** is added through **ViaPath AdvancePay** — online at the ViaPath/ConnectNetwork site or by phone at **800-483-8314** — and through **PIN Debit** for the person's calling account.

**Trust (commissary) deposits:** confirm the current method and address with the SD DOC before sending. A **U.S. postal money order mailed to the facility** is the traditional method; an electronic option may also be available through the DOC's vendor.

**Every deposit needs the person's full name and DOC number.** Confirm the person's current facility on the offender locator first.

**No money at visits.** Funds are never handed over in the visiting room.
:::

Money for a person in South Dakota DOC custody is handled through **two separate accounts**: a **phone account** for calls and tablet services, and a **trust account** for commissary and personal spending. The two are funded differently, and money in one does not pay for the other. The phone-account method is established; the trust-account deposit method should be confirmed with the SD DOC before sending (see below).

## Phone account (ViaPath AdvancePay)

Calls and tablet services in South Dakota run through **ViaPath Technologies** (GTL), billed on the **ConnectNetwork** platform. There are two ways to put money toward calls:

- **AdvancePay** — a prepaid account a family member sets up to **receive** the person's calls. Set it up online through the ViaPath/ConnectNetwork site or by phone at **800-483-8314**.
- **PIN Debit** — funds added to the person's own calling account, which the person draws down when placing calls.

A per-deposit service fee, set by the vendor, applies. For how calls, tablet messaging, and video visits are set up, see [Phone & Video in South Dakota](/states/south-dakota/guides/phone-video/).

## Trust (commissary) account

The **trust account** is what the person uses for **commissary** — food, hygiene items, and other approved goods sold at the institution — and other personal spending. It is **separate** from the phone account above.

**Confirm the current trust-account deposit method and address with the South Dakota DOC before sending money.** Published information is not consistent on a single deposit vendor, so this guide does not name one:

- A **U.S. postal money order made payable to the person and mailed to the facility** is the traditional method. Include the person's **full name and DOC number**, and send it to the address the facility lists for funds.
- An **electronic deposit option** may also be available through the DOC's vendor (online or by phone). Confirm whether one is offered, who the payee is, and the correct address before sending.

Because the payee, address, and fees can differ depending on the method, verify the **current** details on the SD DOC site or by calling the DOC rather than relying on a third-party listing.

:::reality-check{title="The trust-deposit vendor is not firmly established"}
Public sources do not agree on a single way to deposit to the South Dakota **trust (commissary) account** — some point to an electronic vendor, others to a money order mailed to the facility. The **phone account** is settled (ViaPath AdvancePay at **800-483-8314**, plus PIN Debit), but for trust deposits, confirm the **current method, payee, and address** with the SD DOC before sending. Do not assume the trust account uses the same vendor as the phone account.
:::

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the methods above. Money is **not** accepted in person during a visit. South Dakota also **scans incoming personal mail** and delivers it to the person's tablet, so cash or a money order tucked into a letter does not reach the person (see [Mail in South Dakota](/states/south-dakota/guides/mail/)). A money order intended for the trust account must go to the address the DOC lists for funds — confirm that address first.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the person's **full name and DOC number** ready, and confirm the person's **current facility** on the SD DOC offender locator before sending money. **Phone money** goes through **ViaPath AdvancePay** (800-483-8314) and **PIN Debit**. For the **trust (commissary) account**, confirm the **current deposit method, payee, and address** with the South Dakota DOC before sending — published sources do not consistently name one vendor, a money order mailed to the facility is the traditional method, and an electronic option may exist through the DOC's vendor. Keep the two accounts separate; money added to one does not pay for the other. Fees are set by the vendor and change — confirm current amounts before depositing. Money is never handed over at a visit.
:::
