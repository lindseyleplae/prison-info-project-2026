## Who provides care

Medical, dental, behavioral-health, addiction-recovery, and maternal-child health care inside IDOC prisons is delivered through a **contracted health-services vendor** working with the department. The **most recent contractor is Centurion Health of Indiana,** which took over in **July 2021,** replacing the previous vendor (Wexford).

:::reality-check
**The vendor can change.** Centurion's contract term **ended in mid-2025,** and it is not confirmed whether IDOC renewed it or selected a new provider. Confirm the **current provider** through [IDOC](https://www.in.gov/idoc/divisions/support-hub/).
:::

These arrangements apply across all Indiana state prisons, **including the two facilities operated by GEO Group** under contract with IDOC.

## Requesting care and the services provided

A person accesses routine care by submitting a **health-care request (sick call),** which is **triaged by health staff.** Available services include:

- sick call
- chronic-care management
- behavioral-health and mental-health care
- addiction-recovery services
- maternal-child health care for incarcerated women

## The co-pay

Indiana charges a **co-pay for some inmate-initiated (self-referral) health-care visits.** Care that **IDOC initiates** is generally **not charged.**

:::reality-check
**The exact co-pay amount is not confirmed,** and published figures change. Confirm the **current amount** with the facility or IDOC before relying on it.
:::

## The grievance process

IDOC has an **offender grievance process** for complaints about care. The specific steps, levels of review, and deadlines are set by IDOC policy. Confirm the current procedure with the facility or IDOC.

### The Indiana Ombudsman Bureau

Separately from the grievance process, the **Indiana Ombudsman Bureau** is an **independent body** created by state law and **housed outside IDOC.** It reviews complaints that **IDOC violated a policy or law or endangered a person's health or safety.**

## Emergencies and family contact

A **family member cannot authorize or arrange treatment.** In a **medical emergency,** the prison arranges care and, if needed, **transport to a community hospital.**

For an **urgent concern,** families typically contact the **facility** directly. Confirm the facility's current process for handling such contacts.

## Privacy

**Medical information is protected.** Staff may be **limited in what they can share with family** without the incarcerated person's authorization.
