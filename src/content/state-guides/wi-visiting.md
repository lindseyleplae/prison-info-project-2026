:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Get approved first.** The incarcerated person mails a **DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire** to the prospective visitor; that person completes it and returns it to the institution. The incarcerated person is notified when a visitor is approved.

**There is no statewide online visit scheduler.** Visits are arranged with each institution — call the facility.

**Days and hours are set by each facility,** not statewide.

**Visitor caps differ by status:** intake (A&E) status allows up to **4 adult close-family members;** general population allows up to **12 adults.** Minors do not count toward the cap until they turn 18.
:::

## Getting on the visiting list

No one may visit until they are an approved visitor, and the **incarcerated person starts the process.** The incarcerated person mails a **DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire** to the prospective visitor. That person completes the questionnaire and returns it to the institution, which reviews it. The incarcerated person is notified once a visitor is approved.

## Visitor limits

How many people may be on a visiting list depends on the incarcerated person's status:

- A person on **intake (Assessment & Evaluation, "A&E") status** may have up to **4 adult close-family members** on the list.
- A person in **general population** may have up to **12 adults** on the list.

**Minors do not count toward these caps until they turn 18.**

## Scheduling a visit

Wisconsin has **no statewide online visit scheduler.** Visits are arranged with each institution. To confirm the current visiting days and hours and to make arrangements, call the facility. Visiting days and hours vary by facility, and visitors arrive no more than 15 minutes before the visit. At facilities with restrictive housing, visits there are often conducted by audio-visual equipment rather than in person.

(Video visits are scheduled separately through ICSolutions — see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/wisconsin/guides/phone-video/).)

## Identification

Visitors **16 and older must show a valid government-issued photo ID.**

## Dress code

The dress code is statewide.

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Statewide dress code"}
The following are not permitted in the visiting room:

- **Camouflage clothing.**
- **Transparent or revealing clothing,** and **strapless or tube tops.**
- **Spandex worn as pants.**
- **Shorts, skirts, or dresses shorter than fingertip length.**
- **Gang-related or obscene clothing,** and **torn clothing.**
- **Watches and purses.**

Visitors pass through a **metal detector,** so clothing with underwire or metal can cause delays.
:::

## During the visit

In the visiting room, a visitor may bring in **up to $25 in cash, or one debit or credit card,** for vending. **Food and gifts may not be brought in or given to the incarcerated person.** A visitor who needs an accommodation can request one using **form DOC-2424.**

## Reception centers

People entering WI DOC custody are first held at a reception center, where security level is assigned, before transfer to a general-population institution:

- **Men** are received at **Dodge Correctional Institution** (Waupun).
- **Women** are received at **Taycheedah Correctional Institution** (Fond du Lac).

Because a person's institution can change after intake, confirm the current location on the [Offender Locator](https://appsdoc.wi.gov/lop/welcome) before traveling. See [Transfers & Finding Someone](/states/wisconsin/guides/transfers/).

## Video visits

Video visits are a separate option from in-person visiting. They are provided through **ICSolutions** and are scheduled through that system rather than with the institution. Details on cost and setup are in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/wisconsin/guides/phone-video/).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the facility's current visiting days and hours by calling the institution before traveling; a visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved list before a visit can occur. Visiting rules and schedules change — the WI DOC Visiting Information page and the facility are the authoritative sources.
:::
