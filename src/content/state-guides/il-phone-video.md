:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**ICSolutions (ICS) is IDOC's communications vendor.** As of a 2025 transition, ICSolutions provides phone calls, tablets, video visits, and electronic messaging across Illinois prisons.

**You must be on the person's approved calling list.** The incarcerated person builds the list from inside — to receive calls, write to the person and ask to be added.

**Set up and fund an account through ICSolutions.** Families create a prepaid ICSolutions account to pay for calls, messaging, and video visits, or money in the person's trust account can cover calls.

**Old Securus and GTL accounts may no longer work.** Accounts and messages from the prior vendors do not carry over automatically — verify with ICSolutions.
:::

Communications for Illinois prisons run through **ICSolutions (ICS)**, the Illinois Department of Corrections' communications vendor. ICSolutions handles **phone calls, tablets, video visits, and electronic messaging**. This replaced the prior arrangement, in which phone service was provided by Securus and video visits and messaging by GTL (Global Tel\*Link / ConnectNetwork) — a change IDOC made in **2025**.

## How phone calls work

Incarcerated people in Illinois **place outgoing calls** to numbers on their **approved calling list** — they **cannot receive** incoming calls. To be called, a person must write to the incarcerated individual and ask to be added to that list.

ICSolutions is the **only** provider IDOC uses for individual-in-custody telephone service. Calls can be paid for two ways:

- A **prepaid ICSolutions account** funded by family or friends.
- The incarcerated person's **trust (commissary) account**, used to buy calling through the prison.

For phone questions, IDOC directs families to ICSolutions customer service at **888-506-8407** or **Customer@icsolutions.com**.

## Setting up and funding an account through ICSolutions

Families register with **ICSolutions** to create and fund a **prepaid account**, then use it to pay for calls, electronic messages, and video visits. Registration and deposits are handled through the ICSolutions system. Adding the incarcerated person's **IDOC ID number** during setup helps link the account correctly.

Sending money to the person's **trust account** for commissary is a separate process from funding a communications account — see [Sending Money in Illinois](/states/illinois/guides/money/).

## Tablets

Tablets in Illinois facilities are **provided by ICSolutions**. Tablet access is administered by the prison and the division's rollout, and availability and features vary by facility and by the person's status. As the system completes its transition to ICS tablets, families may fund their **ICSolutions prepaid account** to cover messages, calls, and video visits used on the tablet. Confirm tablet availability with the specific prison.

## Electronic messaging

IDOC offers **electronic messaging through ICSolutions' CorrLinks system** (corrlinks.com) at all facilities except the Adult Transition Centers (ATCs). Messages are **screened and monitored**, and IDOC applies a delay before incoming and outgoing messages are delivered.

Messages sent through the **prior GTL system (ConnectNetwork) will not be delivered** — electronic messaging now goes through ICSolutions' CorrLinks. Families who used the previous vendor should set up messaging through ICSolutions and verify the correct product before sending.

## Video visits

Illinois offers **video visits through ICSolutions** at all facilities except the ATCs, after transitioning this service from GTL in 2025. Visitors **register and schedule through ICSolutions**. Because video visits are part of the visiting program — including registration, scheduling, and per-day and per-week limits — they are covered in detail in [Visiting in Illinois](/states/illinois/guides/visiting/). When registering, entering a prior **GTL Visitor ID** can help link an existing record during the transition.

:::reality-check{title="Rates and free-call programs change"}
Per-minute **call rates** and any current **free-call program** are set by IDOC and ICSolutions and change over time. Illinois has run **time-limited** free-call periods, but these start and end on their own schedule. Confirm the **current rate**, deposit options, and whether any **free-call program** is active directly with **ICSolutions** or **IDOC** before relying on a figure.
:::

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm with **ICSolutions** and the **specific prison** before relying on any plan: that you are on the person's **approved calling list** (the person arranges this from inside), how to **set up and fund** an ICSolutions prepaid account, and the **current call rate** and whether any **free-call program** is in effect — these change, so do not rely on a quoted number. Because of the **2025 vendor change**, old **Securus** phone accounts and **GTL/ConnectNetwork** messages may no longer work; messaging now runs through **ICSolutions' CorrLinks**, and video visits through **ICSolutions**. Tablet and video-visit availability vary by facility. Funding the person's **trust account** for commissary is separate from a communications account — see [Sending Money in Illinois](/states/illinois/guides/money/).
:::
