:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Personal mail currently goes directly to the prison.** As of mid-2026, GDC staff open and inspect incoming personal mail for contraband at the facility and then deliver it — Georgia does **not** currently use an off-site mail-scanning vendor.

**This may change.** In February 2025, GDC issued an "Offender Mail Solutions" request for proposals to route all incoming mail through a single central location. No vendor was confirmed live as of this writing, so **confirm the current mailing address on GDC's site before sending**.

**Address mail with the full committed name, GDC ID number, and the full prison name and address.** GDC headquarters in Forsyth is not an inmate-mail address.

**Books, magazines, and newspapers must come from a publisher or established retailer.**

**Legal mail is opened only in the person's presence,** inspected for contraband, and not read.
:::

## How to address personal mail

As of mid-2026, incoming personal mail (letters, cards, photos) goes **directly to the prison**, where GDC staff open and inspect it for contraband before delivering it. Georgia does **not** currently route personal mail through an off-site scanning vendor.

Address mail with:

- The person's **full committed name**
- Their **GDC ID number**
- The **full, un-abbreviated prison name**
- That **prison's mailing address**

:::callout{variant="warning"}
GDC headquarters at **300 Patrol Road, Forsyth** is **not** an inmate-mail address. Mail must go to the mailing address of the specific prison where the person is held.
:::

## A change may be coming

In **February 2025**, GDC issued an "**Offender Mail Solutions**" request for proposals, seeking to route all incoming mail through a single central location rather than to each prison. This is the same model many states have adopted to scan or photocopy mail off-site.

As of this writing, **no vendor had been confirmed live**, and personal mail still goes to the facility.

:::reality-check{title="Confirm the address before every send"}
Because the "Offender Mail Solutions" procurement could change where mail is sent — and when it changes, mail sent the old way may be delayed or returned — confirm the **current mailing address on GDC's site before sending**, rather than relying on an address used previously.
:::

## Books and publications

Books, magazines, and newspapers must come **directly from a publisher or an established retailer**, under **Georgia Administrative Rule 125-3-3**. Content that threatens the security or order of the facility can be rejected.

## Legal mail

Legal and privileged mail is opened **only in the person's presence** and inspected for contraband — it is **not read**, and it **cannot be withheld as punishment**.

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
