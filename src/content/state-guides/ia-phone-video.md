:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Iowa's communications run through Ameelio,** a nonprofit provider used across all nine Iowa DOC prisons. Ameelio provides **video calls, voice (phone) calls, and electronic messaging.**

**These services are offered free to families** per the Iowa DOC and Ameelio. Most states use a for-profit vendor such as Securus or ViaPath; Iowa's provider is a nonprofit.

**Video calls and visit scheduling use the Ameelio Connect mobile app,** available for iOS and Android. The app is also used to schedule in-person visits at the facilities where that has rolled out.

**The incarcerated individual places the call.** Families set up an account and are added as an approved contact; they cannot call in.
:::

## How Ameelio works

Iowa DOC's communications provider is **Ameelio**, a nonprofit that operates across all nine state prisons. To use it, families set up an account through the **Ameelio Connect** app, available for iOS and Android. The app is **mobile-only** and is not used on a computer.

Setting up an account follows these steps:

- Download the Ameelio Connect app and create an account.
- Verify your identity (the app requests a government-issued photo ID and a selfie).
- Add the incarcerated individual as a contact, identified by **facility and ID number**.
- Wait for the Iowa DOC to approve the contact before scheduling or connecting.

Each visitor age 13 and older completes this setup. Have the incarcerated individual's full name and Iowa DOC offender number ready when adding the contact.

## Video visits

Video visits are scheduled and connected through the **Ameelio Connect** app. After your contact request is approved, you can schedule a session with the incarcerated individual. Video visiting is a **separate process from in-person visiting** at the facility.

The Ameelio Connect app is also being used for **in-person (on-site) visit scheduling**, which has been rolling out facility by facility. Because the schedule and what is available differ by facility, confirm the current options for the specific institution before booking.

## Voice (phone) calls

Voice calls run through **Ameelio**, and the Iowa DOC and Ameelio describe voice calls as **offered free to families.** As with phone systems generally, the **incarcerated individual places the call** to an approved contact; families set up an account and cannot call in.

Iowa's per-minute phone-cost picture is unsettled. Legacy third-party data from an earlier phone vendor still circulates and conflicts with the free-call description, so the current per-minute rate is not stated here. Confirm what applies — and whether all calls are handled through Ameelio — with the Iowa DOC or Ameelio before relying on a specific cost.

## Electronic messaging

Electronic messaging for approved contacts is also provided through **Ameelio**. The Iowa DOC family-services materials list Ameelio as the messaging service for approved contacts. As with video and voice, messaging is set up and used through the Ameelio account once a contact is approved.

## Tablets

Ameelio supplies tablets to the Iowa system per the provider. The official Iowa DOC materials do not describe a tablet program in detail, so confirm what is available — and how tablets are used for calls and messaging — with the Iowa DOC or Ameelio before relying on specific tablet features.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Set up an account in the **Ameelio Connect** app to reach an incarcerated individual in an Iowa prison, verify your identity, and add the contact by facility and ID number; the incarcerated individual places the call once you are an approved contact. Voice calls are described as free to families by the Iowa DOC and Ameelio, but the per-minute picture is unsettled and tablet details are not fully confirmed, so confirm the current costs, what is offered, and the visit-scheduling options for the specific facility with the Iowa DOC or Ameelio before relying on anything here.
:::
