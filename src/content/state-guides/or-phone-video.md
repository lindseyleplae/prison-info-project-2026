:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**The person inside places the calls.** You cannot call in; calls are outgoing only, and capped at 30 minutes.

**Set up an account first.** Families open a free **ICSolutions** account, add money, and **validate their phone number** before calls will connect.

**Calls run about 9 cents a minute.** Video is separate and costs more — confirm the current rates at the source, since they change.

**Tablets are coming.** Oregon is giving every adult in custody a personal tablet, and calling, messaging, and scanned mail are moving onto it.
:::

## How calls work

The **adult in custody places outgoing calls** — there is no way to call in — and each call is limited to about **30 minutes** and is monitored and recorded. To receive calls, set up a free account with **ICSolutions** (ICS Corrections), add a prepaid balance, and **validate your phone number**; number validation has been required since late 2022. The person can also leave a short voicemail for about **$0.50** a message.

## What it costs

As of 2026, ODOC's phone rate is about **$0.09 per minute** for in-state and domestic calls. Rates and deposit fees (which differ for web, kiosk, and walk-in payments) change with the contract, so confirm the current numbers on the ICSolutions/ODOC pages rather than relying on a figure here.

## Video visits

Oregon offers video visits through **ICSolutions ("The Visitor")**. Register for free, validate your phone number, and schedule from a computer or smartphone. At-home video sessions are **fee-based** — roughly $0.21 a minute for a session of about half an hour — and the exact cost varies by facility, so confirm it when you schedule. All video calls are monitored. (Visiting in person is covered in [Visiting in Oregon](/states/oregon/guides/visiting/).)

## Messaging and tablets

Electronic messages are handled through **GettingOut** (also ICS); fees apply, so check the current per-message price before relying on it. Oregon is deploying **personal ViaPath tablets** to everyone in custody, replacing the older shared-checkout tablets. The tablets themselves are free; calling, messaging, and scanned mail run on them, and some content is paid. Confirm what is available at a given prison on ODOC's tablet-deployment page.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Open a free ICSolutions account and validate your phone number before expecting calls, and confirm the current per-minute call and video rates at the source — they change with the vendor contract. As tablets roll out, check ODOC's site for what calling and messaging look like at the specific prison.
:::
