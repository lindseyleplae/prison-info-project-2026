**Deposit to the trust (commissary) account** through **ViaPath/ConnectNetwork**, with **TouchPay** as the trust processor.

**Four methods:** online at ConnectNetwork.com or in the ConnectNetwork app, by phone, by Western Union, or by money order to TouchPay.

**Only approved visitors may deposit funds** to an incarcerated person's account.

**The phone and messaging account is separate** — it is funded through ViaPath, not TouchPay (see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/north-carolina/guides/phone-video/)).

**Fees change** — confirm current amounts with the vendor at checkout.
:::

## How to deposit to the trust account

Money for commissary and personal spending goes into the incarcerated person's **trust account**. NCDAC handles these deposits through **ViaPath (GTL) / ConnectNetwork**, with **TouchPay** as the trust processor. There are four routes:

- **Online** at **ConnectNetwork.com**, using a credit or debit card.
- **In the ConnectNetwork app**, using a credit or debit card.
- **By phone.** ConnectNetwork operates two lines: the **Trust / PIN Debit** line at **888-428-1845** and the **AdvancePay** line at **800-483-8314**.
- **By Western Union**, using the payment code **"NCDAC TRUST"**.
- **By money order**, made payable to **TouchPay** and mailed to **TouchPay Holdings, LLC, P.O. Box 174, Houston, TX 77001-0174**.

**Only approved visitors may deposit funds** to an incarcerated person's account.

## Fees and limits

**Deposit fees are set by the vendor and change over time.** ConnectNetwork, TouchPay, and Western Union each charge a service fee that is shown before a transaction is completed, so confirm the current amount with the vendor at checkout. Confirm current fees with the vendor.

## The phone and messaging account is separate

The trust account is not the same as the account that pays for calls and tablet messages. Phone calls and tablet messaging run on **ViaPath**, and that account is **funded separately** — money added to the trust account through TouchPay does not pay for phone time. For how to set up and add money to the calling and messaging account, see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/north-carolina/guides/phone-video/).

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the methods above. Money is not accepted in person during a visit, and personal mail does not reach the facility — North Carolina sends incoming personal mail to an off-site scanning vendor, so anything enclosed there is returned to the sender (see [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/)). A money order intended for the trust account goes to **TouchPay**, not to the prison and not to the mail vendor.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the incarcerated person's full name and OPUS number ready, and confirm the current fee with ConnectNetwork, TouchPay, or Western Union before depositing. Only approved visitors may deposit funds. Make money orders payable to TouchPay at the Houston P.O. box — not to the prison — and keep trust deposits separate from the ViaPath phone and messaging account. Fees change; confirm current amounts with the vendor.
:::
