:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Deposit to the trust account** (used for commissary) through **JPay Send Money** — online, by phone, at a kiosk, or by mailed check or money order.

**Fund calls, messaging, and video** through a separate **Securus** account.

**JPay online, phone, and kiosk deposits carry a fee** that scales with the amount (roughly $3.25 to $10.95); **mailed checks and money orders are free**.

**Money is never handed over at a visit.** Both accounts are funded only through the vendors.
:::

## Two accounts, two vendors

WA DOC contracts a single vendor, Securus, for technology services; Securus also owns the JPay brand used on the tablets. Money for an incarcerated person is held in two separate accounts, each funded through a different path:

- The **trust account** pays for commissary and other personal purchases. It is funded through **JPay Send Money**.
- The **Securus account** pays for phone calls, electronic messaging, and video visits. It is funded through **Securus Debit**.

A deposit to one does not move to the other, so confirm which account is being funded before sending money.

## Depositing to the trust account (JPay Send Money)

JPay Send Money deposits go to the trust account. There are several ways to send:

- **Online** at JPay.com or in the JPay app.
- **By phone** through JPay's automated line.
- **At a kiosk** (JPay walk-in locations).
- **By mail**, with a check or money order.

Every deposit must identify the incarcerated person by **committed name and DOC number**. Look up the DOC number on the [incarcerated search](https://doc.wa.gov/records/incarcerated-data-search/incarcerated-search) before sending.

## Funding calls, messaging, and video (Securus Debit)

Phone calls, e-messaging, VideoGrams, and video visits are paid from a **Securus** account, funded through **Securus Debit**. This account is separate from the JPay trust account. Details on how calls and video work are in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/washington/guides/phone-video/).

## Fees

**Online, phone, and kiosk deposits through JPay carry a service fee** that increases with the amount sent — roughly **$3.25 to $10.95** at the time of writing. **Checks and money orders sent by mail are free.** Securus Debit deposits also carry their own vendor fees. Fees change, so confirm current amounts with the vendor before paying.

## Money is not handed over at a visit

Money cannot be given to an incarcerated person or to staff during a visit. The trust account and the Securus account are funded only through the vendors described above.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the incarcerated person's DOC number ready, and confirm which account is being funded — the JPay trust account for commissary, or the Securus account for calls and video. Fees change; confirm the current amount with the vendor before depositing.
:::
