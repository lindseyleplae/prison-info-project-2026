:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Two separate accounts.** The **trust (commissary) account** holds money for commissary and personal spending. A separate **Securus Debit account** pays for phone calls, messaging, tablet media, and video.

**Trust deposits** are made by **U.S. postal money order**, made payable to the inmate's name and NMCD number, mailed to the inmate at their facility.

**The depositor must be on the inmate's approved visitor list** before sending money.

**An online deposit option is not confirmed.** Before relying on the postal method, ask the facility whether an online or other deposit option is available.

**Money is never handed over at a visit.**
:::

## How to deposit to the trust account

Money for commissary and personal spending goes into the inmate's **trust account**. The method confirmed by the New Mexico Corrections Department is a **mailed money order**:

- Use a **U.S. postal money order**.
- Make it **payable to the inmate's name and NMCD number**.
- Mail it to the **inmate at their facility**.

The **depositor must be on the inmate's approved visitor list** before sending funds. The inmate's **full committed name and NMCD number** are required on the money order so the deposit is credited to the right account. The NMCD number can be confirmed through the Offender Search on the New Mexico Corrections Department site.

New Mexico does not publish a single statewide trust-deposit address, and the facility a person is housed at can change. Confirm the current mailing address for trust deposits with the inmate's facility before sending a money order.

## Is there an online deposit option?

Many state systems also accept trust deposits online or by phone through a third-party vendor. **For New Mexico, an online or phone deposit vendor for the trust account is not confirmed.** The method documented by the Department is the mailed money order described above.

If you would prefer to deposit online, **contact the inmate's facility and ask whether an online or other deposit option is available**, rather than assuming one exists. Family and Constituent Services can also point you to the current process.

## The phone and messaging account is separate

The trust account is **not** the same as the account that pays for calls and tablet services. Phone calls, messaging, tablet media, and video run through **Securus** and are funded through a separate **Securus Debit account**. Money added to the trust account does not pay for phone time, and money added to the Securus Debit account is not commissary money. For how to set up and add money to the calling and messaging account, see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/new-mexico/guides/phone-video/).

## Don't hand money over at a visit

Funds are added only through the methods above. **Money is not accepted in person during a visit.** A money order intended for the trust account is mailed to the inmate at the facility — confirm the current address with the facility first.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Have the inmate's full committed name and NMCD number ready, and make sure you are on the inmate's approved visitor list before sending money. Make the money order payable to the inmate's name and NMCD number, and confirm the current mailing address with the facility before sending. An online deposit option is not confirmed — ask the facility whether one is available rather than assuming. Keep trust deposits separate from the Securus Debit account that funds calls and tablet services.
:::
