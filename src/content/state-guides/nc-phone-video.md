**Phone, tablet, messaging, and video all run through ViaPath (GTL),** with accounts handled through **ConnectNetwork** and JPay-brand tablets.

**Calls are about $0.10 per minute with a 15-minute cap.** Rates change — confirm the current rate with the vendor before funding an account.

**Set up an account** at **ConnectNetwork.com** or by calling **1-800-483-8314** — an AdvancePay prepaid account (the receiving party funds it) or a Phone Debit account (the incarcerated person funds it).

## How phone calls work

North Carolina's phone vendor is **ViaPath (formerly GTL)**, and families use it through **ConnectNetwork**. Calls run about **$0.10 per minute** and are capped at **15 minutes** per call. The per-minute rate is set in the vendor contract and is subject to change, so confirm the current rate with the vendor before funding an account.

There are two account types:

- An **AdvancePay** account is a prepaid balance tied to the phone number that receives calls; the cost of each call is drawn from that balance.
- A **Phone Debit** account is funded by the incarcerated person and drawn down as they place calls.

Set up either account at **ConnectNetwork.com** or by phone at **1-800-483-8314**. ConnectNetwork lists the service fees that apply to deposits and account transactions; review those fees at the point of payment, as they are charged in addition to call costs.

Calls are placed on the housing unit's **wall phones**, and the tablets described below can also place calls. The incarcerated person places the call; families cannot call in.

## Tablets and messaging

NCDAC issues **JPay-brand tablets** on the ViaPath system. In addition to placing phone calls, the tablets handle **electronic messaging** and other media services, and they are the device through which scanned personal mail is delivered. (Incoming paper mail is handled by a separate vendor — see [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).) Per-message and per-item prices are set by the vendor and change, so confirm them before sending. Tablet kiosks on the housing units are an alternative for account and messaging functions where a personal tablet is not in hand.

## Video visits

North Carolina offers **video visits through the GettingOut Visits app** (ViaPath), used on a smartphone or a computer. Sessions can be **on-demand or scheduled**. **The per-session cost is not published**, so confirm the price in the GettingOut Visits app before booking. Because the available options and any prices change, confirm what is offered for the specific facility.

## Sending money

Calls, tablet messaging, and the trust account that funds them are funded separately. For depositing to a phone or trust account — through ViaPath/ConnectNetwork and TouchPay — see [Sending Money](/states/north-carolina/guides/money/).

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Set up a ConnectNetwork account (AdvancePay or Phone Debit) at ConnectNetwork.com or 1-800-483-8314, and have the incarcerated person's information ready; the person places the call. Because rates and fees change and the video-visit price is not posted, confirm the current per-minute call rate, the ConnectNetwork service fees, and the GettingOut Visits session cost with the vendor before funding anything.
:::
