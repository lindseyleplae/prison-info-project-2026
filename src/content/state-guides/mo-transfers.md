:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**New arrivals go through a reception and diagnostic center first.** **Men** enter through one of **three regional centers** — the **Fulton Reception and Diagnostic Center** (Fulton), the **Eastern Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (ERDCC, Bonne Terre), or the **Western Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (WRDCC, St. Joseph). **All women** enter through the **Women's Eastern Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (WERDCC, Vandalia). There, a person is screened (medical and mental health), classified by custody level, and then assigned and transferred to a permanent prison.

**The listed prison can change after classification.** Because the first prison is the reception and diagnostic center, the facility shown in the offender search can change once the person is classified and transferred.

**Find someone with the Missouri DOC offender search** at [web.mo.gov/doc/offSearchWeb](https://web.mo.gov/doc/offSearchWeb/), searching by **name or DOC ID number**. It covers people in **Missouri DOC custody**, not county-jail detainees.

**All 19 Missouri prisons are state-operated** and appear in the offender search the same way.
:::

## Reception and classification

After **sentencing**, a newly committed person goes to a **reception and diagnostic center** for **medical and mental-health screening**, a **custody-level classification**, and a **program assessment**, then is **assigned and transferred** to a **permanent prison**. Which center depends on **sex**:

- **Men:** one of **three regional centers** — the **Fulton Reception and Diagnostic Center** (Fulton), the **Eastern Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (ERDCC, Bonne Terre), or the **Western Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (WRDCC, St. Joseph).
- **Women:** the **Women's Eastern Reception, Diagnostic and Correctional Center** (WERDCC, Vandalia).

During **reception**, **visiting and other privileges are limited**, and the prison shown in the offender search can **change** once the person is **classified and transferred** to a permanent facility.

:::reality-check
**The first prison is not where the person will stay.** Because the reception and diagnostic center is the **first** facility, the prison shown in the offender search can **change** after the person is classified and moved to a permanent assignment. A [visit](/states/missouri/guides/visiting) and a [mail](/states/missouri/guides/mail) address are tied to the **current** facility, not the reception center.
:::

## State prison or county jail

The **Missouri DOC offender search** covers people in **Missouri DOC custody**. **All 19 Missouri prisons are state-operated** and appear in the offender search the same way.

A person sentenced to **state time** may still be **physically held in a county jail** awaiting transfer to a DOC prison. **County jails are a separate system** and are **not in the DOC offender search**. To locate someone in a county jail, **search that county's roster**.

## Finding someone

To find where someone is held, use the **Missouri DOC offender search** at **[web.mo.gov/doc/offSearchWeb](https://web.mo.gov/doc/offSearchWeb/)**, searching by **name or DOC ID number**. It covers people in **Missouri DOC custody**, **not** county-jail detainees. A person sentenced to state time who is **not yet listed** may still be held in a **county jail** awaiting transfer; in that case, **search the county's roster**.

## Transfers and current location

A person may be **transferred again** later in the sentence for **custody, program, medical, or disciplinary reasons**. Transfers are **Missouri DOC decisions**, and **families are not always notified in advance**. Because the **location can change** with transfers, **confirm the current facility on the offender search** before traveling, applying to visit, or sending mail.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the current location and status on the Missouri DOC offender search at web.mo.gov/doc/offSearchWeb before traveling, applying to visit, or sending mail, searching by name or DOC ID number. A newly committed person passes through a reception and diagnostic center — one of the three regional centers (Fulton, ERDCC in Bonne Terre, or WRDCC in St. Joseph) for men, and WERDCC in Vandalia for women — before moving to a permanent prison, so the listed facility can change once the person is classified and transferred. The Missouri DOC may transfer a person again for custody, program, medical, or disciplinary reasons, and transfer timing and notification are set by the Missouri DOC. All 19 Missouri prisons are state-operated and appear in the offender search the same way; the search covers people in Missouri DOC custody, not county-jail detainees. A person sentenced to state time may be held in a county jail awaiting transfer, so if they are not listed in the offender search, search that county's roster.
:::
