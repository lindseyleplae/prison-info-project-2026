:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Personal mail is scanned off-site — it does not go to the prison.** Send letters, cards, and photos to the **Securus Digital Mail Center** in Dallas, Texas; the person reads them as a digital color image on a tablet.

**Originals are not delivered.** The physical letter is scanned and uploaded, not forwarded to the facility. People without device access can request a free black-and-white printout.

**Legal mail, books, and packages go to the facility — not to Dallas.** Legal mail and special correspondence go directly to the prison; publications and packages go to the facility's address.

**The inmate's full name and seven-digit ODOC number must appear on the mail.**
:::

## How personal mail works now

Since September 1, 2024, incoming personal mail to an Oklahoma DOC facility does not go to the prison. Oklahoma DOC routes personal letters, cards, and photos to an off-site vendor, the **Securus Digital Mail Center**, which opens and scans each item and delivers a digital color image to the incarcerated person's Securus tablet. The original mail is not forwarded to the facility. A person without tablet or kiosk access can request a free black-and-white printout.

Address personal mail with the inmate's full committed name, their **ODOC number** (the seven-digit Oklahoma DOC ID), and the Securus Digital Mail Center lines:

```
[Full name] [ODOC number]
Securus Digital Mail Center - Okla. Dept. of Corrections
P.O. Box 223566
Dallas, TX 75222-3566
```

:::callout{variant="warning"}
Illegible or incorrectly addressed mail may be returned or delayed. Personal mail sent to the prison's own address instead of the Securus box in Dallas does not follow the digital-mail process. Money enclosed with a personal letter is not the way to deposit funds — see [Sending Money](/states/oklahoma/guides/money/) for how to add money to a trust account.
:::

## Legal and privileged mail

**Legal mail does not use the digital mail center.** Mail from courts, attorneys, and other privileged or special correspondents goes **directly to the facility** where the person is housed, not to the Dallas scanning center, and is handled under the rules for privileged mail. Attorneys confirm the facility's current handling instructions with the prison.

## Books, magazines, and publications

Publications — magazines, newspapers, and books — also go **directly to the facility**, not to the Securus P.O. box. They must come directly from the **publisher or an approved vendor**, not from family or friends. Look up the current address for the specific facility on the Oklahoma DOC Facilities page, since each prison's address differs.

## Packages

Packages **cannot** be sent to the digital mail center. They are handled **through the facility**, not through the Securus scanning center. Confirm the facility's package rules with the prison, since they differ by location and custody level.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Send personal letters, cards, and photos to the Securus Digital Mail Center in Dallas (P.O. Box 223566, Dallas, TX 75222-3566) with the inmate's full name and seven-digit ODOC number, but send legal mail, books, and packages to the facility — look up that facility's address on the Oklahoma DOC Facilities page, since each one differs. Mail rules change; the Oklahoma DOC Digital Mail page is the authoritative source.
:::
