:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Every visitor must be approved first.** Create an account at **login.mt.gov** and complete the online **Visitation Application.** Facility staff review each application, and approval can take **up to 90 days.**

**Scheduling is not the same at every prison.** At **Montana State Prison** and **Montana Women's Prison,** visits are scheduled online through **ICSolutions.** Montana Women's Prison has required visits to be scheduled online in advance **since August 1, 2025.**

**The two contract prisons run their own visiting process.** **Crossroads Correctional Center** (Shelby) and **Dawson County Correctional Facility** (Glendive) handle visiting separately — their process is confirmed with the facility, not through the statewide application alone.

**Arrive 15–30 minutes before the scheduled visit** to complete check-in.
:::

## Getting on the approved visitor list

No one may visit until they are an approved visitor. Each prospective visitor:

:::steps
1. Creates an account at **login.mt.gov.**
2. Completes the online **Visitation Application.**
3. Waits for facility staff to review the application and run a background check.
:::

Review can take **up to 90 days,** though it is often less. Approval is handled at the facility level, so the outcome applies to that prison.

## Scheduling a visit

Montana does not use a single statewide visit scheduler. How a visit is arranged depends on which prison the person is held at.

- **Montana State Prison (Deer Lodge):** visits are scheduled online through **ICSolutions.**
- **Montana Women's Prison (Billings):** visits are scheduled online through **ICSolutions.** Online scheduling in advance has been required **since August 1, 2025.**
- **Crossroads Correctional Center (Shelby)** and **Dawson County Correctional Facility (Glendive):** these two contract prisons run their **own visiting process.** The Montana DOC directs visitors to each facility directly. Confirm the scheduling method, days, and rules with the facility itself.

:::callout{variant="tip"}
The Montana DOC notes that not all communication and visiting options are the same across DOC-contracted facilities. Confirm the current process for the specific prison where the person is held before making any arrangements.
:::

## Visiting days and hours

Visiting days and hours are set by each facility rather than by a single statewide schedule, and they can differ from one prison to the next. The current days, hours, and any limit on the number of visitors are published in each facility's own visiting materials. Confirm them with the facility before visiting.

## Arrival and check-in

Check-in takes time before the visit begins. The Montana DOC directs visitors to arrive **15–30 minutes before the scheduled visit** to complete the check-in process.

## Dress, identification, and what visitors may bring

The Montana DOC states that requirements and restrictions for visitation apply at all DOC facilities, but the specific dress code, identification rule, and limits on personal items are set in each facility's policy documents rather than on the statewide visitation page. As a general matter across prison systems:

- **Dress is modest.** Revealing, suggestive, or gang-associated clothing is typically not permitted.
- **A valid government-issued photo identification** is generally required to enter.
- **Personal items are kept to a minimum.** Phones, smartwatches, cameras, and other electronics are typically not allowed inside the visiting area.

Because Montana sets these specifics at the facility level, confirm the exact dress code, identification requirement, and item limits with the prison where the visit will take place before traveling.

## Reception and where someone enters the system

Where a person first enters the Montana prison system depends on their gender:

- **Men:** newly sentenced men are processed through the **Martz Diagnostic Intake Unit** at **Montana State Prison (Deer Lodge),** where they are assessed and classified before placement.
- **Women:** women enter through **Montana Women's Prison (Billings),** which handles women's reception.

Montana also holds some state-sentenced people in county detention centers while they await a bed at a DOC facility, so a person's location can change. See [Transfers & Finding Someone](/states/montana/guides/transfers/).

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm that the visitor is approved and that a visit is scheduled before traveling, and confirm which method applies to the specific prison: Montana State Prison and Montana Women's Prison schedule online through ICSolutions, while Crossroads and Dawson County run their own process. Dress, identification, and item rules are set at the facility level. Visiting rules and schedules change; the Montana DOC In-Person Visitation page and the individual facility are the authoritative sources.
:::
