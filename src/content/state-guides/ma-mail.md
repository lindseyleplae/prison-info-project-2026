:::key-info{title="Key info"}
**Incoming personal mail goes to the prison.** Unlike many states, Massachusetts does **not** route personal mail to an off-site scanning vendor. Address the letter with the person's **full name, commitment number, and the facility's mailing address.**

**All incoming mail is opened and inspected** before delivery. **Legal mail** is handled separately and is not opened outside the incarcerated person's presence.

**Electronic messaging is available through Corrlinks** and, since the December 2023 law, is **free** (along with phone and video). A family member sets up the account and starts the exchange; the incarcerated person replies.

**Books and publications** are governed by DOC regulation (103 CMR 481) — confirm the current rules (such as publisher- or retailer-only) with the facility before ordering.
:::

## How to address personal mail

Incoming **personal mail and photos** go **directly to the prison** — Massachusetts does not use an off-site mail-scanning vendor. Address mail with the person's **full committed name and commitment number** and the **facility's mailing address** (each facility page on this site lists its address).

> [Incarcerated person's full name and commitment number]
> [Facility name]
> [Facility mailing address]

**All incoming mail is opened and inspected** for contraband before it is delivered. Include a return address. Because the prison shown in the locator can change after a transfer, confirm the person's current facility before sending.

## Legal mail

**Legal and privileged mail** — from attorneys and courts — receives special handling and is **not opened outside the incarcerated person's presence**. Confirm the current legal-mail labeling and procedure with the specific prison.

## Electronic messaging

Massachusetts offers **electronic messaging through Corrlinks**. A family member or friend creates the account, adds the incarcerated person, and starts the exchange; the incarcerated person can reply. Since the **December 2023 law that made prison communications free**, electronic messaging — like phone and video — carries **no charge** (see [Phone & Video Calls](/states/massachusetts/guides/phone-video/)). Confirm current details when setting up the account.

## Books and publications

**Books and publications** are governed by DOC regulation **103 CMR 481**. Corrections systems commonly require that books arrive **new, directly from a publisher or approved retailer**. Because these rules change, confirm the current book and publication policy — including where they must be sent — with the specific facility before ordering.

## Verify Before Acting

:::callout{variant="warning" title="Verify Before Acting"}
Confirm the person's **current facility before sending**, since mail goes to the prison where they are held and the location can change after a transfer. Address **personal mail** with the person's **full name and commitment number** and the **facility's mailing address** — all incoming mail is **opened and inspected**, and Massachusetts does **not** use an off-site mail vendor. Send **legal mail** under the facility's legal-mail procedure (it is opened only in the person's presence). Use **Corrlinks** for free electronic messaging, and confirm the current **book and publication** rules with the facility before ordering.
:::
