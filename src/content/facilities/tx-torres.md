## Overview

The Torres Unit is a general-population men's prison near Hondo in Medina County, west of San Antonio. TDCJ established the unit in January 1993 and lists its capacity at about 1,384 beds. It holds people classified at the G1, G2, and G4 custody levels.

The unit's full name is the Ruben M. Torres Unit. Medical care is managed by UTMB and covers ambulatory medical and dental services.

## What Makes Torres Unit Different

-   Notable canine specialization — alongside security pack canines, the unit works scent-specific canines and Human Remains Detection (HRD) canines
-   Operates a print shop, security horses, and a unit garden
-   Houses a faith-based dormitory and a separate Transformational Ministry dormitory
-   Education and vocational programs include Adult Basic Education, GED, CHANGES pre-release, cognitive intervention, automotive, HVAC, and electrical trades

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Torres Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit holds a G1, G2, and G4 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. Two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences depending on the person each is visiting.

## Getting There and Parking

The Torres Unit is in a rural part of Medina County, about three miles west of Hondo and roughly 40 miles west of San Antonio. The main route runs west from San Antonio on US-90 toward Hondo, then onto the local road that reaches the unit.

:::key-info
**From San Antonio:** US-90 west toward Hondo, then the final approach to the unit; often about 50-60 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Services thin out west of San Antonio, with the nearest concentration of gas, food, and lodging in Hondo and the larger set of options back in San Antonio.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Hondo is a small city with limited commercial services. It is the nearest service center for gas, food, and groceries before the unit, with a fuller range of lodging and restaurants in San Antonio to the east.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
