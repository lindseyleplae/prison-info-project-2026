## Overview

The Ramsey Unit is a men's TDCJ prison in Rosharon, in Brazoria County south of Houston. TDCJ lists its full name as the W. F. Ramsey Unit and its capacity at about 1,865 beds. It is one of the system's older agricultural units.

The unit sits in the Rosharon prison cluster, where several TDCJ units operate within the same area. Ramsey, Stringfellow, Terrell, and other units sit close together and are easy to confuse; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to.

## What Makes Ramsey Unit Different

-   One of the system's older agricultural units, with a long history in TDCJ
-   Part of the Rosharon prison cluster, where several units sit close together and their names are easy to confuse; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, and a nearby cluster unit is a different drive and a different visitation list
-   Houses a broad G1-G5 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Located in Brazoria County south of Houston, reached by farm-to-market roads off the main highways

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Ramsey Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

TDCJ units run visitation on weekends, generally Saturday and Sunday. Visits are arranged through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal, which is where the unit's current hours and available time slots are posted. Because the portal reflects each unit's current schedule, the listed days, hours, and slot length are confirmed there rather than assumed from another unit.

Ramsey's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

Ramsey is in Rosharon, in Brazoria County south of Houston. The unit is reached by farm-to-market roads off the main highways, and it sits near several other TDCJ units in the same area.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** South on the highways toward the Rosharon area, then the local FM-road approach; often about 45 minutes

**From Angleton:** North toward Rosharon on local highways and FM roads; often about 20 minutes

**From Galveston:** West and northwest toward Brazoria County, then the Rosharon approach; often about 1 hour

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit; the trip is by car. Because Ramsey sits in a cluster of units in the same area, confirming the exact unit on the TDCJ locator before the drive avoids arriving at the wrong gate.

The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in Angleton, not near the unit. The UTMB Health Angleton Danbury Campus on East Hospital Drive operates a 24-hour hospital emergency room, and Angleton ER on East Mulberry Street is a freestanding 24-hour emergency room.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Rosharon is small, with limited commercial services. Pearland, to the north, and Angleton, to the south, are the nearest larger service centers for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, and options thin out on the rural FM-road approach to the unit. Hotel sites list chain properties in the Pearland and Angleton areas.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
