## Overview

The Pam Lychner State Jail is a men's TDCJ state jail in Humble, in the Houston metro area. TDCJ lists it at about 2,276 beds, making it one of the system's larger state jails, and it sits in unincorporated northeast Harris County, east of Humble and about 20 miles northeast of downtown Houston.

The unit holds a mix of state-jail felons, general-population inmates, and people awaiting transfer, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule.

## What Makes the Lychner Unit Different

-   Opened in July 1995 as the **Atascocita State Jail** and was later renamed the Pam Lychner State Jail; the older name still appears in some records and the TDCJ inmate locator shows the current unit assignment
-   A large urban-area state jail — TDCJ lists it at about 2,276 beds — in the Houston metro rather than a remote rural location
-   Houses a mix of state-jail (J1-J5), general-population (G1, G2), and transfer (transient) populations, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Runs substance-abuse programming (SJSAP, PRSAP) and the Prisoner Reentry Initiative, with on-site ambulatory medical, dental, and mental-health care through UTMB

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Pam Lychner State Jail. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
:::

The Lychner Unit's mixed population is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population and state-jail housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because the unit also holds people awaiting transfer, an assignment can change before a scheduled visit. Confirm the incarcerated person is still housed here on the TDCJ inmate locator before traveling, and schedule visits through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal.

## Getting There and Parking

The Pam Lychner State Jail is in Humble, in unincorporated northeast Harris County, on the northeast side of the Houston metro. Unlike TDCJ's remote rural units, it sits within the metro road network, but the trip is still by private vehicle.

:::key-info
**From downtown Houston:** Northbound highways toward the Humble area, then local roads to Atascocita Road; often about 25-35 minutes depending on traffic

**From the Houston metro generally:** The unit is on the northeast side of the metro, reached by the area's highway and beltway network into Humble

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route transit serving the unit on the weekend visiting days, so the trip is by car.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Humble and the surrounding northeast Houston metro have full commercial services — gas, food, groceries, and lodging are widely available near the unit, including along the US 59/I-69 corridor through Humble and the Atascocita area. Hotel sites list chain properties in the Humble and Atascocita area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
