## Overview

The Kyle Unit is a TDCJ prison for men with a substance-abuse treatment mission. TDCJ lists it at about 520 beds. It sits in Kyle, in Hays County, along Interstate 35 between Austin and San Marcos.

The unit is now state-operated by TDCJ. It was previously the privately run Kyle Correctional Center before TDCJ took over direct operation.

Two facts shape visiting here: the unit's treatment-program focus and its location on the I-35 corridor south of Austin.

## What Makes the Kyle Unit Different

-   A dedicated substance-abuse treatment unit, with a Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF) program and an In-Prison Therapeutic Community (IPTC)
-   Placement is treatment- and program-driven, so the population turns over and a person's assignment can change as they move through treatment phases
-   Because the mission is treatment-tied, visiting can be affected by a person's treatment phase; the format follows the incarcerated person's program status rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Now operated directly by TDCJ after running for years as the privately operated Kyle Correctional Center

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Kyle Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because the Kyle Unit is a treatment unit, a person's program phase can affect visiting. Treatment programs such as SAFPF and the IPTC run in phases — intake and orientation, the main treatment phase, and reentry planning — and visiting access during those phases is not always the same. Families should confirm with the unit whether a planned visit falls in a phase with limited or modified visiting before making the drive.

The unit is smaller than many TDCJ prisons, with about 520 beds, but the treatment schedule rather than unit size is the main variable for visiting here.

## Getting There and Parking

The Kyle Unit is in Kyle, in Hays County, on Interstate 35 between Austin and San Marcos. The unit sits along the I-35 corridor, so the approach is by major highway rather than rural farm-to-market roads.

:::key-info
**From Austin:** I-35 South toward Kyle and San Marcos; often about 30-45 minutes

**From San Marcos:** I-35 North a short distance toward Kyle; often about 15-20 minutes

**From San Antonio:** I-35 North toward San Marcos and Kyle; often about 60-75 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The trip is by private vehicle. The unit is just off Interstate 35, between Austin to the north and San Marcos to the south, so gas, food, and lodging are available in both Kyle and the nearby I-35 towns before reaching the unit.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
