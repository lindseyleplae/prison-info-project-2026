## Overview

The Southern Ohio Correctional Facility (SOCF), on State Route 728 in Lucasville, Scioto County, is **Ohio's maximum-security (Level 4) men's prison** and the site of the **state's execution chamber**. SOCF opened in **1972** and held about **1,320** men in late 2025. Ohio carries out executions at SOCF; the state has not carried out an execution since 2018 under a governor's pause. For statewide policy and procedure, see the [Ohio overview](/states/ohio/) and [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/). Because it operates at the maximum-security level, visiting procedures are more restrictive than at lower-security prisons.

## What Makes SOCF Different

- **It is Ohio's maximum-security (Level 4) prison,** so visiting rules are stricter than at lower-security facilities.
- **It houses the state's execution chamber;** Ohio has not carried out an execution since 2018 under a governor's pause.
- **Visits may be contact or non-contact** depending on the person's status, so confirm before traveling.
- **It is about 10 miles north of Portsmouth** and about 79 miles south of Columbus, via U.S. 23.

## Visiting

The statewide ODRC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Southern Ohio Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits must be reserved through ViaPath (gtlvisitme.com); the visitor must first be approved on the person's list

**Visit type:** Because SOCF is maximum security, confirm with the facility whether the person's visits are contact or non-contact

**Schedule:** SOCF sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on State Route 728 in Lucasville, in southern Ohio.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 10 miles north of Portsmouth and about 79 miles south of Columbus via U.S. 23; Lucasville is the nearest town

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Portsmouth, about 10 miles south, has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Southern Ohio Medical Center** in Portsmouth.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
