## Overview

Wilcox State Prison, on South Broad Street in Abbeville in Wilcox County, is a **medium-security state prison for men** in south-central Georgia. It houses general-population medium-security men and has a capacity of about **1,827**. The prison is also the **host facility for the Turner Residential Substance Abuse Treatment Facility**.

## What Makes Wilcox State Prison Different

- **It is a medium-security prison for general-population men.**
- **It is the host facility for the Turner Residential Substance Abuse Treatment Facility.**
- **It is in Abbeville, in south-central Georgia,** in Wilcox County.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Wilcox State Prison. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays — confirm the current hours with the facility

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on South Broad Street in Abbeville, in south-central Georgia.

:::key-info
**By car:** Abbeville is in Wilcox County, between Cordele and Fitzgerald; the prison is on South Broad Street (U.S. 129) in town

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Abbeville has limited services; Cordele, about 25 minutes west on U.S. 280, and Fitzgerald, about 20 minutes south, have the closest gas, food, and lodging. The nearest hospitals with 24-hour emergency rooms are in those towns — **Crisp Regional Hospital** in Cordele and **Dorminy Medical Center** in Fitzgerald.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
