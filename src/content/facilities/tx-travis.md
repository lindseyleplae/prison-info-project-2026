## Overview

The Travis Unit is a TDCJ state jail in Austin, in eastern Travis County. TDCJ lists it at about 1,161 beds. It operates as a state jail and runs the State Jail Substance Abuse Program.

The unit has been state-operated by TDCJ since 1999; before that, it ran under a private contract. It now follows standard TDCJ procedures.

Two facts shape much of what visiting looks like here: the unit's broad custody mix and its urban Austin location.

## What Makes the Travis Unit Different

-   Operates as a state jail and runs the State Jail Substance Abuse Program, a treatment program based at the unit
-   Houses a broad custody mix — state-jail custody levels along with G1-G2 housing, transient (in-transit) population, and youthful offenders — so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Located within Austin in eastern Travis County, making it one of the more urban and accessible TDCJ units

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Travis Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing or transient status can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because the unit moved to TDCJ state operation, families used to earlier private-operator visiting procedures should follow standard TDCJ rules. Confirming the current unit-specific day and time with TDCJ before traveling is the surest way to avoid a wasted trip.

## Getting There and Parking

The Travis Unit is in eastern Travis County, within the City of Austin in Central Texas. Unlike most TDCJ units, it sits in an urban area rather than a rural one, on FM 969 east of Highway 183.

:::key-info
**From central Austin:** East on FM 969 (Martin Luther King Jr. Boulevard) past Highway 183 toward the unit; typically a short city drive

**From Houston:** I-10 West to Austin, then north and east toward the FM 969 corridor; often about 2.5-3 hours

**From San Antonio:** I-35 North to Austin, then east toward the FM 969 corridor; often about 1.5-2 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Because the unit is inside Austin, gas, food, lodging, and other services are widely available in the surrounding city rather than concentrated in a single distant town. Travis County has extensive medical facilities throughout the Austin metro area.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
