## Overview

Women's Huron Valley Correctional Facility, in Ypsilanti near Ann Arbor, is **Michigan's only prison for women** and the **women's reception center** — every woman sentenced to the state system is processed here, and because there is no other women's prison, women of all custody levels are held here, about **1,800 as of early 2026**. The current consolidated facility opened in **2009** on the former Huron Valley site. It has general-population housing (Levels I, II, and IV), a residential treatment program and other mental-health units, an infirmary, and a Vocational Village job-training program. The facility has also been the subject of **lawsuits and news reporting over crowding and building conditions**, and a 2026 internal state assessment documented aging building systems.

## What Makes Women's Huron Valley Different

- **It is the only women's prison in Michigan.** Families from across the entire state travel to this one Ypsilanti location to visit an incarcerated woman.
- **It is also the women's reception center.** Every woman entering MDOC is processed here first, so it is the first place to locate a newly sentenced woman.
- **Visiting times are set by housing unit.** WHV runs visits seven days a week across morning, afternoon, and evening blocks, but the times depend on the unit — confirm the specific schedule.
- **It is in the Ann Arbor area,** comparatively reachable (about 15 minutes from Ann Arbor, 45 from Detroit) with nearby hospitals and transit.

## Visiting

The statewide MDOC rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Women's Huron Valley. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days:** Seven days a week, in morning, afternoon, and evening blocks — but the times depend on the **housing unit**

**Scheduling:** By appointment through the ViaPath scheduler, booked 48 hours to 7 days ahead; the visitor must be on the approved list first

**Find the schedule:** Look up the unit's current times on MDOC's WHV visiting-schedule page, since they change
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Michigan](/states/michigan/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Bemis Road in the Ann Arbor–Ypsilanti area, just off Interstate 94.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 15 minutes from Ann Arbor and roughly 45 minutes from Detroit via I-94

**By transit:** The Ann Arbor–Ypsilanti area is served by AAATA ("TheRide") buses through the Ypsilanti Transit Center; check the trip planner for the nearest stop, as routes may not reach the facility directly
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Ypsilanti and Ann Arbor area has extensive gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **Trinity Health Ann Arbor Hospital** at 5301 McAuley Drive in Ypsilanti, about five to six miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the woman's name and **MDOC number**. Michigan photocopies incoming mail in black and white and delivers the copy; the original is shredded. For a color copy, send a JPay electronic message. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/michigan/guides/mail/).
