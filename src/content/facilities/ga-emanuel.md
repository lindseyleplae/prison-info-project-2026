## Overview

Emanuel Women's Facility, on Gumlog Road in Swainsboro in Emanuel County, is a **medium-security prison for women** in east-central Georgia. It has a capacity of about **415** and offers academic, vocational, and faith-based programming. The facility is about 70 miles southwest of Augusta.

## What Makes Emanuel Women's Facility Different

- **It is a medium-security women's prison,** one of a small number of facilities in Georgia that hold women.
- **It offers academic, vocational, and faith-based programming.**
- **It is in east-central Georgia,** about 70 miles southwest of Augusta.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Emanuel Women's Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays, generally 9:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. — confirm with the facility

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Gumlog Road in Swainsboro, in east-central Georgia.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 70 miles southwest of Augusta; Swainsboro sits at the junction of U.S. 1 and U.S. 80

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Swainsboro has the closest gas, food, and lodging; Augusta, about 70 miles northeast, has the fullest range. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Emanuel Medical Center** in Swainsboro.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
