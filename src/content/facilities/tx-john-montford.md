## Overview

The John Montford Unit is TDCJ's psychiatric and medical regional hub for West Texas, located in Lubbock. TDCJ lists the unit as housing multiple custody levels, with people assigned there for mental-health and medical treatment.

That mission makes Montford different from a standard permanent-housing unit. Some incarcerated people are assigned there for ongoing psychiatric or medical care, while others are there temporarily for evaluation, treatment, or transitional care before another move.

## What Makes John Montford Unit Different

- TDCJ uses Montford as the psychiatric and medical hub for the West Texas region, with 24-hour medical, dental, and mental-health services.
- The unit has about 94 infirmary beds and 550 inpatient mental-health beds, along with a surgical suite, dialysis services, and geriatric services.
- Medical and mental-health operations are run by Texas Tech University Health Sciences Center, the correctional managed-care partner for West Texas.
- Some assignments are temporary placements for treatment rather than long-term housing, so a person may be moved as their care changes.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

Weekend visiting uses TDCJ's standard scheduling system, but medical and psychiatric operations can affect whether a person is available for a visit on a given day.

:::key-info
**Hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the John Montford Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

Medical status, treatment status, and housing status can change visit eligibility at Montford more often than at a typical general population unit. A person may be unavailable for a scheduled visit because of treatment, psychiatric placement, infirmary movement, or a transfer tied to medical needs.

That same dynamic affects temporary placements. A person may be at Montford for evaluation or treatment rather than a long-term assignment, so location records can change during a stay.

:::callout{variant="info"}
Because Montford is a psychiatric and medical unit, treatment or medical status can affect whether a visit happens on a given day. Confirm the visit directly with the unit before traveling, and check the TDCJ inmate locator to verify the person is still housed there.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Montford is within the Lubbock area, so the trip relies on Lubbock as the main service center.

:::key-info
**From Amarillo:** I-27 South to Lubbock; often about 2 hours

**From Midland:** US-87 North to Lubbock; often about 2 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in takes place at the unit's visitor processing point, where identification review and standard TDCJ screening happen before internal movement begins.
:::

Lubbock is a regional hub with a commercial airport and full city services, so visitors traveling from a distance generally pass through Lubbock before reaching the unit.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Montford uses Securus for phone service, and TDCJ lists Securus Video Connect where video visiting is available. Communication patterns can be less predictable here than at a standard permanent-housing unit because treatment and housing status can interrupt access to calls or remote contact.

Mail routing can also be affected by temporary placement. A person who is moved for treatment, psychiatric care, or evaluation may not stay at Montford long enough for communication to follow the same pattern as a stable general population assignment.

## Programs

TDCJ lists education and reentry programming at the unit alongside its treatment mission, including literacy and GED instruction, the CHANGES pre-release program, a faith-based dormitory, and reentry planning.

## Nearby Services

Most fuel, food, pharmacies, and hotel options are available throughout Lubbock rather than only near the unit entrance. As a regional city, Lubbock offers a full set of services for visitors traveling from elsewhere in West Texas.

For broader family support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
