## Overview

Denmar Correctional Center, near Hillsboro in Pocahontas County, is a **medium-security men's prison** holding about **190 men** in custody levels 1-3 (FY2024). People placed here are generally **within a few years of parole or discharge**. Its main worksite is a **correctional-industries garment operation** that produces uniforms worn by incarcerated people across West Virginia's prisons; the facility also runs substance-abuse treatment, education, and vocational programs. The site has a long institutional history: it opened in **1919 as a state tuberculosis sanatorium**, later became Denmar State Hospital, and was converted to a prison in **1993** (see the note on history below). West Virginia has no death penalty, so there is no death row.

## What Makes Denmar Different

- **It runs a correctional-industries garment operation** that makes uniforms worn across West Virginia's prisons.
- **It holds men who are generally within a few years of release,** in medium custody.
- **It is in the mountains of Pocahontas County,** off U.S. 219.
- **It sits on historic ground** — see the note below.

## Visiting

The statewide WV DCR rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Denmar.

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** WVDCR does not post Denmar's specific schedule; statewide, prison visits are Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. — call (304) 653-4201 to confirm

**How it works:** Visits are scheduled in advance by the incarcerated person, not walk-in; the visitor must be on the approved list first

**Tablet video visits:** Denmar also offers scheduled video visits through the tablet system, by request and approval
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in West Virginia](/states/west-virginia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Denmar Road near Hillsboro, off U.S. Route 219 in the eastern mountains.

:::key-info
**By car:** Hillsboro is on U.S. 219 about 30 miles north of Lewisburg and about 12 miles south of Marlinton, on a winding mountain road where cell signal is limited

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Marlinton, to the north, has the nearest cluster of food, gas, and lodging. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **Pocahontas Memorial Hospital** near Marlinton, about 9 miles north of Hillsboro.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since 2023, West Virginia routes personal letters to an **off-site scanning center in Maryland**, addressed with the person's name and **OID number**; the person receives the mail as **images on a tablet**, and photos must be sent through the tablet rather than mailed. **Legal mail, books, and magazines** go to the facility. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/west-virginia/guides/mail/).

## A Note on History

Denmar opened in **1919** as the **State Colored Tuberculosis Sanatorium**, established by the Legislature in 1917 to treat African American tuberculosis patients during the era of racial segregation. It was **desegregated in 1957** and became Denmar State Hospital, a facility for the chronically ill, which closed in 1990. The state reopened the site as **Denmar Correctional Center in 1993**. Records compiled in 1995 identified the names of hundreds of former sanatorium patients believed to be buried on the grounds. This history is documented in the sources cited above.
