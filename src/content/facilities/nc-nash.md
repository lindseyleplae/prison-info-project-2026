## Overview

Nash Correctional Institution (Nash CI) is a medium-security prison for men in Nashville, Nash County, in north-central North Carolina, near Rocky Mount. The facility is operated by the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction (NCDAC), the state agency that has run the adult prison system since it separated from the Department of Public Safety on January 1, 2023.

North Carolina uses a distributed reception model for men, with major processing handled at Craven Correctional Institution in Vanceboro and other designated centers; a person's custody level is assigned at intake. Because families are not automatically notified of routine transfers, the [NCDAC offender locator](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view) is the way to confirm where someone is currently held, using the person's name or seven-digit OPUS number.

## What Makes Nash Different

According to the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction, Nash Correctional Institution is a medium-custody reentry facility. It houses two Correction Enterprises industrial plants: a print plant that produces documents and publications for state and local government agencies, and an optical plant that manufactures eyeglasses. The institution also operates the Field Minister Education Program, a partnership with Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary that trains incarcerated students to serve as field ministers within the prison system.

## Visiting

The statewide NCDAC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Nash Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approval first:** Every visitor must be on the offender's approved visiting list. The incarcerated person mails an application to each prospective visitor (up to 18 applications); applicants 16 and older attach a copy of a government photo ID, and a minor under 16 attaches a copy of a birth certificate. The completed form goes to the prison where the person is housed.
- **Scheduling:** Visits are by appointment only. North Carolina has no statewide online visit scheduler; call or email Nash Correctional Institution to arrange a visit, and confirm visitation status the day before.
- **Visit limits:** Generally one visit per week, up to two hours, with up to three approved visitors. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's custody class and disciplinary status.
- **Identification and items:** Visitors 16 and older present a government photo ID. Phones, smartwatches, cameras, and recording devices are not permitted. The governing rule is Visitation Policy D.0200.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Nash Correctional Institution is at 2869 US 64 Alt., Nashville, NC 27856, in Nash County in north-central North Carolina, just west of Rocky Mount along U.S. 64 Alternate. There is no public-transit service to the institution; visitors generally arrive by private vehicle. Confirm visiting days and hours with the facility before traveling.

## Nearby Services

Nashville is in Nash County, with Rocky Mount adjacent to the east. The closest full-service hospital emergency rooms are in the Rocky Mount area. Lodging, fuel, and restaurants are available in Rocky Mount and along the U.S. 64 and Interstate 95 corridors.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. North Carolina sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and scans it and delivers a digital copy to the incarcerated person's tablet; original letters, cards, and packages are returned to the sender. Address personal mail with the person's full name and **OPUS number** and the full (unabbreviated) prison name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 247, Phoenix, MD 21131 — and include the sender's full first and last name in the return address. **Legal mail** is exempt and goes directly to the facility. **Books and publications** must come from a publisher or online retailer, shipped to the prison's street address, not the TextBehind box. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).
