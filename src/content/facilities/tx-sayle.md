## Overview

The Walker Sayle Unit is a Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF), one of the TDCJ facilities built around court-ordered substance-use treatment rather than general-population custody alone. A SAFPF holds men placed into an in-prison treatment program; the program runs in structured phases over a set period, and program participation is the central part of daily life at the unit. TDCJ lists the unit at about 632 beds, and it opened in August 1994 in Stephens County.

Because the unit is organized around a treatment program, two facts shape almost everything about visiting: the program structure and the unit's rural North-Central/West Texas location.

## What Makes Sayle Unit Different

-   A Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF) — the unit's organizing mission is in-prison substance-use treatment rather than general-population custody
-   Runs multiple treatment tracks on site, including an In-Prison Substance Use Treatment Program (IPSUTP), an In-Prison Therapeutic Community (IPTC), and a Pre-Release Therapeutic Community (PRTC)
-   Carries a faith-based program component alongside the treatment tracks
-   Medical care is provided through Texas Tech, the regional university health provider for West Texas TDCJ units

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Sayle. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because Sayle is a SAFPF, the treatment program is the main reason one posted schedule does not always mean one visiting experience. Treatment-program visiting can be limited or tied to a person's phase in the program, so the format and timing may differ from a general-population unit and can change as a person moves through the program. Confirm the current procedures with the unit before traveling, and verify the person's assigned unit on the TDCJ inmate locator, since a SAFPF placement can change after treatment ends.

## Getting There and Parking

Sayle is near Breckenridge in Stephens County, in North-Central/West Texas. The unit sits on a farm-to-market road outside town, and services thin out on the final approach.

:::key-info
**From the Dallas-Fort Worth area:** West on I-20 toward the Abilene corridor, then north toward Breckenridge and the FM 1800 approach; often about 2-2.5 hours

**From Abilene:** Northeast toward Breckenridge, then the FM 1800 approach; often about 60-75 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route public bus to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle. Breckenridge is the nearest service center for gas, food, and groceries before the unit, and options thin out on the final farm-to-market approach.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

Mail follows TDCJ's statewide process. Correspondence is addressed to the incarcerated person by name and TDCJ number at the unit, and what may be sent — along with what gets rejected — is covered in [Mail & Packages](/states/texas/guides/mail/). For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
