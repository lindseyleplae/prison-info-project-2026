## Overview

Northeast Correctional Center, on Airport Road in Bowling Green, is a **medium- and minimum-security prison for men** in northeastern Missouri. It opened in 1998 and sits on about 112 acres in Pike County. Visiting and movement follow the statewide Missouri DOC rules, with arrangements that vary by housing unit.

## What Makes Northeast Different

- **It is a medium- and minimum-security men's prison** in northeastern Missouri.
- **It opened in 1998** and sits on about 112 acres in Pike County.
- **It is in Bowling Green,** in the northeastern part of the state, north of St. Louis along the U.S. 61 corridor.

## Visiting

The statewide Missouri DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Northeast Correctional Center. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be approved through the Missouri DOC online visiting application before visiting

**Right facility:** People are transferred between prisons — confirm which one holds the person before traveling

**Visit type:** Visiting for men in restrictive housing may differ from general population — confirm the housing and visit type

**Schedule:** The facility sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them before traveling
:::

The full application process is in [Visiting in Missouri](/states/missouri/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Airport Road in Bowling Green, in northeastern Missouri.

:::key-info
**By car:** Bowling Green is in Pike County in northeastern Missouri, along the U.S. 61 corridor north of St. Louis; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Bowling Green and the nearby Hannibal area have gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Bowling Green and Hannibal area.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since 2022, Missouri routes incoming letters and photos to an **off-site Securus digital-mail center in Tampa, Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person's **tablet**; address them to **[person's name and DOC ID#], C/O Digital Mail Center–Missouri DOC, P.O. Box 25678, Tampa, FL 33622-5678**. **Legal mail** still goes directly to the prison, and **books** come publisher-direct to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/missouri/guides/mail/).
