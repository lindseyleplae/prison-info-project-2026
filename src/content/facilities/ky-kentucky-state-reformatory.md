## Overview

The Kentucky State Reformatory is a state prison for men operated by the Kentucky Department of Corrections. It is in La Grange, in Oldham County, about 30 miles northeast of Louisville. The facility houses men in medium custody and is one of the larger institutions in the Kentucky system.

Kentucky assigns custody level based on factors that include sentence length, time remaining, and conduct, and a person's custody level can change during incarceration. The custody class and housing unit held at a given prison determine whether visits are contact or non-contact, so families confirm the arrangement that applies to the specific person before a first visit.

## What Makes the Kentucky State Reformatory Different

The Kentucky State Reformatory is one of the system's oldest institutions, opened in 1939, and it is a large men's prison about 30 miles northeast of Louisville. Its defining feature is its mission: the reformatory is the system's specialized institution for mental health and medical care, housing a Correctional Psychiatric Treatment Unit and a medical unit that provides services such as on-site dialysis and chemotherapy. Because of that mission, the facility serves a significant share of the system's medically involved and aging population.

The reformatory is one of three correctional facilities clustered in or near La Grange in Oldham County. The Roederer Correctional Complex is the statewide assessment and classification center, where incoming male state prisoners are processed and assigned, and the Luther Luckett Correctional Complex is an adjacent medium-security men's prison with treatment programming. Because the three names are similar and the facilities are close together, a person, a court document, or an older directory can be unclear about which one applies. Confirm the person's current facility on the Kentucky Offender Online Lookup before traveling or sending mail.

## Visiting

The statewide KY DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Kentucky State Reformatory. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** Before any visit, each visitor must be on the inmate's approved visiting list. The list is set up through the institution; all adult visitors need valid government photo identification, and minors must be accompanied by an approved adult.
- **Scheduling:** Visits are arranged with the facility — Kentucky has no statewide online visit scheduler, and days and hours are set institution by institution. Some facilities require visits to be scheduled in advance. Confirm the current schedule and any advance-scheduling requirement with the facility before traveling.
- **Contact vs. non-contact:** Standard visits are contact; non-contact visits apply to people in restricted housing and are arranged by appointment. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's security level and housing unit.
- **Main line:** 502-222-9441
:::

## Getting There and Parking

The Kentucky State Reformatory is at 3001 W Hwy 146 in La Grange, in Oldham County. La Grange is northeast of Louisville and is reached by way of Interstate 71 and Kentucky Route 146. The nearest large commercial airport is in the Louisville area to the southwest.

Parking is on site. Visitors confirm current entry procedures, the visitor-processing location, and what may be brought onto the grounds with the facility before arriving, because electronic devices and personal items are not permitted inside — visitors may generally bring only identification, a car key, and coins for vending machines.

## Nearby Services

La Grange has lodging, dining, and fuel, with a wider range of options in the Louisville area to the southwest. Emergency medical care is available locally, with larger hospitals in Louisville. Visitors traveling a long distance generally find the most options for fuel, food, and overnight stays along the Interstate 71 corridor and in the Louisville area.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail goes directly to the institution, addressed to the person by their committed name and Kentucky DOC inmate number, followed by the facility's mailing address. Mail is opened and inspected for contraband before delivery; under Kentucky Corrections policy, correspondence is delivered to the person within 48 hours of receipt on normal workdays.

Legal and other privileged mail — from a licensed attorney, a court, a government official, the Department of Public Advocacy, or Corrections officials — is opened only in the person's presence and should be clearly marked as legal mail.

Books and magazines must be shipped new, directly from a publisher or an approved retailer; items sent by individuals are refused. Packages are limited to approved-vendor care packages arranged through the facility.

Verify the exact mailing address, current mail rules, and approved package vendor with the facility before sending anything, because procedures change.
