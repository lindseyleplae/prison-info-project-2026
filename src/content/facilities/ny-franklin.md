## Overview

Franklin Correctional Facility, in Malone, is a **medium-security prison** for men in Franklin County, in the far North Country near the Canadian border. It is a long drive from the downstate area, which is a real factor for visitors planning a trip.

## What Makes Franklin Different

- **It is a medium-security prison** for men.
- **It is in the far North Country,** in Malone, Franklin County, near the Canadian border.
- **It is a long drive from downstate,** so travel and timing are a real planning factor.
- **Weekend visiting may be split by the last digit of the person's DIN,** so confirm which day applies before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Franklin Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; weekend visiting may be divided by the last digit of the person's DIN — confirm the current schedule and which day applies

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Malone, in Franklin County, in the far North Country near the Canadian border — a long drive from downstate.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Malone, Franklin County, in the far northern reaches of the state; a private vehicle is required, and the distance from downstate is a real factor

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Malone and the surrounding Franklin County area have gas, food, and lodging. Services are more limited than in downstate areas given the rural North Country setting, so plan fuel and meals ahead. The nearest hospital emergency services are in the Malone area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
