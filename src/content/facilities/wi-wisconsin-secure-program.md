## Overview

The Wisconsin Secure Program Facility, on Morrison Drive in Boscobel, is the state's **administrative-maximum (supermax) prison for men**. It sits in Grant County, in southwestern Wisconsin. The facility holds people in maximum-security and restrictive housing, including those who require the highest level of security in the Wisconsin system. It operates a phased step-down behavior program through which a person can progress to less restrictive conditions over time.

## What Makes the Wisconsin Secure Program Facility Different

- **It is the state's administrative-maximum prison.** The facility is designed for the highest custody level in the Wisconsin system, and much of its housing is restrictive.
- **It uses a phased step-down program.** People housed here can move through defined behavior phases toward less restrictive conditions, rather than a single fixed regime.
- **Visiting arrangements reflect the security level.** Because the facility houses people in restrictive housing, visits are limited and are often conducted by audio-visual equipment rather than in person; the type of visit available depends on the person's housing assignment.

## Visiting

The statewide WI DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Wisconsin Secure Program Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approval:** Every visitor must first be on the incarcerated person's approved visiting list — the person mails a DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire to the prospective visitor, who completes it and returns it to the institution

**Scheduling:** Wisconsin has no statewide online visit scheduler; visits are arranged with the institution. Video visits are scheduled separately through ICSolutions

**Contact:** Visits are limited and are often conducted by audio-visual equipment rather than in person; confirm the visit type available for the person's housing assignment

**Days and hours:** Set by the facility; confirm the current schedule before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Wisconsin](/states/wisconsin/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Boscobel, in Grant County, in southwestern Wisconsin.

:::key-info
**By car:** Boscobel is in the southwestern part of the state, along the Wisconsin River; the facility is reached via local roads off Morrison Drive

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Services near the facility are limited. The Boscobel area has basic gas and food, and the larger town of Prairie du Chien, to the west, has the nearest hospital emergency room and the fullest range of services in the immediate region.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. Wisconsin sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and photocopies it and forwards copies to the institution; people with tablets may also receive electronic photos. Address personal mail with the person's full name, **DOC number**, and the full (unabbreviated) facility name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 189, Phoenix, MD 21131. **Legal and privileged mail** is exempt and goes directly to the institution at its own legal-mail address. **Books and other publications** must come from an approved vendor, shipped directly from the retailer, not from individuals. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/wisconsin/guides/mail/).
