## Overview

Chippewa Valley Correctional Treatment Facility (CVCTF) is a minimum-security state prison for men in Chippewa Falls, Wisconsin, operated by the Wisconsin Department of Corrections (WI DOC) through its Division of Adult Institutions. It is a treatment facility with a substance-abuse mission rather than a general-population institution.

Wisconsin abolished the death penalty in 1853 — the first U.S. state to do so — and has no death row and no execution chamber.

Men are assigned a security level at intake, which for the Wisconsin system takes place at Dodge Correctional Institution in Waupun. People are placed at Chippewa Valley by the department, generally for participation in its treatment programming; families do not choose the facility, and the department does not automatically notify families of a transfer. Re-check the [offender locator](https://appsdoc.wi.gov/lop/welcome) before traveling.

## What Makes Chippewa Valley Different

Chippewa Valley Correctional Treatment Facility is dedicated to substance-abuse treatment. According to the Wisconsin Department of Corrections, the facility's mission is to develop and operate a treatment program addressing offenders' substance-abuse and related needs in preparation for reintegration into their communities. It has a capacity of about 300 beds and operates at minimum security.

The facility provides the Earned Release Program (ERP) under Wisconsin Statutes section 302.05, a statutory substance-abuse treatment program. Program eligibility, content, and length are set by the department; details and current placement are confirmed with the institution.

## Visiting

The statewide WI DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Chippewa Valley Correctional Treatment Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visiting list before visiting. The person mails a DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire to the prospective visitor, who completes it and returns it to the institution; the person is notified when a visitor is approved.
- **Scheduling:** Wisconsin has no statewide online visit scheduler. In-person visits are arranged with Chippewa Valley Correctional Treatment Facility directly — call the facility at (715) 720-2850 to confirm the current visiting schedule and procedures.
- **Days and hours:** Set by the facility and subject to change. Confirm the current schedule before traveling, and arrive no more than 15 minutes before a visit.
- **Video visits:** Scheduled separately through ICSolutions, not through the facility.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Chippewa Valley Correctional Treatment Facility is at 2909 E. Park Ave. in Chippewa Falls, Chippewa County, in northwestern Wisconsin, just northeast of Eau Claire. The facility is reached by private vehicle; confirm visitor parking and entrance procedures with the institution. Public transit options to the site are limited.

## Nearby Services

Chippewa Falls is in Chippewa County in northwestern Wisconsin, adjacent to Eau Claire. The nearest hospitals with emergency rooms are in the Chippewa Falls and Eau Claire areas. Lodging, fuel, and food are available in Chippewa Falls and in Eau Claire along the Interstate 94 corridor.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. Wisconsin sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and photocopies it and forwards copies to the institution; people with tablets may also receive electronic photos. Address personal mail with the person's full name, **DOC number**, and the full (unabbreviated) facility name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 189, Phoenix, MD 21131. **Legal and privileged mail** is exempt and goes directly to the institution at its own legal-mail address. **Books and other publications** must come from an approved vendor, shipped directly from the retailer, not from individuals. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/wisconsin/guides/mail/).
