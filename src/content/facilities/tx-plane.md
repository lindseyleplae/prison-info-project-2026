## Overview

Lucile Plane State Jail is a women's facility operated by the Texas Department of Criminal Justice (TDCJ). It sits in Liberty County near Dayton, east of Houston, and TDCJ lists it at about 2,296 beds. It holds women in state-jail custody along with some prison-custody assignments.

Two facts shape most of what families need to know about visiting here: it is a women's state jail, and it is reached by rural roads off the highway grid northeast of Houston.

## What Makes Plane Different

-   A women's state jail — one of TDCJ's facilities for women in state-jail custody, with some prison-custody housing as well
-   Texas's first women's state jail, dedicated in June 1995
-   Runs the Baby and Mother Bonding Initiative (BAMBI), a mother-infant program; any residential community component tied to that program operates at a separate site, not at the Dayton unit
-   Co-located with the Henley and Hightower units on a shared campus, so the TDCJ inmate locator should be used to confirm the exact unit a person is assigned to before traveling

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Lucile Plane State Jail. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive or program-tied housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. Two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences depending on the assigned person's status.

Visits are arranged through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal. Because the Plane, Henley, and Hightower units share a campus and a switchboard, confirming the exact unit on the inmate locator before scheduling avoids arriving at the wrong visitation list.

## Getting There and Parking

Lucile Plane State Jail is in a rural section of Liberty County, north of Dayton and east of Houston. The drive starts on highways but finishes on farm-to-market roads, and services thin out over the last stretch.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** Eastbound highways toward the Dayton area, then the FM-road approach north of town; often about 60-75 minutes

**From Beaumont:** West toward the Liberty and Dayton area, then the final FM-road approach; often about 60-75 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle. Because the unit shares a campus with the Henley and Hightower units, families should confirm the exact unit on the TDCJ inmate locator before driving.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Dayton has limited commercial services. The greater Houston area to the west and the Liberty area nearby are the nearest service centers for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, and options thin out on the final farm-to-market approach to the unit.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
