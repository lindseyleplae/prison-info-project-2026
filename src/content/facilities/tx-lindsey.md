## Overview

The John R. Lindsey Unit is a men's prison in Jack County, north-central Texas, roughly 1.6 miles north of Jacksboro on FM 3344. TDCJ lists it at about 1,031 beds.

The unit began as a state jail and operated under a private contract with Management & Training Corporation. On September 1, 2025, TDCJ assumed direct operation of the facility, and it is now part of TDCJ's Correctional Institutions Division.

## What Makes the Lindsey Unit Different

-   State-operated by TDCJ since September 1, 2025 — it was formerly the Lindsey State Jail, run under private contract by MTC, and visiting, communications, and commissary procedures now follow TDCJ's statewide systems
-   Houses the full state-jail custody range (J1-J5) along with minimum-custody G1 and G2 men, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Carries out Regional Transitional Planning, a reentry and release-planning function for the region
-   Rural Jack County location, reached by farm-to-market road north of Jacksboro

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Lindsey Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because the unit moved to state operation on September 1, 2025, families used to the former private-operator procedures should now follow standard TDCJ rules. Visits are arranged through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal, and confirming the current day and time with TDCJ before traveling is worthwhile while post-transition schedules settle.

## Getting There and Parking

The Lindsey Unit is in a rural section of Jack County, about 1.6 miles north of Jacksboro on FM 3344, northwest of Fort Worth. The drive finishes on a farm-to-market road, and services thin out over the last stretch.

:::key-info
**From Fort Worth:** Northwest on highways toward Jacksboro in Jack County, then the final FM 3344 approach north of town

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The trip is by private vehicle. There is no fixed-route public transit to the unit, and the final approach is on a farm-to-market road rather than a highway grid.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
