## Overview

The A.M. "Mac" Stringfellow Unit is a men's prison near Rosharon in Brazoria County, in the bottomland southwest of Houston where several TDCJ units operate close together. TDCJ established the unit in July 1908, making it one of the oldest prison farms in the Texas system. Its official name is styled with the quoted nickname "Mac." TDCJ lists its capacity at about 1,212 beds and its custody range as G1 through G4 plus outside trusty.

It is primarily a general-population unit, but TDCJ assigns it two named treatment missions that shape who is housed there.

## What Makes Stringfellow Unit Different

-   One of the oldest units in TDCJ, established in 1908 as a Texas prison farm in the Brazoria County bottomland cluster
-   Operates a Sex Offender Treatment Program (SOTP), a TDCJ in-prison treatment program
-   Operates a Special Needs Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF) component, a court-ordered substance-abuse treatment program
-   Houses a G1-G4 custody mix plus outside trusty, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Sits in the Rosharon prison area, where several TDCJ units are close together and their names are easy to confuse

## Programs

Alongside its general-population housing, Stringfellow runs the two treatment missions TDCJ lists for the unit: a Sex Offender Treatment Program and a Special Needs Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility component. Education and career programs include literacy classes (ABE and GED), the CHANGES pre-release program, cognitive intervention, and vocational training in construction carpentry and diesel mechanics.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Stringfellow Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

Stringfellow is in a rural section of Brazoria County, south of Houston, reached on farm-to-market roads off the highway grid.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** Generally south on Highway 288 toward the Rosharon area, then the final FM-road approach to the unit; often about 45-60 minutes depending on traffic and the exact route

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Several TDCJ units cluster in the Rosharon area, and their names are easy to confuse. A nearby cluster unit is a different drive and a different visitation list, so the TDCJ inmate locator should be used to confirm the exact unit a person is assigned to before traveling.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Rosharon is small, with limited commercial services. Services for gas, food, groceries, and lodging are concentrated toward Angleton to the south, Pearland to the north, and the larger Houston metro area, rather than near the unit itself.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
