## Overview

Lake Erie Correctional Institution (LaECI), on Thompson Road in Conneaut, Ashtabula County, is a **minimum- and medium-security prison for men** in the northeast corner of Ohio on Lake Erie. It is operated by **CoreCivic**, a private company, under contract with ODRC; it holds ODRC-sentenced men and follows ODRC's statewide visiting, mail, and communication rules. LaECI holds about **1,500** men and opened in **2000**.

## What Makes LaECI Different

- **It is operated by CoreCivic,** a private company, under contract with ODRC.
- **It holds ODRC-sentenced men and follows ODRC's statewide rules** for visiting, mail, and communication.
- **It is a minimum- and medium-security prison,** in the northeast corner of Ohio on Lake Erie.

## Visiting

The statewide ODRC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Lake Erie Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** Visits are reserved in advance through ViaPath; the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Operator:** Lake Erie is operated by CoreCivic but follows ODRC's statewide visiting rules

**Schedule:** LaECI sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Conneaut, in the northeast corner of Ohio near the Pennsylvania border.

:::key-info
**By car:** Reached via Interstate 90 in the northeast corner of Ohio near the Pennsylvania border; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Conneaut has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **University Hospitals Conneaut Medical Center** in Conneaut.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
