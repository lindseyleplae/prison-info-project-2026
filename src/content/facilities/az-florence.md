## Overview

ASPC-Florence, on East Butte Avenue in Florence, is one of Arizona's **oldest prison complexes**. Its **general population was largely closed in 2022**, and most people who had been held there were transferred to other complexes; its **Central Unit** remains in use and houses the **state's execution chamber**. Because so much of the complex has been deactivated, a family member should **confirm on the ADCRR Inmate Data Search which complex actually holds the person** before planning a visit here.

## What Makes ASPC-Florence Different

- **Its general population was largely closed in 2022,** so most people formerly held here are now at other complexes.
- **The Central Unit houses the state's execution chamber.**
- **It is one of Arizona's oldest prisons,** in Florence's cluster of state prisons.
- **Confirm the person's actual complex first,** because the location shown for a past record may have changed.

## Visiting

The statewide ADCRR rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at ASPC-Florence. The complex's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Confirm location first:** Because most of the complex closed in 2022, check the ADCRR Inmate Data Search to confirm the person is actually held here before planning a visit

**Approved list:** Every visitor must be approved through the Application to Visit an Inmate before visiting

**By unit:** Any visiting days, hours, and rules are set by the specific unit — confirm them before traveling
:::

The full application process is in [Visiting in Arizona](/states/arizona/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The complex is on East Butte Avenue in Florence, southeast of Phoenix.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Florence, reached via U.S. 60 and State Route 79/287, southeast of the Phoenix metro

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the complex; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Florence has gas, food, and lodging; the Casa Grande area to the south has more. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is in the Florence–Casa Grande area (Banner Casa Grande Medical Center).

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since December 2025, Arizona routes incoming personal mail to an **off-site Securus digital-mail center in Dallas, Texas**, where it is scanned and delivered to the person's **tablet**; address it to **[person's name and ADCRR number], P.O. Box 211309, Dallas, TX 75211**. **Legal mail, publications, and parcels** still go directly to the complex. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arizona/guides/mail/).
