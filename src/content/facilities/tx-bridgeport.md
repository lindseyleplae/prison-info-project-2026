## Overview

The Bridgeport Unit is a small TDCJ prison — TDCJ lists it at about 520 beds — in Bridgeport, in Wise County, northwest of Fort Worth. It opened in August 1989 and operated under private contractors until September 1, 2025, when TDCJ assumed operations and it became a state-run unit in the Correctional Institutions Division.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's recent transition to state operation and its small size.

## What Makes the Bridgeport Unit Different

-   State-operated since September 1, 2025 — formerly the privately run Bridgeport Correctional Center, so standard TDCJ rules now apply and details should be confirmed directly with TDCJ
-   One of TDCJ's smallest units — TDCJ lists it at about 520 beds, a compact North Texas unit northwest of Fort Worth
-   Houses minimum-custody G1-G2 men, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Offers vocational and education programming on site, including GED instruction

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Bridgeport. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because the unit transitioned to state operation on September 1, 2025, the visiting schedule and procedures now follow TDCJ's standard system rather than any prior operator's rules. Confirm the current day and time directly with TDCJ before traveling, since post-transition arrangements may still be settling.

The unit's custody mix is the other variable. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

Bridgeport is in Wise County, northwest of Fort Worth. The unit sits off Highway 380 on Tenth Street, and the final approach is by local roads.

:::key-info
**From Fort Worth:** Northwest via U.S. 81/287 and Highway 380 toward Bridgeport, then the local approach to Tenth Street; often about 1-1.5 hours

**From Dallas:** West and northwest connectors toward Decatur and Bridgeport, then Highway 380; often about 1.5-2 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The trip is by private vehicle. There is no fixed-route transit to the unit, and services thin out on the final approach, so plan fuel and food stops in Bridgeport or Decatur before arriving.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
