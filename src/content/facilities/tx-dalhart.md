## Overview

The Dalhart Unit is a general-population men's prison operated by TDCJ, established in February 1995. TDCJ lists its capacity at about 780 beds, making it a comparatively small unit. It sits in Hartley County, four miles west of Dalhart on Highway 54, in the far northwest corner of the Texas Panhandle, near where the New Mexico and Oklahoma state lines meet. The unit houses G1, G2, and G4 custody levels.

The single fact that shapes visiting here most is location: the unit is in the remote far Panhandle, a long drive from the state's metropolitan areas.

## What Makes Dalhart Unit Different

-   One of TDCJ's smaller units, listed at about 780 beds
-   Located in the far northwest Panhandle, a long drive from the state's metro areas, with the nearest large city being Amarillo
-   Houses a G1, G2, and G4 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Operates an agricultural program — contract farming and grazing, security horses, security and scent-specific canines, and a unit garden
-   Medical care is managed by Texas Tech, the Panhandle correctional managed-care partner, with telemedicine and CPAP housing on site

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Dalhart Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix means one posted schedule does not produce one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

The Dalhart Unit is in Hartley County in the far Texas Panhandle, near the corner where the New Mexico and Oklahoma state lines meet. It is four miles west of Dalhart on Highway 54, with the final approach on FM 998. Amarillo, about 80 miles to the southeast, is the nearest large city and the nearest source of full traveler services.

:::key-info
**From Amarillo:** US-87 and US-385 northwest toward Dalhart, then Highway 54 and the FM 998 approach; often about 1.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Because the unit sits in a sparsely populated part of the Panhandle, services thin out well before the final stretch, and the drive from any metro area is long. Families confirm the incarcerated person's current unit assignment on the TDCJ inmate locator before traveling, since the distance makes a wasted trip costly.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/). Mail rules and what gets rejected are covered in [Mail & Packages](/states/texas/guides/mail/).

## Nearby Services

Dalhart is a small Panhandle city and the nearest source of gas, food, groceries, and lodging close to the unit, though commercial options are limited. Amarillo, about 80 miles southeast, is the nearest full service center with a broader range of hotels, restaurants, and stores. Options thin out significantly across the open country between the two.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
