## Overview

SCI Benner Township, on Institution Drive in Bellefonte in central Pennsylvania, is a **medium-security prison for men**. It opened in 2013 and is one of the newer State Correctional Institutions in Pennsylvania, with a capacity of about **2,250**. It sits in Centre County near State College, on the same general grounds as SCI Rockview, which closed in early 2026.

## What Makes Benner Township Different

- **It is medium security,** holding men in general population.
- **It opened in 2013,** one of the newer State Correctional Institutions in Pennsylvania.
- **It is in central Pennsylvania near State College,** in Centre County.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Benner Township. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** Benner Township sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Institution Drive in Bellefonte, in Centre County in central Pennsylvania near State College.

:::key-info
**By car:** In the Bellefonte area near State College, off I-99 and I-80 — take local roads to Institution Drive

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The State College and Bellefonte area has the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the State College and Bellefonte area.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
