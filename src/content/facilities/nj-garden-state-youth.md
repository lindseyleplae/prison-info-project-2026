## Overview

Garden State Youth Correctional Facility, near Trenton in Burlington County, holds **younger adult men** — a youthful-offender institution, not a juvenile detention center. It opened in **1968** as the Youth Reception and Correction Center in Yardville and was later renamed. The population skews young, roughly **17 to 30**, and the facility emphasizes **education and vocational training**, including college courses through the NJ-STEP program. It held about **1,030 men** as of January 1, 2026 and holds men across a range of custody levels, from minimum to maximum.

## What Makes Garden State Different

- **It is a youthful-offender facility for younger adult men**, with a population that skews young — not a juvenile facility, but a state prison for adults.
- **Education and vocational programming are central**, including high-school completion, trades, and college classes through the NJ-STEP program.
- **It is near the state capital**, about 15 minutes southeast of Trenton off the I-195 corridor — comparatively convenient for visitors coming from across central New Jersey.

## Visiting

The statewide NJDOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Garden State. The facility's own schedule, which depends on a person's unit and status:

:::key-info
**Weekday — non-contact visits:** general population and restorative housing, in the evening (about 7 to 8 p.m.)

**Weekend — contact visits:** Saturday and Sunday, with staggered registration times by unit

**Scheduling:** Call (609) 298-6300 ext. 2598, 2959, or 2444, Monday through Thursday, 8 a.m. to 4 p.m., at least 48 hours ahead — and confirm which unit's schedule applies
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in New Jersey](/states/new-jersey/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The facility is on Hogback Road in Crosswicks, southeast of Trenton.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 15 minutes southeast of Trenton, off the I-195 and New Jersey Turnpike (Exit 7) corridor

**By transit:** This is a rural area with no transit stop at the facility; a car is the practical way to get there
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Hamilton and Bordentown area, along the Route 130 and I-195 corridor, has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **RWJ University Hospital Hamilton** at 1 Hamilton Health Place in Hamilton, a few miles away.

## Mail

Personal **letters** do not go to the prison — they go to the state's **off-site scanning center**, addressed with the person's name, **SBI number**, and the facility's code (**1408**), to **P.O. Box 96777, Las Vegas, NV 89193** (confirm the code and current format on NJDOC's Staying Connected page). **Legal mail and publications** go directly to the facility at **55 Hogback Road, Crosswicks, NJ 08515**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-jersey/guides/mail/).
