## Overview

The Louisiana Correctional Institute for Women is the state's **only prison for women**, and it was rebuilt after a long displacement. The prior facility in St. Gabriel flooded in August 2016 and was evacuated, and for nine years the state's women were dispersed to temporary sites — chiefly the former Jetson youth center near Baker, and a private facility in Tallulah. A rebuilt, $160 million LCIW **opened on a new footprint at St. Gabriel in August 2025**, engineered against future flooding, and the women have been consolidated back to it. The new campus includes dormitories, a medical building, education and vocational space, and a **postpartum unit** for women who give birth in custody. LCIW holds every custody level, is the **reception point for women** entering the state system, and houses the state's **female death row**.

## What Makes LCIW Different

- **It is the state's only women's prison**, so any woman serving a Louisiana state sentence in a state facility is here.
- **It reopened in 2025.** The population has moved back to St. Gabriel from the temporary Jetson site in Baker, and procedures at a new facility are still settling.
- **Visiting depends on custody level**, including a separate schedule for death row — the women's death row is here, not at Angola.
- **Pregnancy care is on site.** Louisiana law sharply limits restraints on a pregnant woman — none during labor, delivery, transport, or postpartum recovery, and no waist restraints in those situations.

## Visiting

The statewide DPS&C rules above — the approved-list application, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at LCIW. The posted schedule runs by custody level, all from 9:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m., with **no admission after 1:15 p.m.**:

:::key-info
**General population and maximum custody:** Friday

**Contact visits (medium and minimum custody):** Saturday and Sunday

**Death row:** Monday and Wednesday, one approved visitor — non-contact, with two contact visits a year

**How many and how often:** Up to 6 adults for minimum custody, 4 for medium, 2 for maximum, 1 for death row; two visits a month, up to two hours, with minimum-custody women allowed an extra four hours a month for immediate family
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Louisiana](/states/louisiana/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

LCIW is on Highway 74 in St. Gabriel, next to Elayn Hunt, southeast of Baton Rouge.

:::key-info
**From Baton Rouge:** About 16 miles — roughly 25 minutes

**From New Orleans:** About 70 miles northwest

**Transit:** No public transit serves the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. DPS&C publishes no specific visitor parking details for the new facility.

## Nearby Services

St. Gabriel is small; food, gas, and lodging are fullest in Gonzales and Baton Rouge, each within about 20 to 25 minutes. Our Lady of the Lake Ascension Emergency Room at 1125 Highway 30 in Gonzales is open 24/7 — and Baton Rouge's Our Lady of the Lake Regional Medical Center is the region's Level I trauma center, about 20 minutes away.
