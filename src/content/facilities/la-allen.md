## Overview

Allen Correctional Center, near Kinder in Allen Parish, **opened in 1990** and was **operated under private contract** (by Wackenhut, later the GEO Group) for about 27 years before **coming under state operation in 2017**. It is now one of the eight DPS&C state-operated adult prisons, holding men across a **mix of custody levels** — from minimum-custody trusties through maximum custody. DPS&C does not publish a current population for the facility.

Allen is organized around **reception and reentry**: it serves as a reception and assignment point for people entering the system from several high-population parishes, and houses a regional reentry program. Its services include on-site medical, psychiatric, and psychological care, and a broad program slate — high-school-equivalency and adult education, vocational trades (culinary arts, cabinet making, computer office practices, upholstery, and horticulture), college coursework through Upper Iowa University, substance-abuse and faith-based programs, and a 100-hour pre-release program required by state law.

## What Makes Allen Different

- **It is a reception and reentry facility,** so some men are here for intake and assignment or for pre-release programming rather than long-term housing.
- **Its operation passed from a private company to the state in 2017,** so older sources may still describe it as a private prison.
- **Visiting is restricted on fifth weekends** for medium- and maximum-custody people.
- **It is in southwest Louisiana,** about 40 minutes from Lake Charles.

## Visiting

The statewide DPS&C rules above — the approved-list application, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Allen.

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.; no visitor is admitted after 2:00 p.m.

**Contact vs. non-contact:** Visits are contact visits; in a month with a fifth weekend, medium- and maximum-custody visiting is restricted

**How many and how often:** Up to 10 approved visitors on the list, with up to 6 (children included) at one visit; two visits a month

**Screening:** Picture ID required (see the statewide rules above); visitors and vehicles may be searched
:::

In the visiting area, a visitor buys a "Zip Key" from a vending machine to use the visiting-room vending machines. The full approval process is in [Visiting in Louisiana](/states/louisiana/guides/visiting/).

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. DPS&C publishes no specific visitor parking details for the facility.

## Nearby Services

Kinder, a few miles south, has the nearest food and gas, and the casinos near Kinder add some lodging. **Allen Parish Community Healthcare** at 108 Sixth Avenue in Kinder runs a 24/7 emergency room in the same town as the prison; Lake Charles, about 40 miles southwest, has the nearest larger hospitals.

## A Note on History

Allen Correctional Center was built by the state and **opened in 1990**, then run for about 27 years by a private operator under contract. After the operator ended its contract, **DPS&C assumed direct operation on September 1, 2017**, and re-tasked the facility toward reception and reentry. This is documented in the sources cited above.
