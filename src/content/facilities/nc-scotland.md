## Overview

Scotland Correctional Institution is a state prison for men in Laurinburg, the seat of Scotland County, in the southern North Carolina Sandhills near the South Carolina line. The facility houses men in close, medium, and minimum custody and is operated by the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction (NCDAC).

Men's reception in North Carolina uses a distributed model: Craven Correctional Institution in Vanceboro is a major processing center, Piedmont Correctional Institution in Salisbury receives western men age 22 and older with sentences under ten years, and Central Prison in Raleigh runs a special-population diagnostic. Because families are not notified of routine transfers, the [NCDAC offender search](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view) is the way to confirm where someone is currently held.

## What Makes Scotland Different

Scotland Correctional Institution is one of North Carolina's largest and newer high-security prisons for men. It holds three custody levels on one campus — close custody, the highest general-population security level in the North Carolina system, alongside medium- and minimum-custody populations. The custody level assigned to an individual affects day-to-day movement and whether visits are contact or non-contact, so the rules a family encounters can differ from one person to another at the same prison. The current custody assignment and housing for a particular person can be confirmed through the prison or on the [NCDAC offender search](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view).

## Visiting

The statewide NCDAC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Scotland Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approval first:** Every visitor must be on the offender's approved visiting list. The incarcerated person mails an application to each prospective visitor (up to 18 applications); applicants age 16 and older attach a copy of a photo ID, and minors under 16 a copy of a birth certificate. The completed form goes to the prison where the person is housed.
- **Scheduling:** Visits are by appointment only. North Carolina has no statewide online scheduler — call or email Scotland Correctional Institution to schedule, and confirm visitation status the day before.
- **Visit limits:** Generally one visit per week, up to two hours, with up to three approved visitors. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's custody class and disciplinary status; for men in close custody, visiting may be more restricted.
- **Video visits:** Available separately through the GettingOut Visits app (ViaPath), on demand or scheduled.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Scotland Correctional Institution is at 22385 McGirts Bridge Road, Laurinburg, NC 28353, in Scotland County, in the southern North Carolina Sandhills near the South Carolina line. Laurinburg lies along U.S. Route 74 between Rockingham to the west and Lumberton to the east. There is no scheduled public-transit service to the facility; visitors generally arrive by private vehicle, and parking is on site. Confirm visiting days and hours, and the person's custody and visitation status, with the facility before traveling.

## Nearby Services

Laurinburg is the seat of Scotland County. The nearest full-service hospital emergency room is in Laurinburg, with additional hospitals in the Pinehurst area to the north. Lodging, fuel, and restaurants are available in Laurinburg and along the U.S. Route 74 corridor.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. North Carolina sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and scans it and delivers a digital copy to the incarcerated person's tablet; original letters, cards, and packages are returned to the sender. Address personal mail with the person's full name and **OPUS number** and the full (unabbreviated) prison name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 247, Phoenix, MD 21131 — and include the sender's full first and last name in the return address. **Legal mail** is exempt and goes directly to the facility. **Books and publications** must come from a publisher or online retailer, shipped to the prison's street address, not the TextBehind box. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).
