## Overview

Hospital Galveston is the statewide TDCJ inpatient hospital, established in 1983. It sits at the top of the TDCJ medical system, which TDCJ and the University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB) run together as Correctional Managed Care.

TDCJ lists the hospital as providing medical, surgical, intensive care, and holding beds, along with acute hospital care and dozens of specialty and subspecialty clinics. It serves both men and women from prisons across Texas.

People are at Hospital Galveston for treatment, not permanent assignment. A person admitted here arrives from another unit for inpatient or acute care and returns to that unit once treatment is complete.

## What Makes Hospital Galveston Different

- It is the statewide TDCJ inpatient hospital, so a person in any TDCJ prison may be sent here for major medical care.
- It is run with UTMB as part of the TDCJ Correctional Managed Care system.
- It is co-gender, serving both men and women statewide.
- Patients are here temporarily, so location records change as people are admitted and discharged back to their assigned units.

## Visiting

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply, but Hospital Galveston is a hospital, so visiting works differently.

Because it is a hospital, visiting is tied to the patient's medical condition. The official TDCJ unit page publishes no visiting schedule for Hospital Galveston. Per TDCJ practice, normal visits are generally on weekends, and patients who are critically ill may be visited more often. The approved-visitor list and TDCJ's statewide visitation rules still apply.

Hours and rules vary with the patient's medical condition and must be confirmed with the hospital before traveling. A scheduled visit can be affected by the patient's treatment, condition, or movement within the hospital, so the hospital is the source for what is possible on a given day.

## Getting There and Parking

Hospital Galveston is in Galveston, in Galveston County, on Harborside Drive and reached from I-45.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** I-45 South to Galveston; about 50 miles and often around an hour

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at or near the facility.

**Entry:** Check-in takes place at the facility's visitor processing point, where identification review and standard TDCJ screening happen before any visit.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Hospital Galveston is listed with Securus for phone service and Securus Video Visitation. Because patients are here temporarily for medical care, phone and video access depends on the person's medical condition rather than a fixed unit routine.

Mail and other communication may also follow a person differently than at a permanent unit. Someone admitted for treatment may not stay long enough for communication to match the pattern of a stable unit assignment. The Texas guides below cover how mail, phone, and video work across TDCJ.

## Nearby Services

Galveston has a full range of fuel, food, pharmacies, and lodging. Visitors generally find these services in the city before reaching the facility.

For broader family support beyond the facility itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
