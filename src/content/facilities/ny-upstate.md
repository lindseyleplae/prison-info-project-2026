## Overview

Upstate Correctional Facility, in Malone, is a **maximum-security prison** for men in the far North Country, near the Canadian border. Like all maximum-security facilities, visiting and movement are more controlled than at lower-security prisons, and arrangements can differ by housing unit.

## What Makes Upstate Different

- **It is a maximum-security prison,** so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted.
- **It was built around high-security and segregated-confinement housing,** and it operates an active Special Housing Unit (SHU) for disciplinary confinement.
- **Visiting may differ by housing unit,** so confirm the person's unit and visit type before traveling.
- **It is in Malone, Franklin County,** in the far North Country — a long drive from downstate New York.

:::callout{variant="info"}
**Upstate Correctional Facility is at an address on Bare Hill Road, but it is a separate prison from the former Bare Hill Correctional Facility, which DOCCS closed in March 2026.** Despite the shared road name, Upstate remains open. Confirm the exact facility holding the person on the DOCCS Incarcerated Lookup before traveling.
:::

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Upstate Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's current schedule, which may differ by housing unit — confirm the current days and hours

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Malone, in Franklin County, in the far North Country near the Canadian border — a long drive from downstate New York.

:::key-info
**By car:** At 309 Bare Hill Road in Malone, Franklin County; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Malone and the surrounding Franklin County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more options in the larger North Country towns. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is in the Malone area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
