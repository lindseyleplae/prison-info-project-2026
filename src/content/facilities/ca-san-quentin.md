## Quick Facts

## Visiting Hours & Basics

San Quentin operates on a modified visiting schedule. **Always confirm current hours** with the facility directly, as schedules change and visits may be suspended.

:::key-info
**Standard visiting hours:** Generally Saturday and Sunday, 9 AM to 12 PM (noon) and 1 PM to 3 PM. Some weekday evening visits may be available.

**No contact visits:** Video visitation is available through the Video Visitation system for approved visitors.

**What to bring:** Valid ID (required). No bags, phones, or personal items beyond ID and money for vending machines.
:::

### Getting There from the Bay Area

San Quentin's location on a peninsula in Marin County actually makes it one of the more accessible California prisons if you're coming from the Bay Area:

:::callout{variant="tip"}
**Preferred route:** Take the Richmond-San Rafael Bridge (I-580 to CA-37 North) from the East Bay or cross the Golden Gate Bridge from San Francisco. From the bridge, follow signs toward San Rafael, then head north on US-101. San Quentin is just off the highway with clear signage.
:::

**From SF/Peninsula:** Golden Gate Bridge → US-101 North → San Quentin exit (approximately 45-60 minutes depending on traffic).

**From East Bay:** I-580 West (Richmond-San Rafael Bridge) → US-101 North → San Quentin exit (approximately 1-1.5 hours).

**From Marin County:** If you're already in Marin, San Quentin is very accessible—just 10-20 minutes north of San Rafael depending on where you're starting.

### Parking & Visitor Entrance

:::key-info
**Parking:** There is visitor parking at the facility. Arrive early on weekends, as parking can fill up, especially during peak visiting hours.

**Entrance procedure:** Check in at the visitor control gate. Have your ID ready. Allow 15-20 minutes for the check-in process, which includes a search and security screening.

**What's not allowed:** No weapons, drugs, contraband, or outside food/drinks. Bags are not permitted in the visiting room.
:::

### What Makes San Quentin Different

San Quentin is California's oldest prison (founded 1852) and holds a unique place in the state system:

-   **Historical significance:** San Quentin carries a lot of history and notoriety. The facility itself is on a beautiful but strict peninsula location.
-   **Media presence:** San Quentin gets significant media attention. Be aware that visits may be restricted during periods of high-profile incidents or events.
-   **Visiting environment:** The facility is generally well-organized for visitors compared to some other institutions, though it remains a maximum-security prison with all the restrictions that entails.
-   **Tech access:** San Quentin offers video visitation, which can be easier than in-person visits during restricted periods.

## Nearby Food & Gas

San Quentin is located on a peninsula with limited immediate commercial areas, but you'll find services in nearby San Rafael and Larkspur:

:::key-info
**Gas:** Shell, Chevron, and other major brands along US-101 near the San Rafael/San Quentin area.

**Food:** San Rafael (about 5-10 minutes away) has numerous restaurants, coffee shops, and fast-food options. Larkspur, also nearby, has good food options.

**Inside facility:** Limited vending machines available. Bring cash for snacks/drinks if you'll be there during long visiting windows.
:::
