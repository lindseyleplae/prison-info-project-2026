:::key-info
**Location:** Crescent City in Del Norte County, about 13 miles south of the Oregon border.

**Capacity:** About 3,700 designed beds across Level II, Level IV, and minimum support housing.

**Notable feature:** Large coastal prison on roughly 275 acres with a long history as one of CDCR's highest-security institutions.

**Visitation:** Friday-Sunday in-person visiting through VSA, with separate rules for RHU and modified-program housing.
:::

## Overview

Pelican Bay State Prison opened in 1989 to house a growing maximum-security population. CDCR says the institution now includes Level II and Level IV housing, a minimum support facility, and multiple rehabilitative and educational programs.

The prison remains one of the state's most isolated major institutions. Its location near Crescent City and the Pacific coast shapes both travel planning and visitation logistics, particularly during periods of heavy rain or coastal road restrictions.

## Visiting Basics

Pelican Bay's current general schedule is Friday from 12:30 p.m. to 6:45 p.m. and Saturday-Sunday from 8:45 a.m. to 2:45 p.m. The visiting status page states that RHU visits must be scheduled and run on a shorter schedule: Friday from 12:00 p.m. to 3:00 p.m. and Saturday-Sunday from 8:15 a.m. to 11:15 a.m.

Regular appointments are made through CDCR's Visitation Scheduling Application. Pelican Bay states that walk-in visits are available for certain general population housing areas, while RHU visits are not eligible for walk-in processing. General statewide rules remain in [Visiting in California](/states/california/guides/visiting/).

## Directions

From Eureka, the standard route is north on US-101 to Crescent City, then onto local roads toward Lake Earl Drive. The trip follows the North Coast corridor for most of the drive, with the final segment on local roads near the prison.

## Parking and Entry

Parking is available on-site. Pelican Bay's visiting page directs travelers to check current road conditions before leaving, and the institution notes that weather or highway issues can affect access on the North Coast.

Visitors going to RHU or a modified-program housing area should confirm their appointment status before traveling. Pelican Bay also states that walk-in processing is limited to designated general population areas.

## Nearby Services

Crescent City has gas stations, grocery stores, chain food, and a limited hotel supply along US-101 and major commercial streets before the final approach to the prison. Services become more limited north of the main commercial area and around Lake Earl Drive.
