## Overview

Muskegon Correctional Facility, on S. Sheridan Drive in Muskegon, is a **Level II men's prison** on Michigan's western lakeshore. It opened in **1974**. It has a **faith-based housing unit** and hosts the **Hope-Western Prison Education Program** — a partnership of Hope College and Western Theological Seminary that offers an accredited bachelor's degree to incarcerated students at no cost to them. It sits next to the separate Earnest C. Brooks Correctional Facility, also on S. Sheridan Drive.

## What Makes Muskegon Different

- **It has a faith-based housing unit.**
- **It hosts a bachelor's-degree program** (Hope-Western Prison Education Program).
- **It is one of two prisons on S. Sheridan Drive** (with Brooks), so confirm which one holds the person.
- **It is on the western lakeshore,** reachable from Grand Rapids (about 40 miles east).

## Visiting

The statewide MDOC rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Muskegon. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Scheduling:** All visits are by appointment, through the ViaPath scheduler (midoc.gtlvisitme.com), 48 hours to 7 days ahead

**Approved list:** Each visitor must first be approved on the prisoner's visiting list (apply with form CAJ-103)

**Which facility:** Confirm whether the person is at Muskegon or the neighboring Earnest C. Brooks Correctional Facility
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Michigan](/states/michigan/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on S. Sheridan Drive in Muskegon, in the Muskegon metro area.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 41 miles west of Grand Rapids via Interstate 96; in the Muskegon metro area

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Muskegon has the nearest gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Trinity Health Muskegon** in Muskegon.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's name and **MDOC number**. Michigan **photocopies incoming mail in black and white and delivers the copy; the original is shredded**, so do not send originals you want kept. Color photos and messages can be sent through JPay. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/michigan/guides/mail/).
