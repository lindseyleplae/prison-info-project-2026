## Overview

The Marshall County Correctional Facility, in Holly Springs, is a **state-run prison for men** in minimum and medium custody, holding about **830 people** (September 2025). It opened in 1996 and was run by a private company under contract for years; in **September 2021, MDOC took over direct operation** of the facility after staffing problems under the private operator. It is now an MDOC state institution, so the statewide Mississippi visiting rules apply.

## What Makes Marshall County Different

- It is one of the prisons MDOC **brought back under direct state operation** (in 2021), after years of private management.
- It holds **minimum- and medium-custody men** in north Mississippi, the closest state prison to the Memphis and Holly Springs area.
- Visiting runs through a **visitation center**, from which visitors are **bused to the housing units**, and the visiting day depends on the person's unit.

## Visiting

The statewide MDOC rules above — the inmate-initiated application, the approved list, the dress code, ID, and screening — apply at Marshall County. MDOC posts no visiting schedule, and the visiting day depends on the housing unit. Visitors are processed at the facility's visitation center and bused to the units, with the last entry early in the afternoon, so confirm the day and cutoff time by phone before traveling. There are no video visits at MDOC state prisons. The full approval process is in [Visiting in Mississippi](/states/mississippi/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

Holly Springs is in north Mississippi, off U.S. 78 and Highway 7.

:::key-info
**From Memphis, TN:** About 50 miles southeast on U.S. 78, then local roads — roughly 1 hour

**From Oxford:** About 30 miles south on Highway 7 — roughly 35-40 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available on-site; visitors are processed at the visitation center.
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. There is no public transit to the facility.

## Nearby Services

Holly Springs has gas, food, and some lodging, with more in Oxford and across the state line toward Memphis. The Holly Springs hospital's emergency room has faced closure threats in recent years, so confirm it is open before relying on it; a dependable 24-hour emergency room is **Baptist Memorial Hospital–North Mississippi** in Oxford, about 30 miles south.

## Mail

Mail to a person at Marshall County follows MDOC's process: addressed with the person's committed name and MDOC number. Mississippi photocopies incoming personal mail rather than delivering the originals; full addressing details and what is accepted are in [Mail & Packages](/states/mississippi/guides/mail/).
