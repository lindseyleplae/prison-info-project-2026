## Overview

Edgecombe Residential Treatment Facility, in Manhattan, is a **minimum-security Residential Treatment Facility (RTF)** for men and women. As an RTF, it is a community-reentry and work-release-type facility rather than a standard prison, and the people held there are in a reentry status. It is located in the Washington Heights area of New York City.

## What Makes Edgecombe Different

- **It does not run a standard visiting program.** Confirm any arrangements directly with the facility.
- **It is a Residential Treatment Facility (RTF),** a community-reentry / work-release-type facility rather than a standard prison.
- **It is in New York City** — on Edgecombe Avenue in Manhattan (New York County).
- **Its incoming-mail handling differs from other DOCCS prisons** (see Mail, below).

## Visiting

**Edgecombe does not run a standard visiting program.** There is no standard weekend or contact-visiting schedule published for the facility, and visiting arrangements cannot be assumed. Confirm the current arrangements directly with the facility before traveling, and confirm the person's current facility on the DOCCS Incarcerated Lookup.

The full statewide visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Mail

Edgecombe is the one DOCCS facility where incoming personal mail is **not** handled the way it is at other New York state prisons. Because its mail handling differs from the statewide rule, confirm the current mail process directly with the facility before sending anything. Address mail with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. The general statewide rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
