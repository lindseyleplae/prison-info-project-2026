## Overview

The Allan B. Polunsky Unit is a high-security prison in Polk County. TDCJ identifies it as the location for men on Texas death row, in addition to other high-security and restrictive-housing populations.

## Death Row Visiting

Death row visits are non-contact. In practical terms, that means the visitor and the incarcerated person are separated by glass or another physical barrier, and no physical contact occurs during the visit.

### What non-contact means during the visit

-   No handshake, embrace, or handholding
-   Conversation through the unit's non-contact setup
-   Closer staff control over movement and timing
-   More reliance on exact identification, scheduling, and housing status

:::reality-check
For death row visits, the separation is physical and procedural. The visit is more controlled than a standard contact visit and does not convert to contact based on travel distance alone.
:::

## Visiting Logistics

-   TDCJ scheduling rules still apply.
-   Death-row status and restrictive housing can affect available days and internal movement.
-   The unit should be contacted or the portal checked before travel.

## Directions From Houston

Livingston is roughly 75 miles north to northeast of Houston depending on the route.

-   Use US-59 North toward Livingston.
-   Move west toward FM 350 after reaching the Livingston area.
-   Continue south on FM 350 to the unit.

The final stretch is more rural than the interstate portion of the trip.

## Parking and Nearby Services

-   Parking is available at the unit.
-   Livingston has basic food, gas, and lodging options.
-   Services become more limited away from town and along FM roads closer to the facility.

:::callout{variant="warning"}
The Polunsky Unit is not in a major commercial corridor. Fuel, food, and restroom stops are easier to manage before leaving the Livingston retail area.
:::
