## Overview

Topeka Correctional Facility is a state prison for women operated by the Kansas Department of Corrections. It is in Topeka, the state capital and the seat of Shawnee County, in northeast Kansas. The facility holds women across all custody levels, meaning women in minimum, medium, and higher custody are housed within the one institution.

Kansas DOC assigns custody level based on factors that include sentence length, time remaining, and conduct, and a person's custody level can change during incarceration. The custody class and housing unit held at a given prison affect visiting arrangements, so families confirm the arrangement that applies to the specific person before a first visit.

## What Makes the Topeka Correctional Facility Different

Topeka Correctional Facility is the only state prison for women in Kansas. All women in Kansas DOC custody are housed here, and it has served as the state's women's facility since 1995.

It is also the statewide women's reception and intake point. Every woman entering Kansas DOC custody is first received and processed here — through medical, mental-health, and classification screening — before being assigned to a permanent housing unit within the facility. During this reception period, family visits are restricted: only attorneys, clergy, and law enforcement may visit until the resident advances past intake. (Men entering Kansas DOC custody are processed instead at the El Dorado Correctional Facility, the statewide men's reception center.)

Topeka Correctional Facility is the facility designated to hold any woman sentenced to death in Kansas; no woman is currently held under a death sentence. Kansas retains the death penalty in law but has not carried out an execution since 1965. This information is provided as neutral reference; the facility otherwise operates as a general women's prison and reception center.

## Visiting

The statewide KDOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Topeka Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** Every visitor must be on the resident's pre-approved visitation list before any visit can be scheduled.
- **Scheduling:** Reservations are made statewide through ICSolutions at [icsolutions.com](https://www.icsolutions.com), at least 72 hours before visiting begins for that week. Visitors who are not online can contact the facility's visitation clerk.
- **During reception/intake:** Family visits are not available while a woman is in reception; only attorneys, clergy, and law enforcement may visit until the resident advances past intake.
- **Days and hours:** Set by the facility — confirm the current visiting schedule before traveling. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's custody level and housing unit.
- **Main line:** (785) 296-3432
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Topeka Correctional Facility is at 815 S.E. Rice Rd. in Topeka, in Shawnee County in northeast Kansas. Topeka is reached by way of Interstate 70, Interstate 470, and U.S. Highway 75. The nearest large commercial airport is in the Kansas City area, to the east along Interstate 70.

Parking is on site. Visitors confirm current entry procedures, the visitor-processing location, and what may be brought onto the grounds with the facility before arriving, because electronic devices and personal items are generally not permitted inside — visitors typically may bring only identification, a car key, and coins for vending machines.

## Nearby Services

Topeka has lodging and dining, with a wider range of options in the Kansas City area to the east. Emergency medical care is available locally, with larger hospitals in Topeka and the Kansas City area. Visitors traveling a long distance generally find the most options for fuel, food, and overnight stays along the Interstate 70 corridor.

## Mail

Kansas sends mail straight to the facility — there is no off-site scanning vendor. The Kansas Department of Corrections accepts only letters and photographs through the mail; it will not accept other items mailed into a facility. Every envelope must show the resident's full name and KDOC registration number, plus the sender's name and return address — mail missing this information is not delivered. Incoming and outgoing mail that is not legal, official, or privileged may be inspected or read.

Address personal mail to the resident at the Topeka Correctional Facility:

> Resident Full Name + KDOC Registration Number
> Topeka Correctional Facility
> 815 S.E. Rice Rd.
> Topeka, KS 66607

Legal, official, and privileged mail goes to the facility and is handled separately. Books, publications, and packages are not sent as ordinary letter mail; confirm the current approved-vendor and publisher-direct rules with the facility before ordering. Mail rules are set by Kansas Administrative Regulation 44-12-601 and KDOC policy IMPP 12-120.
