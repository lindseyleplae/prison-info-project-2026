## Overview

Cayuga Correctional Facility, in Moravia, is a **medium-security prison** for men in the Finger Lakes region of central New York. As a medium-security facility, it offers contact visiting within posted hours once a visitor is approved.

## What Makes Cayuga Different

- **It is a medium-security prison,** so visiting is generally contact visiting within posted hours.
- **Visiting days may be split by the last digit of the person's DIN,** so the day a family may visit can depend on the calendar date.
- **It is in the Finger Lakes region,** in Moravia, Cayuga County, in central New York.
- **Incoming personal mail to incarcerated people uses a separate P.O. box** from the facility's general mail.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Cayuga Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; visiting days may be assigned by the last digit of the person's DIN — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Moravia, in Cayuga County, in the Finger Lakes region of central New York.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Moravia, Cayuga County, reached from State Route 38A; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Moravia and the surrounding Cayuga County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Auburn and Ithaca areas. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Auburn/Ithaca area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
