## Overview

Phoenix West Correctional and Rehabilitation Facility, on West Cocopah Street in southwest Phoenix, is a **minimum-custody prison for men**. It is **operated by the GEO Group**, a private company, under contract with ADCRR, and has held Arizona state inmates under that contract since 1996. The facility has about **500 beds** and focuses on reentry and DUI programming. Like other ADCRR facilities, it follows ADCRR's statewide visiting, mail, and communication rules, and visiting is arranged at the facility level.

## What Makes Phoenix West Different

- **It is operated by the GEO Group under contract with the state,** rather than directly by ADCRR, while still following ADCRR's statewide rules.
- **It is a minimum-custody men's prison** focused on reentry and DUI programs.
- **It includes a Second Chance Center** of about 200 beds for the last 8 to 9 weeks of a sentence, with job readiness, substance-abuse treatment, vocational training such as CDL and HVAC, and family reintegration.
- **It is in southwest Phoenix, in Maricopa County,** within the metropolitan area.

## Nearby Services

Phoenix has gas, food, and lodging throughout the metropolitan area. Several hospitals with 24-hour emergency rooms operate in the Phoenix area.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since December 2025, Arizona routes incoming personal mail to an **off-site Securus digital-mail center in Dallas, Texas**, where it is scanned and delivered to the person's **tablet**; address it to **[person's name and ADCRR number], P.O. Box 211309, Dallas, TX 75211**. **Legal mail, publications, and parcels** still go directly to the facility. Because this is a privately operated unit, confirm the current mail process with the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arizona/guides/mail/).
