## Overview

Five Points Correctional Facility, in Romulus, is a **maximum-security prison** for men in the Finger Lakes region, between Seneca and Cayuga Lakes. It was built partly on land that was formerly the Seneca Army Depot. Like all maximum-security facilities, visiting and movement are more controlled than at lower-security prisons, and some housing units have non-contact visits.

## What Makes Five Points Different

- **It is a maximum-security prison,** so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted.
- **It is a general-population facility with a Special Housing Unit (SHU).** In SHU custody, a person is allowed one non-legal visit per seven-day period.
- **It offers college and vocational programming.**
- **It is in the Finger Lakes region,** between Seneca and Cayuga Lakes.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Five Points Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days:** Visits are on Wednesdays and weekends, within the facility's posted hours; confirm the current schedule

**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)

**Special Housing Unit:** A person in SHU custody is allowed one non-legal visit per seven-day period
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Romulus, in Seneca County, in the Finger Lakes region between Geneva and Ithaca.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Romulus, Seneca County, reached from State Route 96; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Romulus area and the surrounding Finger Lakes region have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Geneva and Ithaca areas. Confirm the nearest 24-hour emergency room before traveling.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Incarcerated-individual mail uses the facility's **Caller Box 119, Romulus, NY 14541** address; address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)**. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
