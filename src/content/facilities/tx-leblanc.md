## Overview

The Richard P. LeBlanc Unit is a TDCJ pre-release facility for men, listed at about 1,224 beds. It opened in June 1995 and sits in Jefferson County south of Beaumont, in the Southeast Texas coastal plain. As a pre-release facility, it concentrates on reentry preparation, so many people housed here are within a defined window of release or transfer.

Two facts shape much of what visiting looks like here: the unit's pre-release role and its location in a city that also has two other TDCJ facilities.

## What Makes the LeBlanc Unit Different

-   A pre-release facility focused on reentry preparation, so the population turns over and an assignment can be relatively short
-   Runs a Sex Offender Treatment Program (SOTP) and a Sex Offender Education Program (SOEP), along with literacy and faith-based programming
-   One of three TDCJ facilities in Beaumont — the Mark W. Stiles Unit and the Larry Gist State Jail are also here, and the names are easy to confuse; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, and each is a different drive and a different visitation list

## Visiting

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the LeBlanc Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Custody classification is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because this is a pre-release facility, confirm the person is still assigned here before traveling. A transfer or release can move someone on short notice, and the TDCJ inmate locator reflects the current assignment.

## Getting There and Parking

The LeBlanc Unit is in Jefferson County south of Beaumont, in Southeast Texas. The approach is by highway into the Beaumont area, then a farm-to-market road to the unit.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** I-10 East toward Beaumont, then south on local and farm-to-market roads to the unit; often about 1.5-2 hours

**From Beaumont:** South from the city on local roads to FM 3514; typically a short drive

**From Lake Charles, Louisiana:** I-10 West toward Beaumont, then south to the unit; often about 50-70 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Beaumont is a full-sized city with gas, food, groceries, and lodging, so services are available before the final approach to the unit. Confirm the exact route to FM 3514 in advance, since two other TDCJ facilities in the same city sit at different addresses.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
