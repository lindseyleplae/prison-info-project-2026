:::key-info
**Location:** Lower Newton Road northwest of St. Albans in Franklin County — about 30 miles north of Burlington, the closest men's facility to the state's population center

**Population:** Around 220 men, the majority awaiting trial, with custody levels from minimum to close — plus federal immigration detainees held under a separate arrangement

**Notable:** Visiting runs alphabetically by the incarcerated person's last name, and the mailing address uses Swanton while the facility sits in St. Albans

**Visitation:** Walk-in weekend blocks; segregation visits are closed-circuit TV by appointment only
:::

## Overview

Northwest State is the men's facility for Vermont's northwest corner — and the closest one to Burlington. Its roughly 220 men skew heavily pretrial (about 140 detained at the most recent posted count), spread across eleven housing units from minimum to close custody, with Vermont Correctional Industries shops including the state's license-plate shop on site. The facility also houses federal immigration detainees under a separate federal arrangement, with their own posted visiting rules.

One addressing quirk to know: the facility is physically in St. Albans, but **mail uses a Swanton address** — same street, different town and ZIP.

## What Makes Northwest State Different

- **The alphabet sets the visiting day:** the incarcerated person's last name determines the weekend block — A-D and E-L on Saturday, M-R and S-Z on Sunday.
- **Restrictive housing visits go remote:** segregation (Delta unit) visits happen by closed-circuit TV and approved appointment only, and close-custody (Echo) visits run on weekend evenings with a two-visitor cap.
- **It's the reachable one:** about 30 miles from Burlington, with Amtrak's Vermonter terminating in St. Albans about five road miles away and a weekday commuter bus connecting Burlington and St. Albans — though no transit serves the facility itself.
- The mostly-pretrial population means fast turnover — confirming someone is still here before traveling matters.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — the 10-name list, dress code, ID, and screening — apply at Northwest State. What follows is specific to this facility.

:::key-info
**Blocks (by the incarcerated person's last name):** A-D Saturday 8:30-10:30 a.m.; E-L Saturday 1:00-3:00 p.m.; M-R Sunday 8:30-10:30 a.m.; S-Z Sunday 1:00-3:00 p.m.

**Close custody (Echo):** Unit 1 Saturday, Unit 2 Sunday, 7:00-8:30 p.m., two visitors at a time

**Segregation (Delta):** Closed-circuit TV visits by approved appointment only

**Cutoff:** Arrivals in the last 30 minutes of a period are not processed
:::

The schedule carries no posted date — call (802) 524-6771 to confirm before traveling, and to arrange a Delta appointment. Video visits run through ICSolutions at a published 16 cents per minute; the full approval and entry rules are in [Visiting in Vermont](/states/vermont/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The facility is on Lower Newton Road, about four and a half miles northwest of downtown St. Albans.

:::key-info
**From Burlington:** I-89 North to St. Albans, then Lower Newton Road — about 32 miles, roughly 50 minutes

**By train:** Amtrak's Vermonter terminates at St. Albans station, about 5 road miles from the facility

**By bus:** A weekday commuter route links Burlington and St. Albans, but nothing serves the facility itself — the last stretch is by car or taxi
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. DOC publishes no visitor parking guidance.

## Nearby Services

St. Albans covers a visit day: a Hampton Inn downtown on Lake Street, food along the US-7 corridor, and Northwestern Medical Center at 133 Fairfield Street with an emergency department open around the clock — (802) 524-5911.
