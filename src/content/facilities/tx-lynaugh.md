## Overview

The Lynaugh Unit is a general-population men's prison near Fort Stockton in Pecos County, in far West Texas. TDCJ lists it at about 1,416 beds, and it opened in 1994. The unit holds G1, G2, and G4 custody levels.

The defining fact for visiting here is distance. The unit sits in remote far West Texas, off the I-10 corridor, a long drive from the state's metropolitan areas.

## What Makes Lynaugh Unit Different

-   A general-population men's unit established in 1994, with about 1,416 beds and G1, G2, and G4 custody housing
-   Located in remote far West Texas, well off the metro grid, which makes travel distance the main practical visiting factor
-   Operations include a unit garden, security horses, and scent-specific canines
-   Programs include ABE/GED education, cognitive intervention, and vocational training in automotive, piping trades, and electrical trades, along with a Bible College, a faith-based dormitory, reentry planning, and chaplaincy
-   On-site health care includes ambulatory medical, dental, and mental-health services, with telemedicine

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Lynaugh Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visiting format follows the incarcerated person's custody classification rather than one unit-wide rule. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend.

The unit's location is the larger variable. Because Lynaugh is far from the state's population centers, a same-day round trip from most cities is long, and families often build the drive around a single weekend visit.

## Getting There and Parking

The Lynaugh Unit is in Pecos County, in far West Texas, off the I-10 corridor. Fort Stockton sits near the junction of I-10 and I-20, and the unit is about 15 miles southwest of downtown on FM 2037 (South Highway 2037).

:::key-info
**From Midland-Odessa:** Southwest toward Fort Stockton, then the FM 2037 approach south of town; often about 1.5-2 hours

**From El Paso:** East on I-10 toward Fort Stockton, then the FM 2037 approach; often about 3.5-4 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route public transit to the unit, and the final stretch is a rural farm-to-market road, so the trip is by car. Fort Stockton is the nearest town for fuel and food before the unit, and services thin out on the approach south of town.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Fort Stockton is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging before reaching the unit, and commercial options are limited the closer the route gets to the prison. Because the drive from most metro areas is long, families traveling from a distance often arrange lodging in or near Fort Stockton around a weekend visit.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
