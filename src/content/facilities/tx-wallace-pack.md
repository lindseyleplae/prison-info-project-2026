## Overview

The Wallace Pack Unit is one of TDCJ's designated geriatric and expanded-medical men's prisons, located near Navasota in Grimes County. TDCJ classifies it as a geriatric facility and lists custody levels of G1 through G3 and Outside Trusty. The unit opened in September 1983 and has a capacity of about 1,426.

Because of that mission, much of the population is older or has ongoing medical needs. TDCJ lists ambulatory medical, dental, and mental-health services available 24 hours a day, seven days a week, along with 60 wheelchair-accommodated cells and a 12-bed infirmary. Medical care is managed by the University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB) under TDCJ's correctional managed care system.

The Pack Unit shares its location with the Luther Unit. The two are separate units, so families confirm which unit a person is assigned to before traveling.

## What Makes the Wallace Pack Unit Different

- TDCJ runs the unit as a geriatric and expanded-medical facility rather than a standard general-population prison.
- Medical, dental, and mental-health services are available around the clock, and the unit has wheelchair-accommodated cells and an on-site infirmary.
- The housing areas are air conditioned. The unit was the subject of the federal heat litigation *Pack v. Livingston*, later *Cole v. Collier*, which ended in a settlement under which TDCJ installed air conditioning in the housing areas.
- Programs include the DWI Recovery Program, sex-offender treatment and education, GED and adult basic education, construction carpentry, and a faith-based dormitory; the unit also has agricultural and manufacturing operations.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Wallace Pack Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

Weekend visiting uses TDCJ's standard scheduling system, but medical and housing status can affect whether a person is available for a visit on a given day.

:::key-info
**Hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because many people at the Pack Unit are older or have medical needs, medical status can affect a scheduled visit. A person may be unavailable because of treatment, infirmary placement, or a move tied to medical needs.

## Getting There and Parking

The Wallace Pack Unit is near Navasota in Grimes County, in the area between Houston to the southeast and Bryan-College Station to the northwest. The trip is by car along the regional highway corridor before the final local-road approach to the unit.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** US-290 West toward the Navasota area, then local roads to the unit; often about 75-90 minutes

**From Bryan-College Station:** TX-6 South toward Navasota, then local roads to the unit; often about 30-45 minutes

**From Austin:** US-290 East toward the Navasota area, then local roads to the unit; often about 2-2.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in takes place at the unit's visitor processing point, where identification review and standard TDCJ screening happen before internal movement begins.
:::

Public transit to the unit is limited, and the final approach is by car.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

The Wallace Pack Unit uses Securus for phone service, and TDCJ lists Securus Video Connect where video visiting is available. Account setup and call procedures follow TDCJ's statewide phone and video system.

Mail follows TDCJ's standard routing. Communication access can shift when a person is moved for treatment or infirmary care, so contact patterns may differ from a stable general-population assignment.

## Nearby Services

Most fuel, food, pharmacies, and lodging are in Navasota or in the larger Bryan-College Station area rather than at the unit entrance. Visitors generally pass the fullest set of services in those towns before the final local-road segment to the unit.

Immediate services near the prison are limited, so families usually plan stops in Navasota or Bryan-College Station.

For broader family support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
