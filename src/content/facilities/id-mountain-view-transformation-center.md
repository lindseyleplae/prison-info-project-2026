## Overview

Mountain View Transformation Center is a state prison for men operated by the Idaho Department of Correction. It is one of the facilities in the South Boise prison complex in Kuna, Ada County, where most of Idaho's prisons sit clustered along Pleasant Valley Road. The facility's mission is treatment and programming. Specific program details are not published on the facility's page; confirm the current program and a resident's eligibility with the institution.

The center opened in 2010 as the Correctional Alternative Placement Program (CAPP), operated by the private firm Management & Training Corporation (MTC). The Idaho Department of Correction assumed operations on July 1, 2023, and the facility is now state-operated.

## Visiting

The statewide Idaho DOC rules — the approved-visitor-application process, the dress code, identification requirements, and item limits — apply at Mountain View Transformation Center. Idaho does not use a statewide online scheduler for in-person visits at its in-state prisons. A visitor mails a visiting application to the institution, and approval normally takes two to four weeks after the resident is classified and the background check clears. Confirm the facility's current visiting days and hours with the institution before traveling. See the [Visiting in Idaho](/states/idaho/guides/visiting/) guide for the full process.

## Mail

Idaho does not use an off-site mail vendor for its state prisons. Incoming personal mail goes directly to the facility, where staff inspect it for contraband before delivery. Personal mail goes to the Boise PO Box, not the Kuna physical address. Because this PO Box is shared with another facility, address mail with the resident's committed name and IDOC number and the facility name so it routes correctly:

> [Resident's full name and IDOC number]
> Mountain View Transformation Center
> PO Box 8509
> Boise, ID 83707

See the [Mail & Packages](/states/idaho/guides/mail/) guide for what may be sent, the rules on letters and photos, and how books and publications must be ordered.
