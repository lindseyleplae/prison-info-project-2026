## Overview

Wyoming Correctional Facility, in Attica, is a **medium-security prison** for men in Western New York, between Buffalo and Rochester. It is in the same town as the separate, maximum-security Attica Correctional Facility — the two are different prisons. Wyoming offers general-population programming, including education, substance-abuse treatment, and religious programs.

## What Makes Wyoming Different

- **It is a medium-security prison,** distinct from the adjacent maximum-security Attica Correctional Facility in the same town.
- **It holds men in general population,** with education, substance-abuse treatment, and religious programming.
- **It is in Western New York,** between Buffalo and Rochester.
- **It shares the town of Attica with Attica Correctional Facility,** so confirm the exact facility on the DOCCS Incarcerated Lookup before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Wyoming Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Attica, in Wyoming County, between Buffalo and Rochester.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Attica, Wyoming County, reached from State Route 98; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Attica and the surrounding Wyoming County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Batavia and Buffalo areas to the north and west. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Batavia/Buffalo area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
