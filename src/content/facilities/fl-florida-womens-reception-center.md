## Overview

The Florida Women's Reception Center is the **statewide women's reception and intake center** near Ocala in Marion County, in central Florida. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds women in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 1,228. Most women entering Florida's prison system as sentenced felons are processed here, where they are classified before being assigned to a permanent institution.

## What Makes the Florida Women's Reception Center Different

- **It is the statewide women's reception and intake center** — the female counterpart to Florida's men's reception centers, where women are received, processed, and classified after sentencing.
- **It is a separate institution from the nearby Lowell Correctional Institution,** the general-population women's prison also in Marion County. The two are distinct facilities with different addresses and wardens — confirm the exact facility on the FDC Corrections Offender Network.
- **It was named the "Lowell Reception Center" until August 2013,** when it was renamed.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** after intake, when the person is assigned to a permanent institution.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
