## Overview

The French M. Robertson Unit is a large men's TDCJ prison just north of Abilene, in Jones County in West Central Texas. TDCJ lists it at about 2,978 beds, and it sits roughly ten miles northeast of the city.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's size and its location on shared grounds with a second TDCJ facility.

## What Makes French M. Robertson Unit Different

-   A large West-Texas prison — TDCJ lists it at about 2,978 beds
-   Shares its grounds with a sister unit, the Middleton Transfer Facility; the two names are easy to confuse, and the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, which can mean a different visitation list even at the same address
-   Houses a broad G1-G5 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Reached by a farm-to-market road northeast of Abilene rather than a direct highway approach

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at French M. Robertson Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

French M. Robertson Unit follows TDCJ's standard weekend visitation, with general visiting on Saturday and Sunday. Visits are scheduled through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal, which shows the current hours and available time slots for the unit.

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because the Robertson Unit shares grounds with the Middleton Transfer Facility, confirming the exact unit on the TDCJ locator before a trip determines which visitation list and check-in process apply.

## Getting There and Parking

French M. Robertson Unit is northeast of Abilene, reached by a farm-to-market road off the main highways through the area.

:::key-info
**From Abilene:** North and northeast on local highways to FM 3522; often about 20 minutes

**From San Angelo:** North on US 277 toward Abilene, then the FM-road approach northeast of the city; often about 1.5 hours

**From Fort Worth:** I-20 West toward Abilene, then north and east to FM 3522; often about 2.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit. CityLink, the City of Abilene transit system, operates within Abilene on weekdays and does not serve the unit's rural location northeast of the city, so the trip is by car.

The nearest 24-hour emergency room is in Abilene, not near the unit. Hendrick Medical Center in Abilene operates a 24-hour emergency room.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Abilene is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, with options thinning out on the final farm-to-market approach northeast of the city. Hotel sites list chain properties in Abilene, including Hampton Inn & Suites Abilene I-20 and Holiday Inn Express & Suites Abilene.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
