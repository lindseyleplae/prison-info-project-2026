## Overview

The Gib Lewis Unit is a general-population men's prison in deep East Texas, about a mile east of Woodville off Highway 190. TDCJ lists its capacity at about 2,380 beds. The unit opened in August 1990, and its medical care is provided through the University of Texas Medical Branch (UTMB), TDCJ's statewide correctional health partner.

The unit holds a broad custody mix, from G1 through G5, so housing and visiting follow each incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule.

## What Makes Gib Lewis Unit Different

-   Houses a broad G1-G5 custody range, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Runs an Administrative Segregation Intermediate Care Program (ASICP), a mental-health care program that operates as a step-down within restrictive housing; a person in that program may have different housing and visiting procedures than general population
-   Located in the rural Piney Woods of Tyler County, reached off Highway 190 with services thinning out near the unit
-   Offers career and technical trades on site, including electrical trades, HVAC, and woodworking, alongside literacy, GED, and special education

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Gib Lewis Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

The Administrative Segregation Intermediate Care Program is part of the unit's restrictive-housing area. A person in that program may be subject to the housing and visiting procedures that apply to restrictive housing rather than to general population.

## Getting There and Parking

The Gib Lewis Unit is in a rural section of Tyler County, in the East Texas Piney Woods about a mile east of Woodville off Highway 190. The drive starts on highways and finishes on a farm-to-market approach, with services thinning out near the unit.

:::key-info
**From Beaumont:** North on US-69 toward Woodville, then west on Highway 190 to the FM 3497 approach; often about 1.5 hours

**From Houston:** North and east toward the Woodville area, then Highway 190 to the unit; often about 2-2.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Woodville is the nearest town for gas, food, and groceries before the unit, and options thin out on the final farm-to-market approach. Larger service centers sit at a distance — Lufkin to the north and Beaumont to the south — for additional lodging, food, and travel needs.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
