## Overview

SCI Dallas, on Follies Road in Dallas about 10 miles northwest of Wilkes-Barre, is a **medium-security prison for men** in northeastern Pennsylvania, holding about **2,140** people. It opened in 1960 and is one of the State Correctional Institutions operated by the Pennsylvania Department of Corrections.

## What Makes Dallas Different

- **It is medium security,** so visits may be contact or non-contact depending on the person's status — confirm which before traveling.
- **It is located in northeastern Pennsylvania,** in Luzerne County near Wilkes-Barre.
- **It opened in 1960,** and is one of the older institutions in the state system.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Dallas. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Contact or non-contact:** Confirm whether the person's visits are contact or non-contact before traveling

**Schedule:** Dallas sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Follies Road in Dallas, in Luzerne County in northeastern Pennsylvania.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 10 miles northwest of Wilkes-Barre — from Interstate 81 or the area's state routes, take local roads through the Dallas area to Follies Road

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Wilkes-Barre area has the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Wilkes-Barre area, about 10 miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
