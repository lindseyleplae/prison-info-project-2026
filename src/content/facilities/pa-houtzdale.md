## Overview

SCI Houtzdale, on Institution Drive outside the borough of Houtzdale in southern Clearfield County, is a **medium-security prison for men** in central Pennsylvania. It opened in 1996 and is one of the larger State Correctional Institutions in the state. The prison sits in the rural area between Altoona and State College.

## What Makes Houtzdale Different

- **It is medium security,** holding men in general population.
- **It opened in 1996,** in southern Clearfield County.
- **It is one of the larger state prisons,** by population.
- **It is in a rural part of central Pennsylvania,** between Altoona and State College.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Houtzdale. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** Houtzdale sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling

**Current facility:** Confirm the person is held at Houtzdale on the locator before traveling, because people are transferred between institutions
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Institution Drive outside Houtzdale, in Clearfield County in central Pennsylvania.

:::key-info
**By car:** In southern Clearfield County, between Altoona and State College — from U.S. 322 or Interstate 80, take local and state roads through the Houtzdale and Moshannon Valley area to Institution Drive

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The surrounding area is rural; the larger towns of Altoona and Clearfield have the fuller range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the **Clearfield and Altoona area**, including Penn Highlands Clearfield and UPMC Altoona.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
