## Overview

Union Correctional Institution, near Raiford in Union County, is one of Florida's **largest prisons**, a close- and maximum-custody facility for men. It holds much of the state's **male death-row population**. It sits across the area from Florida State Prison, in the same Raiford prison cluster.

## What Makes Union Different

- **It is one of Florida's largest prisons,** a close- and maximum-custody facility for men.
- **It holds much of the male death row,** so part of the population is in higher-custody housing.
- **Some housing is non-contact, and visiting rules differ by unit,** so confirm the person's unit before traveling.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred, so confirm it before traveling.

## Nearby Services

The Raiford area has limited services; the Lake Butler, Lake City, and Gainesville areas, each within driving distance, have the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Lake Butler, Lake City, and Gainesville areas.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
