## Overview

B.B. "Sixty" Rayburn Correctional Center, near Angie in Washington Parish, opened in 1983 as the **Washington Correctional Institute** and was **renamed in 2006** for Benjamin Bailey "Sixty" Rayburn, a long-serving Louisiana state senator from the area. It is a men's state prison holding a **mix of custody levels**, including general-population housing and a segregation unit; DPS&C does not publish the facility's security level or population on its page.

Rayburn's most documented programs are a long-running partnership with **Northshore Technical Community College** — offering trades such as welding, automotive technology, and building technology along with high-school-equivalency classes — and an **on-site reentry program** that works with people on housing and employment before release.

## What Makes Rayburn Different

- **The front gate closes at 2:00 p.m.,** earlier than the visiting room itself; a visitor who arrives after the gate cutoff is turned away.
- **Thursday has two visit types** — contact visits for general population in the afternoon, and non-contact visits for restricted-status people in the morning.
- **It is in the far southeastern corner of the state,** near the Mississippi line, reached from New Orleans across the Causeway.
- **Its education partnership** with Northshore Technical Community College spans decades of vocational and academic programming.

## Visiting

The statewide DPS&C rules above — the approved-list application, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Rayburn.

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Thursday 11:45 a.m. to 3:45 p.m.; Friday through Sunday 8:00 a.m. to 3:45 p.m.; the **front gate closes at 2:00 p.m.**, earlier than the room itself

**Contact vs. non-contact:** General-population visits are contact (a brief kiss at greeting and farewell, then hand-holding only); restricted-status visits are non-contact on Thursday mornings (8:00 to 11:30 a.m., up to 2 visitors, 30 minutes)

**How many and how often:** Up to 6 visitors (children included) per visit; generally twice a month, with a third visit for minimum-custody and reentry-program participants

**Screening:** Every visitor and their vehicle may be searched; picture ID is required (see the statewide rules above)
:::

Visitors may bring cash for the vending machines, and deposits to a person's account are made at the JPay kiosk in the visiting area. The full approval process is in [Visiting in Louisiana](/states/louisiana/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

Rayburn is on Highway 21 north of Angie, near Varnado and Bogalusa.

:::key-info
**From New Orleans:** About 80 miles — roughly 1 hour 45 minutes — across the Causeway to Highway 190, then onto Highway 21 north

**From Baton Rouge:** Interstate 12 east to Covington, then Highway 190 to Highway 21 north

**Transit:** No public transit serves the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. DPS&C publishes no specific visitor parking details for the facility.

## Nearby Services

Bogalusa, the nearest larger town, has the closest cluster of food, gas, and lodging. **Our Lady of the Angels Hospital** at 433 Plaza Street in Bogalusa runs a 24/7 emergency room and is described as Washington Parish's only emergency room.
