## Overview

ASPC-Eyman, on East Butte Avenue in Florence, is a **high-custody men's prison complex** — it holds **maximum and close custody** units and one of Arizona's **two men's death rows**, in its **Browning Unit** (which also serves as the intake point for men newly sentenced to death). The complex is made up of several units, and because of its security level, visiting and movement are more controlled than at lower-custody prisons. Visiting is arranged at the unit level.

## What Makes ASPC-Eyman Different

- **It holds one of Arizona's two men's death rows** (the Browning Unit).
- **It is a high-security complex** (maximum and close custody), so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted.
- **Some housing has non-contact visits,** which differ from general-population contact visits — confirm the person's unit and visit type.
- **It is in Florence,** Pinal County, in a cluster of state prisons southeast of Phoenix.

## Visiting

The statewide ADCRR rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at ASPC-Eyman. The complex's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be approved through the Application to Visit an Inmate before visiting

**Visit type:** Visiting for men in maximum-custody or death-row housing may be non-contact and differs by unit — confirm the person's unit and visit type (scheduling line (520) 868-8513)

**By unit:** Visiting days, hours, and rules are set by the specific unit — confirm them before traveling
:::

The full application process is in [Visiting in Arizona](/states/arizona/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The complex is on East Butte Avenue in Florence, southeast of Phoenix.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Florence, reached via U.S. 60 and State Route 79/287, southeast of the Phoenix metro

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the complex; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Florence has gas, food, and lodging; the Casa Grande area to the south has more. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is in the Florence–Casa Grande area (Banner Casa Grande Medical Center).

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since December 2025, Arizona routes incoming personal mail to an **off-site Securus digital-mail center in Dallas, Texas**, where it is scanned and delivered to the person's **tablet**; address it to **[person's name and ADCRR number], P.O. Box 211309, Dallas, TX 75211**. **Legal mail, publications, and parcels** still go directly to the complex. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arizona/guides/mail/).
