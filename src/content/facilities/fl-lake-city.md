## Overview

Lake City Correctional Facility is a **youthful-offender men's prison** near Lake City in Columbia County, in north Florida. It is operated by **CoreCivic** under contract with the state and monitored by the Florida Department of Management Services. The people held here are in Florida Department of Corrections custody. The facility holds young adult men, generally ages 18–24, in medium custody, with a capacity of about 893.

## What Makes Lake City Different

- **It is privately operated by CoreCivic** under contract and monitored by the Florida Department of Management Services, while the people held here remain in FDC custody.
- **It is a youthful-offender men's prison,** holding young adult men generally ages 18–24 — a distinctive mission, as most Florida men's prisons hold general adult populations.
- **It is a separate facility from Columbia Correctional Institution,** the state-run prison also in Lake City — confirm the exact facility on the FDC offender search before traveling.
- **Money is handled through CoreCivic's own commissary system** rather than the standard FDC vendor — deposits run through Western Union ("TRUST CCA") or a mailed money order; confirm the current method in [Sending Money](/states/florida/guides/money/).

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the facility**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
