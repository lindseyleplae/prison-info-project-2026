## Overview

Cape Vincent Correctional Facility, in Cape Vincent, is a **medium-security prison** for men in the North Country, near the St. Lawrence River in Jefferson County. It is in the far north of the state, a long drive from the New York City area and other downstate population centers.

## What Makes Cape Vincent Different

- **It is a medium-security prison** for men.
- **It is in the North Country,** in Jefferson County near the St. Lawrence River.
- **It is a long drive from downstate New York,** which is a real factor for visiting families.
- **Visiting days may be set by schedule,** so confirm the current days and hours before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Cape Vincent Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Cape Vincent, in Jefferson County, in the North Country near the St. Lawrence River. It is in the far north of the state, a long drive from the New York City area and other downstate population centers — visitors should plan travel time accordingly.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Cape Vincent, Jefferson County, reached from State Route 12E; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Cape Vincent and the surrounding Jefferson County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Watertown area to the southeast. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Watertown area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
