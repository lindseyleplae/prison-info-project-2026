:::key-info
**Location:** US Route 5 about two miles south of downtown St. Johnsbury, in Vermont's Northeast Kingdom

**Population:** Around 190 men — pretrial detainees and sentenced men, in the secure facility and a minimum-security work camp

**Notable:** Vermont's only facility with weekday-only visiting, in one-hour blocks assigned by housing unit

**Visitation:** Walk-in during the unit's posted hour, Monday through Friday — no weekend visits posted
:::

## Overview

The Northeast Correctional Complex is really two facilities on one site: the Northeast Regional Correctional Facility, a secure facility holding pretrial detainees and sentenced men, and the Caledonia work camp, a minimum-security dormitory whose residents have historically worked on paid community projects in the surrounding towns. The complex held about 190 men at the most recent posted count, roughly half of them awaiting trial — Vermont's unified system means this is the jail and the prison for its region.

## What Makes the Northeast Correctional Complex Different

- **Visiting is weekdays only** — the only Vermont facility without weekend visits on its posted schedule, which matters enormously for working families.
- **The blocks are one hour, by housing unit:** afternoon blocks at 2:45 and a morning block at 10:20, with the South and North units split by row across Thursdays and Fridays. The person's unit — and for the dorms, their row — determines the only hour of the week a visit can happen.
- The **work camp** side runs community crews, an arrangement DOC has described as part of the complex's public-service role.
- Like every Vermont facility, the handbook's no-contact rule applies — hands on the table, no physical contact with visitors over 11.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — the 10-name list, dress code, ID, and screening — apply here. What follows is specific to this facility.

:::key-info
**Posted blocks:** Alpha — Monday 2:45-3:45 p.m.; Bravo — Tuesday 2:45-3:45 p.m.; Charlie — Wednesday 10:20-11:20 a.m.; Delta — Wednesday 2:45-3:45 p.m.; South unit — rows 1-2 Thursday, rows 3-4 Friday, 2:45-3:45 p.m.; North unit — rows 1-2 Thursday, rows 3-4 Friday, 10:20-11:20 a.m.

**Format:** Walk-in, one visit per week, up to three visitors

**Cutoff:** Arrivals in the last 30 minutes of a period are not processed — with one-hour blocks, that means arriving in the first half hour
:::

The schedule carries no posted date and unit assignments shift, so calling (802) 748-8151 before traveling — especially from any distance — is the practical check. Video visits run through ICSolutions at a published 16 cents per minute; the full approval and entry rules are in [Visiting in Vermont](/states/vermont/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The complex sits on US Route 5 about two miles south of downtown St. Johnsbury, just off the I-91/I-93 junction that makes it the most reachable Northeast Kingdom facility from southern New England.

:::key-info
**From Burlington:** About 77 miles via I-89/US-2, roughly 1 hour 40 minutes

**From Montpelier:** About 37 miles, roughly 50 minutes

**Transit:** The regional rural transit agency runs weekday shuttles in St. Johnsbury, but no published route serves the facility's stretch of Route 5 — the trip is by car
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. DOC publishes no visitor parking details.

## Nearby Services

St. Johnsbury covers a visit day: the Fairbanks Inn on Western Avenue and a Comfort Inn & Suites on the same Route 5 South corridor as the facility, a Maplefields station on US Route 2, and the downtown restaurant cluster two miles north. Northeastern Vermont Regional Hospital, about four miles away on Hospital Drive, staffs its emergency department with physicians around the clock — (802) 748-7463.
