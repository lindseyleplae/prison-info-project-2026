## Overview

Sampson Correctional Institution is a medium- and minimum-security prison for men in Clinton, Sampson County, in southeastern North Carolina. The institution is operated by the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction (NCDAC) and houses men in medium and minimum custody as a reentry facility, with an offender capacity of about 442.

North Carolina uses a distributed reception model for men: Craven Correctional Institution in Vanceboro is a major processing center, Piedmont Correctional Institution in Salisbury receives western men 22 and older with sentences under ten years, and Central Prison in Raleigh runs a special-population diagnostic. A person's custody level is assigned during processing. Because families are not automatically notified of routine transfers, the [NCDAC offender locator](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view) is the way to confirm where someone is currently held.

## What Makes Sampson Different

Sampson Correctional Institution operates as a medium- and minimum-custody reentry facility for men. It houses the largest of the state's Correction Enterprises laundries, where incarcerated workers are assigned alongside food service, maintenance, janitorial, and recreation jobs. Educational and vocational partnerships with Sampson Community College and Campbell University offer programs that include horticulture, computer applications, laundry technology, GED/HiSET preparation, and college-level coursework. Confirm current program participation and custody status with the facility, as availability changes.

## Visiting

The statewide NCDAC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Sampson Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approval first:** Every visitor must be approved on the offender's visiting list. The incarcerated person mails an application to each prospective visitor; applicants 16 and older attach a copy of a government photo ID, and minors under 16 a copy of a birth certificate. The completed form goes to the prison where the person is housed.
- **Scheduling:** Visits are by appointment only. Call or email the prison to schedule; North Carolina has no statewide online visit scheduler. Confirm visitation status the day before.
- **Visitor limits:** Generally one visit per week, up to two hours, with up to three approved visitors. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's custody class and disciplinary status.
- **Video visits:** Video visits are available separately through the GettingOut Visits app (ViaPath).
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Sampson Correctional Institution is at 700 Northwest Blvd., US Hwy. 421 North, Clinton, NC 28328, in Sampson County in southeastern North Carolina, between Fayetteville and Wilmington. There is no public-transit service to the institution; visitors generally arrive by private vehicle. Confirm visiting days and hours and visitor parking and entrance procedures with the facility before traveling.

## Nearby Services

Clinton is in Sampson County in southeastern North Carolina, with Fayetteville to the west and Wilmington to the southeast. The closest full-service hospital emergency rooms are in the Clinton and Fayetteville areas. Lodging, fuel, and restaurants are available in Clinton and along the U.S. Highway 421 corridor.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. North Carolina sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and scans it and delivers a digital copy to the incarcerated person's tablet; original letters, cards, and packages are returned to the sender. Address personal mail with the person's full name and **OPUS number** and the full (unabbreviated) prison name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 247, Phoenix, MD 21131 — and include the sender's full first and last name in the return address. **Legal mail** is exempt and goes directly to the facility. **Books and publications** must come from a publisher or online retailer, shipped to the prison's street address, not the TextBehind box. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).
