## Overview

Southeastern Correctional Complex (SCC), in Lancaster, Fairfield County, is a **minimum- and medium-security prison for men** in central-southeast Ohio. It holds about **2,000** men and opened in **1980**.

## What Makes Southeastern Different

- **It is a minimum- and medium-security men's prison** in Lancaster, in central-southeast Ohio.
- **It opened in 1980,** one of the older institutions in this part of the state.
- **It is southeast of Columbus,** reached via U.S. 33.

## Visiting

The statewide ODRC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Southeastern Correctional Complex. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** Visits are reserved in advance through ViaPath; the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** SCC sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Lancaster, southeast of Columbus.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Lancaster, reached via U.S. 33 southeast of Columbus; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Lancaster has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Fairfield Medical Center** in Lancaster.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
