## Overview

Suwannee Correctional Institution is a large, modern **men's state prison** complex near Live Oak in Suwannee County, North Florida. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections. The complex is made up of a **main unit (opened 2009)** and the **Suwannee CI Annex (opened 2010)**, together roughly **2,851 beds**. It holds higher-custody men and functions partly as a secure-housing site.

## What Makes Suwannee Different

- **It is a large, modern complex** — a main unit (opened 2009) plus the Suwannee CI Annex (opened 2010), together roughly 2,851 beds.
- **It holds higher-custody men** and functions partly as a secure-housing site.
- **Visiting is by reservation after approval,** so confirm the person's status and institution before traveling.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred between the main unit, the Annex, or another prison.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
