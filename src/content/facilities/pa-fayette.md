## Overview

SCI Fayette, on Overlook Drive in LaBelle in southwestern Pennsylvania, is a **maximum-security prison for men**. It sits in Fayette County, south of Pittsburgh, near the Monongahela River.

## What Makes Fayette Different

- **It is maximum security,** so visits may be contact or non-contact depending on the person's status — confirm which before traveling.
- **It is in southwestern Pennsylvania,** in Fayette County south of Pittsburgh along the Monongahela River.
- **Personal mail is handled off site,** through Pennsylvania's statewide mail center in Florida rather than at the prison.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Fayette. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Contact or non-contact:** Because Fayette is maximum security, confirm whether the person's visits are contact or non-contact before traveling

**Schedule:** Fayette sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Overlook Drive in LaBelle, in Fayette County in southwestern Pennsylvania.

:::key-info
**By car:** South of Pittsburgh in southwestern Pennsylvania, along the Monongahela River — from the Pittsburgh area, take Interstate 70 and local roads through the LaBelle area to Overlook Drive

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Uniontown and greater Pittsburgh area has the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Uniontown area and, farther north, the Pittsburgh metro area.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
