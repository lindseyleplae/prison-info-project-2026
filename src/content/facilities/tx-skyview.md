## Overview

The Skyview Unit is one of TDCJ's inpatient psychiatric facilities, located in Rusk in Cherokee County, East Texas. TDCJ established the unit in 1988 and lists it with about 562 inpatient mental-health beds. The custody designation on the official unit page is "Mental Health."

TDCJ lists Skyview as co-gender, serving both men and women. The unit provides ambulatory medical, dental, and mental-health services 24 hours a day, seven days a week, along with telemedicine and specialty clinics. The official unit page notes a dedicated "Family Visitation Center."

Because Skyview is an inpatient psychiatric unit, an incarcerated person may be placed there for treatment rather than long-term housing, and a person can be moved as treatment needs change. Treatment status can also affect whether someone is available for a visit on a given day.

:::callout{variant="info"}
Skyview shares its Rusk campus with the Jerry H. Hodge Unit, TDCJ's Developmental Disabilities Program facility for men with intellectual or developmental disabilities. The two are administered together but are distinct units with separate missions. Confirm which unit a person is assigned to before traveling, and check the TDCJ inmate locator for the current location.
:::

## What Makes Skyview Unit Different

- TDCJ operates Skyview as an inpatient psychiatric and mental-health facility, not a standard general population unit.
- Placement can be tied to treatment, so some assignments are temporary and a person may be moved as care needs change.
- The unit is co-gender, serving both men and women.
- Skyview has a dedicated Family Visitation Center named on the official unit page.
- The unit shares a campus in Rusk with the separate Jerry H. Hodge Unit.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Skyview Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

Weekend visiting uses TDCJ's standard scheduling system, but psychiatric and medical operations can affect whether a person is available for a visit on a given day.

:::key-info
**Hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Treatment status, housing status, and treatment-related movement can change visit eligibility at Skyview more often than at a typical general population unit. A person may be unavailable for a scheduled visit because of treatment, psychiatric placement, or a move tied to care needs.

That same dynamic affects temporary placements. A person may be at Skyview for treatment rather than a long-term assignment, so location records can change during a stay. Confirming the assignment with the unit and checking the inmate locator before traveling accounts for that.

## Getting There and Parking

Skyview is in Rusk, in Cherokee County, in East Texas. The unit is reached by FM 2972 West, off the main routes through Rusk.

:::key-info
**From Tyler:** US-69 South toward Rusk, then local roads to FM 2972 West; often about 50-65 minutes

**From Dallas:** I-20 East to the Tyler area, then US-69 South toward Rusk; often about 2.5-3 hours

**From Houston:** US-59/I-69 North toward Nacogdoches, then US-84 and local routes west toward Rusk; often about 3-3.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in takes place at the unit's visitor processing point, where identification review and standard TDCJ screening happen before internal movement begins.
:::

Public transit to the unit is limited, and the FM 2972 approach is by car. Rusk and nearby Jacksonville are the closest towns with a fuller set of services before the final drive to the unit.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Skyview uses Securus for phone service, and TDCJ lists Securus Video Connect where video visiting is available. Communication patterns can be less predictable here than at a standard permanent-housing unit because treatment and housing status can interrupt access to calls or remote contact.

Mail routing can also be affected by treatment placement. A person who is moved for psychiatric care or further treatment may not stay at Skyview long enough for communication to follow the same pattern as a stable general population assignment.

## Nearby Services

Most fuel, food, pharmacies, and lodging options are in the Rusk and Jacksonville area rather than at the unit entrance. Visitors usually pass the fullest set of services in town before the final FM-road segment to the unit.

Immediate services near the prison are limited, so visitors traveling from outside the area generally handle fuel, food, and other stops in town first.

For broader family support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
