## Overview

Bridgeport Correctional Center, on North Avenue in Bridgeport, is a **Level 4** jail holding **both people awaiting trial and some sentenced men** — Connecticut's unified system means one facility does both. It serves the Superior Courts in **Ansonia, Derby, Milford, Bridgeport, Danbury, Norwalk, and Stamford**, the greater Bridgeport and Fairfield County region. It opened in **1958**, and also includes a small **minimum-security (Level 2) work-force unit**. Because many people here are held before trial, **being listed at Bridgeport is not by itself evidence of a conviction.**

## What Makes Bridgeport Different

- **It is both a jail and a prison,** holding people before trial and some after sentencing — so visit rules depend on a person's status and unit.
- **Visits are typically non-contact,** conducted through glass, as at Connecticut's other pretrial jails.
- **It serves the Fairfield County courts,** the southwestern corner of the state.
- **It is urban,** near Route 8/25 off Interstate 95.

## Visiting

The statewide Connecticut DOC rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Bridgeport.

:::key-info
**Contact vs. non-contact:** Visits are typically non-contact (through a glass partition) — confirm the visit type for the person's housing unit

**In-person:** Assigned by housing unit; the facility's official page currently states in-person visits must be pre-scheduled and confirmed in advance (one per week) — confirm the current process before traveling; up to 2 visitors per visit

**Video:** Free video visits are scheduled through the DOC request form; see the phone and video guide

**Confirm first:** The day-and-time grid is set by housing unit and has changed since the pandemic — confirm the current schedule before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Connecticut](/states/connecticut/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

Bridgeport Correctional Center is on North Avenue (at Madison Avenue), reached from Route 8/25 off Interstate 95.

:::key-info
**By car:** From I-95, take Exit 27A to Route 8/25 north, then Exit 4 (Lindley Street) to North Avenue; the facility is about two miles north of downtown

**By transit:** Metro-North trains and Amtrak serve Bridgeport's downtown station; from there a local bus or rideshare reaches the facility — confirm the route and parking before arriving
:::

## Nearby Services

Bridgeport has a full range of food, gas, and lodging. The nearest 24/7 emergency rooms are **St. Vincent's Medical Center** (a Level II trauma center on Main Street) and **Bridgeport Hospital**, both in Bridgeport.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's name and **inmate number**; Connecticut delivers the physical letter rather than scanning it off-site. Legal mail marked as privileged is opened in the person's presence and not read. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/connecticut/guides/mail/).
