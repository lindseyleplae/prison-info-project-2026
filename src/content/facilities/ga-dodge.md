## Overview

Dodge State Prison, on Old Bethel Church Road in Chester in Dodge County, is a **medium-security state prison for men** in central Georgia. It houses general-population men and has a rated capacity of about **1,236**. The facility runs work and rehabilitation programs, including academic classes (GED and adult basic education), vocational training, and counseling.

## What Makes Dodge Different

- **It is a general-population medium-security men's prison,** holding men assigned a medium security level after classification.
- **It runs work and rehabilitation programs,** including academic, vocational, and counseling offerings.
- **It is in central Georgia, near Eastman,** in a rural part of Dodge County.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Dodge State Prison. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays — confirm the current days and hours with the facility

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Old Bethel Church Road in Chester, in Dodge County, central Georgia near Eastman.

:::key-info
**By car:** Chester is a small community in Dodge County, just north of Eastman; the facility is reached by local roads off the U.S. 23 / U.S. 341 corridor

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Eastman, just south of Chester, has the closest gas, food, and lodging; Dublin, about 30 miles northeast, has a wider range. The nearest hospital with a 24-hour emergency room is in the Eastman area, with larger hospitals in Dublin.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
