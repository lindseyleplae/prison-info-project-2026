## Overview

In TDCJ, a "state jail" is a prison that holds people serving state-jail-felony terms — the lowest level of Texas felony, served day-for-day rather than under the parole system that applies to prison-custody sentences. State jails run under the same TDCJ visitation rules as the agency's other units.

The Hutchins State Jail is one of TDCJ's large urban state jails. TDCJ lists it at about 2,276 beds. It sits in Dallas County in the town of Hutchins, south of downtown Dallas at the intersection of Interstate 45 and Interstate 20.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's mixed population and its metro-Dallas location.

## What Makes Hutchins State Jail Different

-   One of TDCJ's large urban state jails — TDCJ lists it at about 2,276 beds
-   Holds state-jail-felony (J-custody) men alongside some prison-custody (G1, G2) and transient/transfer populations, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Serves as the Regional Release Site for the Dallas area, which means some people are held here on a short-term or transitional basis; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to
-   Sits at the I-45/I-20 interchange in metro Dallas, making it one of the easier TDCJ units to reach for North Texas families

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Hutchins. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's mixed custody is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. State-jail and general-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because Hutchins also functions as a regional release and transfer point, a person's assignment can change on short notice. Confirming the current unit on the TDCJ inmate locator before traveling avoids a trip to the wrong facility.

## Getting There and Parking

Hutchins is an urban unit in Dallas County, in the town of Hutchins south of downtown Dallas. Unlike TDCJ's rural units, it sits directly at a major interstate interchange.

:::key-info
**From central Dallas:** I-45 South to the I-45/I-20 interchange near Hutchins; often about 20-30 minutes

**From Fort Worth:** I-20 East toward the I-45/I-20 interchange near Hutchins; often about 45-60 minutes

**From Tyler:** I-20 West toward the Dallas-area interchange near Hutchins; often about 1.5-2 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Hutchins sits inside the Dallas-Fort Worth metro, so gas, food, groceries, and lodging are widely available near the unit and along the I-45 and I-20 corridors, unlike at TDCJ's rural units. Dallas is the service center for families traveling from outside the area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
