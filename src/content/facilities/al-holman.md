## Overview

William C. Holman Correctional Facility, on Highway 21 north of Atmore in Escambia County, is a men's prison that serves a narrow, specialized role. It is **Alabama's primary death-row facility for men** and holds the **state's only execution chamber**. In January 2020, ADOC closed Holman's main general-population unit after its underground power, water, and sewer systems failed, transferring more than 400 men to other prisons; the death-row housing and the execution chamber remained in operation. The facility now holds a small population — about **213 men** as of April 2026. It runs Alabama's prison **license-plate (tag) plant** and a sewing operation that produces linens for state prisons.

## What Makes Holman Different

- **It is Alabama's primary death-row facility for men** and holds the state's only execution chamber.
- **Its main general-population unit closed in January 2020** after utility failures, so it now holds a small, specialized population.
- **It operates Alabama's prison license-plate (tag) plant** and a sewing operation.
- **Because of its death-row mission, visiting and housing rules differ** from a general-population prison — confirm the person's status and visit format.

## Visiting

The statewide ADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the no-tan dress code, ID, and the $30 vending limit — apply at Holman. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Schedule:** ADOC does not post Holman's visiting days or hours online; under the 2025 visitation rules (AR 303), all visits are pre-scheduled and approved

**Death row / restrictive housing:** Visits for men on death row or in restrictive housing may be non-contact or otherwise limited — confirm the format for the specific person

**How to confirm:** Call the facility at (251) 368-8173 for the current days, hours, and process

**List:** The person must add the visitor to their approved list first (up to eight approved adults)
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Alabama](/states/alabama/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Highway 21 north of Atmore in Escambia County, in the Interstate 65 corridor between Mobile and Montgomery.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 10 miles north of Atmore on Highway 21; roughly 50 miles northeast of Mobile via I-65 (exit 57) and about 140 miles southwest of Montgomery

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Atmore, about 10 miles south, has the nearest gas, food, and lodging; Mobile, about 50 miles southwest, has the fullest range. The nearest hospital emergency room is **Atmore Community Hospital** in Atmore; higher-level trauma care is in Mobile or in Pensacola, Florida.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since late 2025, ADOC routes personal letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site scanning center in San Antonio, Texas**, addressed with the person's name and **AIS number**; the person receives the mail as **images on a tablet**. **Legal mail and packages** still go to the facility. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/alabama/guides/mail/).
