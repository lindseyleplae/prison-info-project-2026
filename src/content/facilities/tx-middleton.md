## Overview

The John Middleton Unit is a TDCJ men's unit near Abilene, in Jones County, established in 1994. TDCJ lists its capacity at about 2,128. The agency officially classifies it as a Correctional Institutions Division prison, but it functions as an intake and transfer point: it holds people in "Transient" custody and houses the Western Region Offender Transportation Office.

Because of that role, a person assigned here may be in the intake or transfer stage rather than at a permanent placement. The TDCJ inmate locator shows the current unit assignment, which is the reliable check before traveling.

## What Makes the Middleton Unit Different

-   It serves an intake and transfer role, so a person's assignment here may be temporary, ending with reassignment to a permanent unit
-   It houses the Western Region Offender Transportation Office, which makes it a transportation hub for the region
-   It conducts substance-abuse screening
-   Programs include Adult Basic Education and GED, the CHANGES pre-release program, and cognitive intervention
-   Agricultural operations include security horses, security pack canines, and a unit garden

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the John Middleton Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visits are scheduled through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal. Because the unit serves an intake and transfer role, a person may be reassigned to another unit on short notice, and the timing of a first visit is less predictable than at a permanent unit. The TDCJ inmate locator shows the current unit assignment, which is the reliable check before traveling.

## Getting There and Parking

The John Middleton Unit is about eight miles northeast of Abilene, in Jones County, in West Texas. Abilene is the nearest service center and the usual approach point, reached from the Fort Worth area along Interstate 20.

:::key-info
**From the Abilene area:** Local routing northeast of the city toward the unit on FM 3522

**From Fort Worth:** Interstate 20 west toward Abilene; often about 2.5-3 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and during and shortly after intake that approval — along with mail and visitation setup — can take time to process while a person is being received and classified. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Abilene has full services, including gas, food, groceries, hotels, and other lodging for families traveling a longer distance.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
