## Overview

Auburn Correctional Facility, in Auburn, is a **maximum-security prison** for men in the Finger Lakes region of central New York. It is one of the oldest prisons in the state, built in the late 1810s. Auburn is the origin of the **"Auburn system"** of congregate, silent labor — a model of prison organization that spread to other states in the 19th century. Auburn is also one of New York's **men's reception and classification centers**, so it receives and processes some men entering the state prison system. Like all maximum-security facilities, visiting and movement are more controlled than at lower-security prisons.

## What Makes Auburn Different

- **It is a maximum-security prison,** so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted.
- **It is one of New York's men's reception and classification centers,** so newly received men have limited visiting until classification is complete.
- **It is one of the oldest prisons in the state** and the origin of the historic "Auburn system" of congregate, silent labor.
- **It has a Special Housing Unit (SHU).** A person in SHU custody is allowed one non-legal visit per seven-day period.
- **It is in the Finger Lakes region,** between Syracuse and the Finger Lakes.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Auburn Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Days:** Visiting is on Wednesdays and weekends within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Reception and SHU:** A newly received person has limited visiting until classification, and a person in SHU custody is limited to one non-legal visit per seven-day period

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Nearby Services

Auburn and the surrounding Cayuga County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Syracuse area to the east. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in Auburn and the Syracuse area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
