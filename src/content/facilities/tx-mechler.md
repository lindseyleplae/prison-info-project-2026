## Overview

The Mechler Unit is a small TDCJ prison — TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds — near Tulia in Swisher County, in the Texas Panhandle. It opened in June 1992 and later came under TDCJ operation after an earlier role in the state's youth-corrections system, a lineage it shares with the nearby San Saba Unit.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's small size and its remote Panhandle location.

## What Makes Mechler Unit Different

-   A small unit — TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds — holding general-population men at G1-G2 custody
-   Programming centers on literacy, cognitive-intervention, and the CHANGES reentry curriculum
-   Medical care is delivered partly through Texas Tech telemedicine, reflecting the unit's distance from major hospitals
-   Located in the rural Panhandle off State Highway 86, a long drive from the state's large metro areas

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Mechler. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Mechler holds general-population men at G1-G2 custody, so most visits use standard contact visiting. The format still follows the incarcerated person's classification, so housing or status changes can change how a visit is conducted on a given weekend.

The unit's small size works in a family's favor on movement time: with about 606 beds, the walk from housing to the visiting area is shorter than at a large unit, so the wait between check-in and the start of a visit tends to be brief.

## Getting There and Parking

Mechler is in a rural section of Swisher County, near Tulia in the Texas Panhandle. The unit sits off State Highway 86 west of town, and services thin out quickly outside Tulia.

:::key-info
**From Amarillo:** I-27 South toward Tulia, then State Highway 86 west to the unit; often about 60-75 minutes

**From Lubbock:** I-27 North toward Tulia, then State Highway 86 west to the unit; often about 60-75 minutes

**From Plainview:** I-27 North toward Tulia, then State Highway 86 west to the unit; often about 30-40 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, and the rural Panhandle setting means the trip is by car. Tulia is the nearest town for gas and food before the final approach.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Tulia is a small town with limited commercial services. Amarillo to the north and Lubbock to the south are the nearest larger service centers for groceries, lodging, and a wider range of dining, each roughly an hour from the unit. Closer in, Tulia offers basic gas and food before the final stretch on State Highway 86.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
