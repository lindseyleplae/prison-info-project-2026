## Overview

Taycheedah Correctional Institution (TCI) is the Wisconsin Department of Corrections facility that houses women across maximum and medium custody levels. It is the state's women's prison and also serves as the statewide reception and intake center for women, where most newly sentenced women enter the WI DOC system. The institution is operated by the department's Division of Adult Institutions in Fond du Lac, in east-central Wisconsin.

Security level is assigned during the intake and classification process. Because Taycheedah is the women's reception center, the population includes both women in the intake ("A&E") phase and women in general population. Wisconsin does not have a death penalty — the state abolished it in 1853 — so there is no death row or execution chamber at any Wisconsin prison.

## What Makes Taycheedah Different

Taycheedah is the statewide women's prison and the women's reception/intake center in one facility. Women entering WI DOC custody are typically received and classified here before being assigned a security level and housing. The institution holds women at maximum and medium custody, and its programming is gender-responsive — designed for the women in its custody rather than adapted from a men's facility.

Newly admitted women begin in intake ("A&E") status, during which visiting and other arrangements are more limited; general-population status follows after classification. Because the same facility serves both reception and longer-term confinement, a woman's status and housing unit can change after intake. Re-check the WI DOC offender locator for the current facility and status.

## Visiting

The statewide WI DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Taycheedah Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** A visitor must be on the woman's approved visiting list before any visit. The woman mails a DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire to the prospective visitor, who completes it and returns it to the institution; the woman is notified when a visitor is approved.
- **Visitor limits:** Women in intake ("A&E") status may have up to 4 adult close-family visitors; women in general population may have up to 12 adults (minors do not count toward the cap until age 18).
- **Scheduling:** Wisconsin has no statewide online visit scheduler. In-person visits are arranged with Taycheedah directly — call (920) 929-3800 to confirm the current visiting days, hours, and any scheduling steps. Video visits are scheduled separately through ICSolutions.
- **Restrictive housing:** Visits for women in restrictive housing are often conducted by audio-visual equipment rather than in person.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Taycheedah is at 751 County Road K in Fond du Lac, in Fond du Lac County in east-central Wisconsin, near the south end of Lake Winnebago. There is no public transit to the institution; access is by private vehicle. Confirm visitor parking and entrance procedures with the facility, and arrive no more than 15 minutes before the scheduled visit.

## Nearby Services

Fond du Lac has hotels, restaurants, fuel, and pharmacies. The nearest hospital emergency room is in Fond du Lac, with additional emergency care in the Oshkosh area to the north.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. Wisconsin sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and photocopies it and forwards copies to the institution; people with tablets may also receive electronic photos. Address personal mail with the person's full name, **DOC number**, and the full (unabbreviated) facility name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 189, Phoenix, MD 21131. **Legal and privileged mail** is exempt and goes directly to the institution at its own legal-mail address. **Books and other publications** must come from an approved vendor, shipped directly from the retailer, not from individuals. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/wisconsin/guides/mail/).
