## Overview

Sumter Correctional Institution is a **general-population men's prison** near Bushnell in Sumter County, in central Florida, in the I-75 corridor between Ocala and Tampa. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds adult men and youthful offenders (age 18) in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 1,551.

## What Makes Sumter Different

- **It is home to the FDC's Basic Training Unit (BTU) for youthful offenders** — the state's only such program, a roughly 120-day regimented program. The BTU has its own programs and visiting handling, so families of people in that unit should confirm its arrangements.
- **It holds adult men and youthful offenders (age 18)** in minimum, medium, and close custody.
- **It offers academic and vocational programs,** including building maintenance, marketing and management, Braille, and the IN2WORK program, along with a faith-and-character dorm and reentry programming.
- **It has an on-grounds Sumter Work Camp,** so families should confirm which unit the person is housed in before traveling.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
