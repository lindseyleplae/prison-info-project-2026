## Overview

Chain O'Lakes Correctional Facility, in Albion, is a **minimum-security prison for men** in northeastern Indiana, near Fort Wayne. IDOC does not publish a current population count for the facility, which is described here by security level rather than a number.

## What Makes Chain O'Lakes Different

- **It is a minimum-security facility,** which differs from the close- and maximum-security prisons elsewhere in the Indiana system.
- **It is in Albion, in northeastern Indiana,** near Fort Wayne.

## Visiting

The statewide IDOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Chain O'Lakes Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Registration:** Visitors must register and be approved on the person's visitor list through ViaPath before scheduling

**Scheduling:** Each visit is scheduled at idoc.gtlvisitme.com, using Eastern time

**Schedule:** Confirm the facility's current visiting days and hours before traveling
:::

The full registration process is in [Visiting in Indiana](/states/indiana/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Albion, in Noble County, in northeastern Indiana.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Albion, in Noble County, northeastern Indiana; Fort Wayne is the nearest larger city, to the southeast

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Albion and the nearby Kendallville and Fort Wayne areas have the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Kendallville and Fort Wayne areas.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail goes to the prison, but the incarcerated person receives a **photocopy**, not the original. Indiana does not use an off-site mail vendor — each prison's mailroom opens and **photocopies** incoming letters and photos in black and white and delivers the copy. Address mail with the person's full name and IDOC number to the facility's mailing address (above). **Legal mail** is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must be new and shipped directly from a publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/indiana/guides/mail/).
