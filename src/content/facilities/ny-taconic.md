## Overview

Taconic Correctional Facility, on Harris Road in Bedford Hills, Westchester County, in the lower Hudson Valley north of New York City, is a **medium-security state prison for women**. It is **a separate facility from the adjacent Bedford Hills Correctional Facility**, the maximum-security women's prison and women's reception and classification center, which is also in Bedford Hills. Taconic holds a medium-security women's general population with programming that includes substance-abuse treatment.

## What Makes Taconic Different

- **It is a medium-security women's prison,** so visiting and movement are less restricted than at a maximum-security facility.
- **It is a separate facility from Bedford Hills Correctional Facility,** the adjacent maximum-security women's prison and women's reception center — both are women's prisons in the same town.
- **It is not a reception center;** women's reception and classification is at Bedford Hills.
- **It is in Bedford Hills, Westchester County,** in the lower Hudson Valley north of New York City.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Taconic Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Confirm the facility:** Taconic and the adjacent Bedford Hills Correctional Facility are separate prisons — confirm which one before traveling
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Harris Road in Bedford Hills, in northern Westchester County, about 40 miles north of New York City.

:::key-info

**By transit:** The Metro-North Harlem Line stops at Bedford Hills; a private vehicle is also an option

## Nearby Services

Bedford Hills and the surrounding Westchester County area have the full range of gas, food, and lodging, with more throughout the lower Hudson Valley. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Westchester County area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
