## Overview

York Correctional Institution, in Niantic, is **Connecticut's only prison for women** and the **women's reception center** — every woman in the state system, whether held before trial or serving a sentence and at any security level, is held here. It holds about **850 women**. The current facility opened in 1994 on a site that has housed Connecticut's women's prison since 1918, and it is named for a longtime superintendent. York runs an enhanced **parent-child visitation program** (the Caregiver and Me program, operated with the nonprofit A Sacred Place).

## What Makes York Different

- **It is the only women's facility in Connecticut.** Families from across the state travel to this one location to visit an incarcerated woman.
- **It is also the women's reception center,** so a newly admitted woman — pretrial or sentenced — enters the system here.
- **It runs a parent-child visitation program.** The Caregiver and Me program provides extended, child-friendly visits between incarcerated mothers, their children, and caregivers.
- **The weekend visiting day depends on the inmate number** — Saturdays for even numbers, Sundays for odd — a facility-specific rule to check before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide Connecticut DOC rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at York. The facility's own schedule:

:::key-info
**How often:** Up to two weekday visits and one weekend visit a week (each may be in person or video)

**Weekday hours:** Morning, afternoon, and evening blocks Monday through Friday

**Weekend:** Saturdays are for **even** inmate numbers, Sundays for **odd**

**Scheduling:** In-person visits are walk-in (not pre-scheduled); video visits are scheduled through the DOC's online request form. Up to three visitors at a time
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Connecticut](/states/connecticut/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on West Main Street in Niantic, in southeastern Connecticut.

:::key-info
**By car:** Off Interstate 95 at Exit 72, then Route 156 — about 10 minutes from New London, and roughly 50 minutes from Hartford or New Haven

**By transit:** There is no transit stop at the facility; the nearest rail is in New London, and a car completes the trip
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Niantic and New London have gas, food, and lodging, with more in New London about 10 minutes away. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **Lawrence + Memorial Hospital** at 365 Montauk Avenue in New London.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the woman's name and **inmate number**; Connecticut delivers the physical letter rather than scanning it off-site. Legal mail marked as privileged is opened in her presence and not read. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/connecticut/guides/mail/).
