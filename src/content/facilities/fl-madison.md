## Overview

Madison Correctional Institution is a **general-population men's prison** near the town of Madison in Madison County, in north Florida's Big Bend region. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections and holds men in close, medium, minimum, and community custody, with a capacity of roughly 1,050 to 1,190.

## What Makes Madison Different

- **It is a general-population men's institution** off the I-10 corridor in north Florida, west of Live Oak.
- **It hosts a PRIDE (Prison Rehabilitative Industries and Diversified Enterprises) operation** — a textile and mattress factory and a shoe factory where people in custody work.
- **It has an affiliated Madison Work Camp,** a minimum-security satellite unit; the person may be housed at the main institution or the work camp, so confirm the placement on the offender search.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
