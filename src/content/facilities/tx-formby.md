## Overview

The Formby State Jail is a men's TDCJ state jail near Plainview, in the Texas Panhandle/South Plains region of West Texas. TDCJ lists it at about 1,100 beds. It opened in September 1995 and sits about five miles east of Plainview in Hale County.

Two facts shape most of what families need to plan for: the unit's state-jail and transfer roles, which set the visiting format, and its Panhandle location, which sets the drive.

## What Makes Formby Different

-   A state-jail facility holding men in state-jail custody (J1-J5) along with general-population and closer-custody housing (G2, G4) and people held briefly pending transfer, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Located in the Texas Panhandle/South Plains, a long drive from most of the state's population centers; under TDCJ rules, visitors traveling more than 300 miles one way may request a longer special visit
-   Shares its grounds with the separate Wheeler Unit (a transfer facility, not the same as Formby); the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to
-   Runs a scent-specific canine program, a faith-based dormitory, vocational training, and Habitat for Humanity community projects; medical care is managed by Texas Tech (the West Texas region) with telemedicine

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Formby State Jail. Visitation is limited to people on the incarcerated person's approved Visitors List (everyone 18 and older except the attorney of record), and visits are scheduled through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal. A regular visit is generally up to two hours, with one visit per visiting cycle (Monday through Sunday). Visitors traveling more than 300 miles one way may request a special visit of up to four hours.

TDCJ does not publish unit-specific visiting hours for Formby on its unit page. Current days and hours are confirmed through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal and the [Visiting in Texas](/states/texas/guides/visiting/) guide rather than assumed from a posted schedule.

Formby's custody mix is the main reason one schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

Formby is in Hale County, about five miles east of Plainview in the Texas Panhandle, on the interstate corridor between Lubbock to the south and Amarillo to the north. The trip is by private vehicle.

:::key-info
**From Lubbock:** North on I-27 toward Plainview, then the local approach east of town; often about 50-60 minutes

**From Amarillo:** South on I-27 toward Plainview, then the local approach east of town; often about 1.5-2 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Because the unit is in the Panhandle/South Plains, many families travel a long distance to visit. Visitors coming more than 300 miles one way may request a longer special visit under TDCJ's rules; that request is made in advance, not at the gate.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Plainview is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, with more extensive options in Lubbock about an hour to the south. Travelers planning an overnight trip generally find the widest selection of chain lodging and restaurants in the Lubbock area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
