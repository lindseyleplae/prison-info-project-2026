## Overview

The Ouachita River Correctional Unit, in Malvern, is the **central intake center for men** entering the Arkansas system — newly sentenced men are received, assessed, and classified here before being assigned to a long-term unit. It is also the ADC's **medical hub**: it operates a **Special Needs Unit** (opened in 2012) that provides hospital-level beds and sheltered living for **elderly, chronically ill, and acutely ill** prisoners. ADC lists a rated capacity of about **1,782** and does not publish a current count. ADC's own page does not assign a single custody level; the facility holds men across custody levels.

## What Makes Ouachita River Different

- **It is the men's reception and classification center,** so most newly sentenced men in Arkansas pass through here first.
- **It is the system's medical hub,** with a special-needs unit for chronically and seriously ill prisoners and a hospital area.
- **Visiting during reception may be limited,** because newly received men are still being processed.
- **It is off Interstate 30 near Malvern,** more central than Arkansas's Delta prisons.

## Visiting

The statewide ADC rules above — the approved visitor list, the no-white dress code, ID, and pre-scheduled visits — apply at Ouachita River. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days:** Saturdays and Sundays for routine visits; how many visits a month depends on the inmate's class

**Reception:** A newly received man in intake may have limited visiting at first — confirm with the unit

**Scheduling:** Request a visit through the TeleGov online portal, or call the unit's Visitation Clerk at (501) 467-3400

**List:** The visitor must be approved on the inmate's list first (the inmate mails out the application form)
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Arkansas](/states/arkansas/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The unit is on Walco Lane, off U.S. Highway 67 south of Malvern, reached from Interstate 30.

:::key-info
**By car:** From Interstate 30, take the Malvern exit (Exit 98A) and follow local roads; the unit is about 2 miles south of downtown Malvern. Little Rock is about 45–50 miles northeast; Hot Springs is about 20 miles away

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the unit; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Malvern, about 2 miles away, has the nearest restaurants and lodging, with more in Hot Springs. The nearest hospital emergency room is **Baptist Health Medical Center–Hot Spring County** in Malvern, the closest 24-hour ER to the unit.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the unit. Since September 2025, ADC routes non-legal personal letters and photos to an **off-site scanning center in Tampa, Florida**, addressed with the person's name and **ADC number**; the person receives the mail electronically. **Legal mail, books, and magazines** still go directly to the unit at the Walco Lane address. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arkansas/guides/mail/).

## A Note on History

The unit opened in **2003**, and its special-needs medical unit was added in 2012. In **2025**, the Arkansas Division of Correction agreed to a **voluntary settlement with the U.S. Department of Justice** under the Americans with Disabilities Act, addressing accessible cells, showers, and medical and mobility services for prisoners with disabilities at the Ouachita River Unit. This is documented in the sources cited above.
