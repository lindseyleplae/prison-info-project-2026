**Location:** 16 Howard Avenue — the first facility on the left entering the ACI campus from Pontiac Avenue

**Population:** About 180 minimum-custody men in a 710-bed converted hospital complex, every one of them employed or job-seeking

**Notable:** The only ACI facility where visitors may bring food — at outside visits, weather permitting

**Visitation:** Weekday evenings only, alternating between B and D dorms — no Wednesdays, and no weekend sessions on the posted schedule
:::

## Overview

Minimum Security is the ACI's work-release and employment facility, operating since 1978 in a converted hospital building behind a low fence. Its defining rule is work: everyone there, unless medically excused, is employed inside the institution, on public-service crews, on work release in the community, or actively seeking a job. Recent programming includes a welding course run with the New England Institute of Technology and state job coaches who come to the facility.

## What Makes Minimum Security Different

- **Visiting is evenings only, on weekdays** — Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday, in two 90-minute sessions alternating between B and D dorms. There are no Wednesday or weekend visits on the posted schedule, the reverse of most facilities' patterns.
- **Food is allowed** — uniquely on this campus, visitors may bring food to outside visits, weather permitting, with drinks only from the vending machines.
- **Work-release visits run during every period**, not just the dorm rotation.
- The work requirement means the person's schedule matters too — daytime jobs are why visiting lives in the evening.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — the 9-name visiting list, the dress code, ID, and screening — apply at Minimum. What follows is specific to this facility.

:::key-info
**Sessions:** 5:15-6:45 p.m. and 7:15-8:45 p.m., Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday, alternating B Dorm and D Dorm

**Dark days:** No visits Wednesdays; weekday holidays are posted separately

**Food:** Allowed at outside visits only, weather permitting — no outside drinks

**Video:** Securus video visits, booked at least 24 hours ahead
:::

The monthly schedule PDF on RIDOC's facility page matches dorms to dates, and RIDOC's advice is to verify before each visit — the 24/7 information line at (401) 414-2871 covers visiting times. The full approval-list process, dress code, and entry rules are in [Visiting in Rhode Island](/states/rhode-island/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

RIDOC's published directions: I-95 to Exit 14 (14B northbound), Route 37 West to Exit 3 (Pontiac Avenue), left at the end of the exit, then right onto Howard Avenue at the third light — Minimum Security is on the left entering the campus. From the west, I-295 to Route 37 East, exiting at Oaklawn Avenue (Route 2 South), left at the first light onto Howard Avenue.

:::key-info
**From Providence:** About 8 miles, 15 minutes, via I-95 South to Route 37

**By bus:** RIPTA Routes 21 and 22 serve the campus, both from Kennedy Plaza in downtown Providence with a Pastore Center stop — Minimum is among the closest facilities to the bus corridor

**Trip planning:** RIDOC directs riders to the RIPTA Trip Planner with destination "40 Howard Ave., Cranston"
:::

RIDOC publishes no visitor parking details for the facility.

## Nearby Services

The Garden City Center shopping area is minutes away — on the same RIPTA Route 21 that serves the campus — with food at the Chapel View development and gas on Pontiac and Oaklawn Avenues. Lodging clusters near T.F. Green Airport in Warwick, including a Crowne Plaza with free parking. Kent Hospital in Warwick runs an emergency department staffed around the clock; Rhode Island Hospital in Providence is the state's only Level I trauma center.
