## Overview

SCI Camp Hill, on Lisburn Road in Camp Hill across the river from Harrisburg, is **Pennsylvania's diagnostic and classification center for men** — the statewide intake point where all newly committed men are received, evaluated (medical, mental health, security level, and programming), and then assigned and transferred to a permanent State Correctional Institution. Because of that role, a man may be at Camp Hill only briefly, and the prison shown in the state locator can change within the first weeks or months. It is a **close-security** facility that also houses a general-population men's population, holding about **2,276 men** (capacity about 3,028). It opened in 1941.

## What Makes Camp Hill Different

- **It is the men's intake and classification center,** so a newly committed man usually begins here before transferring to a permanent prison.
- **Visiting is limited during the reception period,** so confirm the person's status and location before traveling.
- **It is in the Harrisburg area,** off Interstate 83 — more central than many Pennsylvania prisons.
- **Classification can take weeks to months,** after which the person is transferred.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Camp Hill. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Reception status:** A person in reception has limited visiting and will transfer to a permanent prison after classification

**Schedule:** Camp Hill sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Lisburn Road in Camp Hill, in the Harrisburg metro area.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 4 miles from downtown Harrisburg — cross the Susquehanna on Interstate 83 or U.S. 15 into the Camp Hill area, then local roads to Lisburn Road

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Camp Hill and the Harrisburg area have the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Penn State Health Holy Spirit Medical Center** in Camp Hill; UPMC Harrisburg, across the river, is another 24-hour option.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
