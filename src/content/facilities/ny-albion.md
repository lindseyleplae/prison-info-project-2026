## Overview

Albion Correctional Facility, in Albion (Orleans County) in western New York between Rochester and Buffalo, is a **medium-security prison for women**. It is one of New York's principal women's prisons; it opened in the 1890s as the Western House of Refuge for Women. The facility has a **Special Housing Unit (SHU)** and comprehensive programming, including substance-abuse treatment, education and vocational programs, and trauma-informed services. It is **not a reception center** — women's reception and classification is handled at Bedford Hills.

## What Makes Albion Different

- **It is a medium-security prison for women,** not a maximum-security facility and not a men's prison.
- **Visiting is weekends only and assigned by last name** on a posted alphabetical schedule, so the eligible day depends on the person's surname.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Albion Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Days:** Weekends only; the visiting day is assigned by the first letter of the incarcerated person's last name on a posted alphabetical schedule

**Group size:** A maximum of 3 visitors plus 1 child under age 5
:::

Because the eligible day depends on the surname, confirm the current letter-day on the DOCCS Albion page before traveling. The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
