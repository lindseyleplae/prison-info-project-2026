## Overview

Lakeview Shock Incarceration Correctional Facility, in Brocton, runs New York's **Shock Incarceration program** for both men and women. Shock Incarceration is a **roughly six-month intensive program** — a military-style regimen of physical training, work, education, and substance-abuse counseling for eligible people — and completing it **can shorten the time a person serves**. Because the program is structured in phases, visiting is more limited than at a standard prison and is tied to the participant's phase in the program.

## What Makes Lakeview Different

- **It is a Shock Incarceration facility,** running a roughly six-month military-style program rather than holding people for a standard sentence.
- **It holds both men and women,** unlike most New York facilities.
- **Completing the program can shorten the time served.**
- **Participant visiting is limited and program-dependent,** with the profile noting about one visit every two weeks.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Lakeview Shock Incarceration Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Participant visiting:** As a Shock Incarceration program, visiting is limited and tied to the program phase — the profile notes about one visit every two weeks; confirm the current schedule with the facility

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Nearby Services

Brocton and the surrounding Chautauqua County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Dunkirk and Fredonia areas to the north. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Dunkirk/Fredonia area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
