## Overview

The Reynaldo V. Lopez State Jail is a men's TDCJ facility in Edinburg, in Hidalgo County. It opened in November 1997 and sits about 10 miles north of Edinburg off Highway 281, in the Rio Grande Valley of far south Texas. TDCJ lists its capacity at about 903 beds.

The unit holds a mix of populations — state-jail felons, general-population housing, and people held briefly pending transfer — so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule.

## What Makes the Lopez Unit Different

-   A men's state jail that combines state-jail, general-population, and transfer roles on one campus, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than a single unit-wide rule
-   Located in the Rio Grande Valley of far south Texas, near the Mexico border and a long drive from most of the state; families traveling more than 300 miles may request a special visit of up to four hours under TDCJ's statewide rules
-   Shares its grounds with the separate Segovia Unit, a different facility on the same site, so the TDCJ inmate locator should be checked for the exact unit a person is assigned to
-   Programs include faith-based dormitory housing, the Texas Second Chance program, and vocational training in construction carpentry, culinary arts, and electrical trades

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Reynaldo V. Lopez State Jail. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visits are scheduled through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal. Visitors must be on the incarcerated person's approved Visitors List, and a visit is generally allowed once per visiting cycle (Monday through Sunday), lasting up to two hours for a regular visit.

The unit's mixed population is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while state-jail and more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

The Lopez Unit is in Hidalgo County, about 10 miles north of Edinburg off Highway 281, in the Rio Grande Valley of far south Texas. Edinburg sits next to McAllen in the Valley's main metro area, but the unit itself is on the area's northern edge where commercial services thin out.

:::key-info
**From McAllen / Edinburg:** North on Highway 281 toward the unit; typically a short local drive of about 20-30 minutes from central Edinburg

**From San Antonio:** I-37 and US-281 South toward the Rio Grande Valley; often about 4-4.5 hours

**From Houston:** US-59/I-69 South toward the Valley, then local roads to Edinburg; often about 5.5-6 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Because the unit is in far south Texas near the Mexico border, families coming from most of the state face a long drive. Travel of more than 300 miles may qualify for a special visit of up to four hours under TDCJ's statewide rules — confirmed with the unit in advance.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Edinburg and the neighboring city of McAllen form the Rio Grande Valley's main metro area, with gas, food, groceries, and lodging widely available before the drive north to the unit. Options thin out on the unit's northern approach, so families often plan stops in the Edinburg-McAllen area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
