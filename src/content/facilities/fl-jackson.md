## Overview

Jackson Correctional Institution is a **general-population men's prison** near Malone in Jackson County, in the far-northwest Florida Panhandle. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, opened in 1991, holds men in mixed custody (minimum, medium, and close), and has a capacity of about 1,346. The institution is on **Central Time**, and it is a separate facility from the Jackson County jail/detention center in Marianna.

## What Makes Jackson Different

- **It is on Central Time,** unlike most of Florida — confirm visiting hours in the institution's local (Central) time.
- **A Jackson Work Camp sits on the north boundary** of the institution as a separate unit; confirm whether the person is at the main unit or the work camp.
- **It is in the far-northwest Panhandle** near the Alabama and Georgia lines, a long drive from most of the state.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
