:::callout{variant="info"}
**Name change:** This unit was the Mountain View Unit until the Texas Board of Criminal Justice renamed it the Patrick L. O'Daniel Unit (announced January 2024). It is the same facility at the same Gatesville address, and TDCJ still uses the old unit code "MV." Older paperwork, websites, and word of mouth may use either name.
:::

:::key-info
**Location:** Ransom Road off FM 215, about four miles north of Gatesville in Coryell County

**Capacity:** 644 per TDCJ's unit directory

**Notable:** Houses the women on Texas's death row, alongside general population with education and program missions

**Visitation:** Saturday-Sunday 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m. for general population; death row visits run on a separate weekday and Saturday-evening schedule
:::

## Overview

The Patrick L. O'Daniel Unit, formerly Mountain View, is one of five women's facilities in Gatesville and the one with the most specialized mission: TDCJ's death row information page states that the women on death row are housed here. The rest of the unit holds women across the custody range, with around-the-clock medical and inpatient mental health beds on site.

The site has held institutions since 1962, when it opened as a school for boys; TDCJ acquired it and opened the women's prison in July 1975. Among its operations today is a Braille manufacturing facility, described in published accounts as one of the largest prison Braille programs in the country, transcribing textbooks for Texas schoolchildren.

## What Makes Patrick L. O'Daniel Unit Different

- It houses Texas's women's death row — the female counterpart to the men's row at Polunsky — which brings death row's distinct visiting rules to this unit.
- The 2024 renaming means records and search results split between two names for the same prison; the unit code remains "MV."
- Death row here includes a work-capable classification: under TDCJ's visitation policy, work-capable women on death row are allowed weekly visits and use the general-population non-contact visiting area.
- A Braille manufacturing facility and Braille instruction operate on site, alongside horticulture, cosmetology, and academic education programs.
- Medical services run 24/7 with 20 inpatient mental health beds — a larger clinical footprint than the unit's size suggests.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at O'Daniel. General population and death row follow different schedules and different scheduling paths.

:::key-info
**General population (TDCJ unit visitation schedule, June 2026):**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Restricted-status visits are posted from 8:30 a.m.
- Video visiting: Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m., scheduled in advance through the portal.

**Death row:**
- Visits are non-contact only, generally two hours, and scheduled through the warden's office — not the online portal.
- Visit frequency depends on the person's death row classification: weekly for work-capable and Level 1, twice monthly for Level 2, monthly for Level 3.
- TDCJ's two official sources currently post different death-row schedules (the live portal lists Tuesday and Thursday afternoons plus Saturday evening; the April 2025 policy lists different windows). The warden's office confirms the current schedule when the visit is booked.
:::

Because death row visits route through the warden's office, families arranging a first visit call the unit at (254) 865-7226. Account setup and phone rates are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Getting There and Parking

O'Daniel is on Ransom Road off FM 215, about four miles north of central Gatesville per TDCJ's directory.

:::key-info
**From Waco:** US-84 West about 37 miles to Gatesville, then north on FM 215 to Ransom Road; often about an hour

**From Killeen:** US-190/FM 116 connectors north to Gatesville; often about an hour

**From Austin:** I-35 North to the Belton/Temple area, then TX-36 North to Gatesville and FM 215 north to Ransom Road; often about 2-2.5 hours

**From Dallas-Fort Worth:** I-35W South toward Waco, then US-84 West; often about 2.5-3 hours

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area. Vehicles entering TDCJ property are subject to search.
:::

TDCJ does not publish visitor parking details for the unit. There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, and no direct intercity bus serves Gatesville; The HOP's in-town microtransit runs weekdays only, so weekend visits are by car.

## Nearby Services

Gatesville is a small town with services along Main Street and the TX-36 corridor; hotel sites list a handful of in-town properties, including Days Inn by Wyndham and Comfort Inn & Suites. Waco, about an hour east, has the nearest large hotel and restaurant cluster.

Coryell Health on West Main Street in Gatesville operates the local hospital with a Level IV trauma center emergency room; the nearest comprehensive (Level I) trauma center is Baylor Scott & White in Temple, about 35 miles away.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Incarcerated Families Association (TIFA) is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation for families; its helpline is (512) 371-0900.
