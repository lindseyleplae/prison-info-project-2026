## Overview

Wakulla Correctional Institution is a **men's state prison** in Crawfordville, Wakulla County, in the Florida Panhandle south of Tallahassee. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections. The institution has two parts with separate missions, and its combined capacity is about **2,929**.

The **main unit** transitioned in 2005 to operate as Florida's flagship **Faith- and Character-Based Institution** — the largest faith-based programming in the state. The **Wakulla CI Annex**, which opened in 2008, **does not share that mission**: it holds a higher classification level and includes protective-management and mentally-ill populations.

## What Makes Wakulla Different

- **The main unit is Florida's flagship faith- and character-based institution,** with the largest faith-based programming in the state (since 2005).
- **The Wakulla CI Annex has a different mission** — it holds a higher classification level and includes protective-management and mentally-ill populations.
- **Visiting is by reservation after approval,** so confirm the person's status, institution, and unit before traveling.
- **The institution and unit shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
