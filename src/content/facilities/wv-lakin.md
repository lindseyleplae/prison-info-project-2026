## Overview

Lakin Correctional Center, in West Columbia on the Ohio River, is **West Virginia's only women's prison**, holding women at **all custody levels from minimum to maximum**. Because it is the state's sole women's facility, every woman sentenced to state prison is held here. It **opened in 2003**, and its programs include adult education and high-school equivalency, culinary arts, horticulture and landscaping, and an on-site garment and sewing operation. It also operates a **KIDS Unit** (Keeping Infant Development Successful), a prison-nursery program in which a baby born to a woman while she is incarcerated can stay with her at the facility for up to about 18 months. West Virginia has no death penalty, so there is no death row.

## What Makes Lakin Different

- **It is the only women's prison in West Virginia,** holding women at every custody level.
- **It is on the Ohio River near Point Pleasant,** in the far western part of the state close to the Ohio line.
- **It offers vocational programs** including culinary arts, horticulture, and a garment factory.
- **It runs a prison nursery (the KIDS Unit),** where an infant born to a woman during her incarceration can remain with her for up to about 18 months.
- **It sits on historic ground** — see the note below.

## Visiting

The statewide WV DCR rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Lakin.

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Statewide, prison visits are Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.; the facility sets its own times within that — call (304) 674-2440 to confirm

**How it works:** Visits are scheduled in advance by the incarcerated person, not walk-in; the visitor must be on the approved list first

**Contact vs. non-contact:** Prison visits at Lakin are contact visits
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in West Virginia](/states/west-virginia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Ohio River Road (Route 62) in West Columbia, north of Point Pleasant.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 60 miles from Charleston via U.S. 35 to Point Pleasant, then north about 6 miles; about 48 miles from Huntington via Route 2

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Point Pleasant has the nearest food, gas, and lodging, with more across the Ohio River in Gallipolis, Ohio. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **Pleasant Valley Hospital** in Point Pleasant, about 6 miles south.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since 2023, West Virginia routes personal letters to an **off-site scanning center in Maryland**, addressed with the person's name and **OID number**; the person receives the mail as **images on a tablet**, and photos must be sent through the tablet rather than mailed. **Legal mail, books, and magazines** go to the facility. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/west-virginia/guides/mail/).

## A Note on History

The prison opened in 2003 on land that was once part of the **Lakin State Hospital**, a state hospital established in **1926** to serve African American patients during the era of racial segregation and notably staffed by Black professionals. A separate long-term-care state hospital still operates near the prison. This history is documented in the sources cited above.
