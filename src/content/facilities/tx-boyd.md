## Overview

The William R. Boyd Unit is a TDCJ men's prison near Teague in Freestone County, in east-central Texas. TDCJ lists it at about 1,372 beds on roughly 734 acres. The unit holds a general-population custody mix (G1, G2, G4) along with a dedicated safekeeping population.

Two facts shape much of what visiting looks like here: the unit's mixed custody levels and its rural east-central Texas location.

## What Makes the Boyd Unit Different

-   Houses a **safekeeping** population — incarcerated people identified as vulnerable — in addition to general population, with a small single-cell housing area that includes wheelchair accommodations
-   Holds a broad G1, G2, and G4 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Houses the Windham School District Region II administrative office and offers career-and-technology training (automotive, carpentry, landscape design) and a faith-based dormitory
-   Sits near Teague in Freestone County, west of Fairfield off Highway 84, in a rural part of east-central Texas

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Boyd Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing and safekeeping status can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

The Boyd Unit is near Teague in Freestone County, in a rural section of east-central Texas. It sits west of Fairfield off Highway 84, and the trip is by private vehicle.

:::key-info
**From Dallas:** I-45 South toward the Fairfield area, then west on Highway 84 toward Teague; often about 1.5-2 hours

**From Waco:** Eastbound highways toward the Mexia and Teague area; often about 1-1.25 hours

**From Houston:** I-45 North toward the Fairfield area, then west toward Teague; often about 2.5-3 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Teague is a small town with limited commercial services. Fairfield, the Freestone County seat to the east on I-45, is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging before the unit, and options thin out on the final approach toward Teague.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
