## Overview

Santa Rosa Correctional Institution is one of Florida's primary **close-management** (long-term restrictive-housing) men's prisons, near Milton in Santa Rosa County, in the Panhandle about 25 miles east of Pensacola. It opened in 1996 and is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections. The institution comprises a **main unit, an Annex (opened 2006), and a work camp**, and holds men across custody levels, including close management.

## What Makes Santa Rosa Different

- **It is one of Florida's primary close-management institutions,** holding men in long-term restrictive housing alongside other custody levels.
- **It comprises a main unit, an Annex, and a work camp.**
- **Because it includes restrictive housing, visiting can be more limited for people in close management** — confirm the person's status and the current schedule before traveling.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
