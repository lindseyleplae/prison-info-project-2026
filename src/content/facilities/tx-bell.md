## Overview

The Oliver J. Bell Unit is a pre-release facility for men in Cleveland, in Liberty County, northeast of Houston. TDCJ lists it at about 520 beds, making it one of the agency's smaller units. It was renamed from the Cleveland Unit in 2020, and TDCJ has operated it directly since September 2023.

The unit's pre-release mission and its smaller size are the two facts that shape most of what visiting looks like here.

## What Makes the Bell Unit Different

-   A pre-release unit, so it holds minimum-custody men generally within range of parole or release, and the population turns over as people move on
-   Renamed from the Cleveland Unit in 2020 and operated directly by TDCJ since September 2023
-   Reentry-focused programming, including the Prison Entrepreneurship Program (PEP) alongside education and vocational training
-   A smaller unit at about 520 beds, distinct from the larger general-population prisons elsewhere in TDCJ

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Bell Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because the Bell Unit is a pre-release facility, a person's assignment here can be short. A move toward parole, release, or another unit can happen between scheduled visits, so confirming the current unit on the TDCJ inmate locator before traveling avoids a wasted trip.

Visiting formats follow the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule, so families should confirm the current arrangement with the unit when scheduling.

## Getting There and Parking

The Bell Unit is in Cleveland, in Liberty County, northeast of Houston. The unit sits just north of downtown Cleveland.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** Northbound highways toward Cleveland in Liberty County, then local streets to the unit; often about 60-75 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The trip is by private vehicle. There is no fixed-route bus service to the unit, so families plan on driving.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
