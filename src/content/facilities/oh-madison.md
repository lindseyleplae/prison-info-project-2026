## Overview

Madison Correctional Institution (MaCI), on State Route 56 in London, Madison County, is a **men's prison** spanning **minimum to close security** in central Ohio. It holds about **2,500 men** and opened in **1987**. Madison is a separate prison from the nearby London Correctional Institution, also in London — confirm which one holds the person.

## What Makes Madison Different

- **It is a separate prison from the London Correctional Institution,** which is also in London — confirm which one holds the person before traveling.
- **It spans minimum to close security,** holding men across a range of custody levels.
- **It is in London, central Ohio,** west of Columbus.
- **It opened in 1987.**

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in London, west of Columbus.

:::key-info
**By car:** In London, reached via Interstate 70 and State Route 56, west of Columbus; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

London has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Madison Health** in London.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
