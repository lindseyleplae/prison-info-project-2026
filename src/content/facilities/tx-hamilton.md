## Overview

The J. W. Hamilton Unit is a TDCJ men's prison in Bryan, in Brazos County. TDCJ lists it at about 1,166 beds. It operates as a pre-release and transfer-type facility, with a pre-release therapeutic community and a DWI recovery program, and houses G1 and G2 custody levels along with a substance-abuse program.

The unit's pre-release function and its Central Texas location near College Station shape most of what families need to know about visiting here.

## What Makes the Hamilton Unit Different

-   This is the Texas J. W. Hamilton Unit in Bryan, Brazos County — a men's pre-release and transfer-type facility, unrelated to similarly named facilities in other states
-   Operates as a pre-release and transfer unit, which can mean shorter stays; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, so families should confirm the person is still housed here before traveling
-   Houses G1 and G2 custody levels plus a substance-abuse program, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Runs vocational training and community-work programs from a roughly 350-acre site west of Bryan

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Hamilton Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visits are arranged through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal, and the visitor must already be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list. The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because this is a pre-release and transfer unit, assignments can change. Confirming the current unit on the TDCJ inmate locator before a trip avoids a drive to the wrong facility.

## Getting There and Parking

The Hamilton Unit is in Bryan, in Brazos County, in Central Texas near College Station. The drive finishes on local roads west of the city.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** Northwest highways toward the Bryan-College Station area, then local roads to the unit; often about 1.5-2 hours

**From Austin:** East and northeast highways toward the Bryan-College Station area, then local roads to the unit; often about 1.5-2 hours

**From Dallas:** I-45 South with connectors toward the Bryan-College Station area, then local roads to the unit; often about 3-3.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The trip is by private vehicle. Bryan-College Station has commercial air and intercity bus service, but there is no fixed-route transit to the unit itself, so the final approach is by car.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Bryan and College Station form the service area for the unit, with gas, food, groceries, and lodging available across the two cities. Hotel sites list chain properties throughout the Bryan-College Station area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
