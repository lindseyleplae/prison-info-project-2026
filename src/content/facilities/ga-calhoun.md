## Overview

Calhoun State Prison, on Main Street in Morgan in Calhoun County, is a **medium-security state prison for men** in southwest Georgia. The facility opened in 1994 and holds adult men across nine housing units and eight open dormitories, with a capacity of about **1,677**. Its mission covers medium- and minimum-security men, and it operates a Corrections Emergency Response Team (C.E.R.T.) and an on-site fire station.

## What Makes Calhoun Different

- **It is a general-population medium-security men's prison,** without the intake or special-mission roles of some larger GDC facilities.
- **It opened in 1994** and houses men across nine housing units and eight open dormitories.
- **It operates a C.E.R.T. team and an on-site fire station.**
- **It is in rural southwest Georgia,** about 40 miles west of Albany.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Calhoun State Prison. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays — confirm the current hours with the facility

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Main Street in Morgan, in Calhoun County in rural southwest Georgia.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 40 miles west of Albany — Morgan is reached by State Route 45 and State Route 37; the facility is on Main Street in town

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Morgan is a small town; Albany, about 40 miles east, has the closest full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest large hospital with a 24-hour emergency room is **Phoebe Putney Memorial Hospital** in Albany.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
