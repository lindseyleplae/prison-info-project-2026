## Overview

The Massachusetts Treatment Center, on Administration Road in Bridgewater, is a **medium-security DOC facility for men**. It provides treatment for men serving sentences for sex offenses, and it also holds men who have been **civilly committed as "sexually dangerous persons"** under Massachusetts law (Chapter 123A) — that is, held for treatment beyond a criminal sentence. The Treatment Center is **one of several DOC facilities at the Bridgewater complex**, alongside the Old Colony Correctional Center and Bridgewater State Hospital, so confirm which one holds the person before traveling.

## What Makes the Massachusetts Treatment Center Different

- **It provides sex-offense treatment for men,** combining a correctional setting with a treatment mission.
- **It holds men civilly committed as sexually dangerous persons** under Chapter 123A — held for treatment beyond a criminal sentence.

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Administration Road in Bridgewater, in southeastern Massachusetts.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Bridgewater, reached from Route 24 and Route 104, south of Brockton; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Bridgewater and Brockton areas have gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Signature Healthcare Brockton Hospital** in Brockton, about 10 miles away.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail goes to the prison — Massachusetts does not use an off-site mail vendor. Address it with the person's full name and commitment number and the facility's mailing address (above); all incoming mail is opened and inspected. **Legal mail** is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/massachusetts/guides/mail/).
