## Overview

Macon State Prison, on Highway 49 South in Oglethorpe in Macon County, is a **close-security state prison for men** in central Georgia, southwest of the city of Macon. Opened in 1994, it is one of the state's higher-security men's prisons, with a capacity of about **1,762**. As a Tier I and II facility, it includes segregation and special-management (restrictive-housing) units alongside general-population housing, and it runs academic, vocational, counseling, and religious programs.

The name can cause confusion: **Macon State Prison is in Oglethorpe (Macon County)**, while **Central State Prison is a separate GDC prison in the city of Macon (Bibb County)**. Confirm which facility holds the person on GDC's Find an Offender before traveling.

## What Makes Macon Different

- **It is a close-security prison,** one of the higher-security tiers in the Georgia system, so visiting and movement may be more restricted than at a medium-security prison.
- **Some housing units may be non-contact.** Men in segregation or special management may have restricted or non-contact visits — confirm the current arrangements with the facility.
- **It is in Oglethorpe, Macon County,** in central Georgia southwest of the city of Macon — not to be confused with Central State Prison in the city of Macon.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Macon State Prison. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays — confirm the facility's current visiting hours

**Higher-security restrictions:** As a close-security prison, visiting may be more restricted, and some housing units may be non-contact — confirm with the facility

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Highway 49 South in Oglethorpe, in Macon County, central Georgia southwest of the city of Macon.

:::key-info
**By car:** Oglethorpe is in central Georgia, southwest of Macon and near Montezuma; the prison is on Highway 49 South

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Montezuma and nearby Americus have the closest gas, food, and lodging; Macon, about 40 miles northeast, has the fullest range. The nearest hospitals are in the Americus area (Phoebe Sumter Medical Center) and in Macon (Atrium Health Navicent), each with a 24-hour emergency room.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
