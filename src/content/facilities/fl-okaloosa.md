## Overview

Okaloosa Correctional Institution is a **general-population men's prison** near Crestview in Okaloosa County, in the Florida Panhandle. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds men in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 894. Because it is in the Panhandle, the institution observes **Central Time**, while most of Florida is on Eastern Time.

## What Makes Okaloosa Different

- **It is on Central Time.** Crestview and the western Panhandle observe Central Time, so the institution's visiting hours — generally about 8:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. Central — run an hour behind most Florida prisons. Confirm the current schedule before traveling.
- **It operates the Okaloosa Work Camp** as a satellite unit.
- **It offers vocational programs** through Northwest Florida State College, including construction, heavy equipment operation, and masonry, along with landscape and turf management.
- **It runs faith and reentry programming,** including "7 Habits on the Inside," a 100-hour transition program, Prison Fellowship, and a reentry dorm.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
