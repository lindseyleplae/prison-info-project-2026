## Overview

Maury Correctional Institution is a close- and medium-security prison for men in Maury, Greene County, in eastern North Carolina. The facility opened in April 2006 and houses adult men in close and medium custody. It is operated by the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction (NCDAC), the agency that has run the state's adult prisons since the prison system separated from the Department of Public Safety on January 1, 2023.

North Carolina assigns custody level at intake and uses a distributed reception model for men, with major processing at Craven Correctional Institution in Vanceboro, a western diagnostic at Piedmont Correctional Institution in Salisbury, and a special-population diagnostic at Central Prison in Raleigh. Because families are not notified of routine transfers, the [NCDAC offender locator](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view) is the way to confirm where someone is currently held. The locator uses each person's seven-digit OPUS number.

## What Makes Maury Different

Maury Correctional Institution operates specialized housing units for several offender populations. According to NCDAC, these include residential mental health, outpatient mental health, chronic medical, a unit for military veterans, restrictive housing, and a therapeutic diversion unit. The custody level assigned to an individual affects day-to-day movement and whether a visit is contact or non-contact, so the rules a family encounters can differ from one person to another. A separate facility, Eastern Correctional Institution, is also located in Maury in Greene County; the [NCDAC offender locator](https://webapps.doc.state.nc.us/opi/offendersearch.do?method=view) is the way to confirm which of the two holds a particular person before arranging a visit or sending anything.

## Visiting

The statewide NCDAC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Maury Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approval first:** Every visitor must be on the offender's approved visiting list. The incarcerated person mails an application to each prospective visitor (up to 18 applications); applicants 16 and older attach a copy of a photo ID, and minors under 16 a copy of a birth certificate. The completed form goes to the prison where the person is housed.
- **Scheduling:** Visits are by appointment only. North Carolina has no statewide online visit scheduler; call or email Maury Correctional Institution to schedule, and confirm the person's visitation status the day before.
- **Visit limits:** Generally one visit per week, up to two hours, with up to three approved visitors. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on custody class and disciplinary status.
- **Video visits:** Scheduled separately through the GettingOut Visits app (ViaPath).
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Maury Correctional Institution is at 2568 Moore Rouse Road, Hookerton, NC 28538, in Greene County in eastern North Carolina, between Goldsboro and Kinston. The area is rural, and there is no scheduled public transit to the prison; visitors generally arrive by private vehicle. Parking is on site. Confirm the visiting schedule and the person's custody and visitation status with the facility before making the trip.

## Nearby Services

Greene County is rural; the closest full-service hospital emergency rooms are in the Goldsboro and Kinston areas, each within roughly a half-hour drive. Lodging, fuel, and restaurants are available in and around Goldsboro and Kinston.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. North Carolina sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and scans it and delivers a digital copy to the incarcerated person's tablet; original letters, cards, and packages are returned to the sender. Address personal mail with the person's full name and **OPUS number** and the full (unabbreviated) prison name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 247, Phoenix, MD 21131 — and include the sender's full first and last name in the return address. **Legal mail** is exempt and goes directly to the facility. **Books and publications** must come from a publisher or online retailer, shipped to the prison's street address, not the TextBehind box. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).
