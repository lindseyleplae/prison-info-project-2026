## Overview

The Souza-Baranowski Correctional Center, on Harvard Road in Lancaster, is **Massachusetts's only maximum-security prison** and the **statewide reception and diagnostic center for men** — the point where newly committed men are received, assessed, and then assigned and transferred to a permanent prison (a role consolidated here after the closures of MCI-Cedar Junction and MCI-Concord). It opened in 1998. The prison is physically in Lancaster, but its mailing address uses a **Shirley P.O. box**, so both place names appear in records. Because of its security level and reception role, visiting and movement can be more controlled than at lower-security prisons.

## What Makes Souza-Baranowski Different

- **It is the men's reception and diagnostic center,** so a newly committed man usually begins here before transferring to a permanent prison.
- **It is the state's only maximum-security prison,** so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted, and some housing has non-contact visits.
- **It is in Lancaster but uses a Shirley mailing address** — confirm the correct address when sending mail.
- **Visiting is limited during the reception period,** so confirm the person's status and location before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide MA DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Souza-Baranowski. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Scheduling:** Visits are scheduled by phone with the facility — there is no statewide online scheduler; confirm the days, hours, and how far ahead to call

**Visit type:** Visiting for men in maximum-security or restrictive housing may be non-contact — confirm the housing and visit type
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Massachusetts](/states/massachusetts/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Harvard Road in Lancaster, in central Massachusetts.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Lancaster, reached from Route 2 and Route 70 in central Massachusetts, between Leominster and the Shirley/Ayer area

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Leominster and Shirley/Ayer areas have gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **UMass Memorial HealthAlliance-Clinton Hospital** in Leominster.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail goes to the prison — Massachusetts does not use an off-site mail vendor. Address it with the person's full name and commitment number and the facility's mailing address (above); all incoming mail is opened and inspected. **Legal mail** is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/massachusetts/guides/mail/).
