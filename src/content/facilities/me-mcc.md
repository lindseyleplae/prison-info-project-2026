## Overview

The Maine Correctional Center is the **primary reception and classification facility** for Maine DOC — the place where most newly sentenced men and women are received and assessed before being assigned a custody level and facility. The campus was **rebuilt and opened in 2022**, with an official grand opening in 2023, and is organized around assessment on arrival and immediate enrollment in treatment and programs, including substance-use treatment, education and GED, vocational training, and a gardening program. It holds men in medium and minimum custody and, in its **Women's Center**, most of the state's incarcerated women. A separate women's facility, the **Southern Maine Women's Reentry Center**, is also in Windham — so families should confirm which of the two (or which unit) holds a person, since visiting differs.

## What Makes the Maine Correctional Center Different

- **It is Maine's intake hub.** Both men and women are received and classified here before going to their assigned facility.
- **It holds women.** The Women's Center is the custodial unit for most incarcerated women in Maine; the Southern Maine Women's Reentry Center, also in Windham, holds women in pre-release.
- **It is the most accessible facility.** About 22 minutes from Portland, with major hospitals, lodging, and a bus corridor nearby — closer for southern-Maine families than the midcoast or Downeast prisons.

## Visiting

The statewide Maine DOC rules above — the application and background check, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at the Maine Correctional Center. The facility's own schedule runs **by housing unit**:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Each housing unit (the men's pods, the women's units, and the Women's Center) schedules on its own days, in daytime blocks and an evening block — confirm the current grid for the person's unit

**Scheduling:** By appointment through the online MCC visit-request form, at least two business days ahead; the voicemail (207) 893-7109 is for questions only

**Video:** Free video visits are offered through Ameelio at the pilot units, alongside a paid option — see the phone and video guide
:::

Because the per-unit schedule is posted as a grid that changes, confirm it on the facility's page or with the visit office. The full approval process is in [Visiting in Maine](/states/maine/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The facility is on Mallison Falls Road in Windham, off Route 302 northwest of Portland.

:::key-info
**From Portland:** About 10 miles — roughly 22 minutes

**From Augusta:** About 61 miles — roughly 1 hour 15 minutes

**Transit:** The Lakes Region Explorer bus serves the Route 302 corridor in North Windham, several miles from the facility; there is no transit to the door, so a vehicle is needed for the final leg
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

North Windham, on Route 302, has gas, food, and lodging, with more in Portland a short drive south. **MaineHealth Maine Medical Center** at 22 Bramhall Street in Portland is the region's Level I trauma center with a 24/7 emergency room, and **Northern Light Mercy Hospital** in Portland also has 24/7 emergency care.

## Mail

The Maine Correctional Center uses Maine's split mail routing. First-class **letters** go to **813 Cushing Road, Warren, ME 04864** (the central scanning address), while **packages, magazines, and legal mail** go to the campus at **17 Mallison Falls Road, Windham, ME 04062**. Women at the Women's Center use the same addresses. Put the resident's name and MDOC number on everything — full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/maine/guides/mail/).
