## Overview

Waupun Correctional Institution (WCI) is a maximum-security prison for men in Waupun, Dodge County, Wisconsin. Opened in 1851, it is the oldest prison in the state. The institution is operated by the Wisconsin Department of Corrections through its Division of Adult Institutions.

Wisconsin's statewide men's intake and reception is handled at Dodge Correctional Institution, also in Waupun; a person's security level is assigned at intake. Because families are not automatically notified of transfers, the [WI DOC offender locator](https://appsdoc.wi.gov/lop/welcome) is the way to confirm where someone is currently held.

Wisconsin has no death penalty. The state abolished capital punishment in 1853, and there is no death row or execution chamber anywhere in the Wisconsin prison system.

## What Makes Waupun Different

Waupun Correctional Institution is the oldest prison in Wisconsin, opened in 1851. It predates the rest of the state's adult institutions and has operated as a maximum-security men's prison.

Under a 2025 state plan, Waupun is slated to convert from a maximum-security prison to a medium-security vocational prison. As of mid-2026, only planning funds had been approved, and the institution remains maximum security. Because a facility's mission and population can change during a transition like this, the [offender locator](https://appsdoc.wi.gov/lop/welcome) should be used to confirm the person's current institution before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide WI DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the Waupun Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approval first:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visiting list. The person mails a **DOC-21AA Visitor Questionnaire** to the prospective visitor, who completes it and returns it to the institution; the person is notified when a visitor is approved.
- **Scheduling:** Wisconsin has no statewide online visit scheduler. In-person visits are arranged with the institution. Video visits are scheduled separately through ICSolutions.
- **Visitor limits:** People on intake ("A&E") status may have up to 4 adult close-family visitors; general population allows up to 12 adults. Minors do not count toward the cap until age 18.
- **Restrictive housing:** Visits for people in restrictive housing are often conducted by audio-visual equipment rather than in person.
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Waupun Correctional Institution is at 200 S. Madison St., Waupun, WI 53963-0351, in Dodge County in east-central Wisconsin. There is no public-transit service to the institution; visitors generally arrive by private vehicle. Confirm visiting days and hours with the facility before traveling, and arrive no more than 15 minutes before the scheduled visit.

## Nearby Services

Waupun is in Dodge County, with the Fond du Lac and Beaver Dam areas nearby. The closest full-service hospital emergency rooms are in those communities. Lodging, fuel, and restaurants are available in Waupun and in the larger Fond du Lac and Beaver Dam areas.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. Wisconsin sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and photocopies it and forwards copies to the institution; people with tablets may also receive electronic photos. Address personal mail with the person's full name, **DOC number**, and the full (unabbreviated) facility name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 189, Phoenix, MD 21131. **Legal and privileged mail** is exempt and goes directly to the institution at its own legal-mail address. **Books and other publications** must come from an approved vendor, shipped directly from the retailer, not from individuals. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/wisconsin/guides/mail/).
