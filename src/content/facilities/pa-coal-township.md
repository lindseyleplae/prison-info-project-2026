## Overview

SCI Coal Township, on Kelley Drive in Coal Township, is a **medium-security prison for men** in Northumberland County, in central Pennsylvania's coal region near Shamokin and Sunbury. It is one of Pennsylvania's State Correctional Institutions, operated by the Department of Corrections.

## What Makes Coal Township Different

- **It is medium security,** and visits are reserved in advance through the Inmate Visitation System.
- **It is in the coal region,** in Coal Township near Shamokin and Sunbury, in central Pennsylvania.
- **Personal mail goes off-site,** to a Smart Communications center in Florida, rather than to the prison.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Coal Township. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** Coal Township sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Kelley Drive in Coal Township, in Northumberland County in central Pennsylvania.

:::key-info
**By car:** In the coal region near Shamokin and Sunbury — from U.S. 11 or State Route 61, take local roads through the Coal Township and Shamokin area to Kelley Drive

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Shamokin, Sunbury, and Danville area has the usual range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Sunbury and Danville area, about 20–30 miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
