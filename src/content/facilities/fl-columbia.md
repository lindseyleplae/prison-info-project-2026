## Overview

Columbia Correctional Institution is a **men's state prison** in Lake City, Columbia County, in North Florida. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections and opened in 1992. The institution has two parts — the **main unit** and the **Columbia CI Annex** (opened in 2004) — and holds men across a range of custody levels, while also carrying a **close-management (restrictive-housing)** function alongside general population.

Columbia Correctional Institution (the state prison) is distinct from the privately operated **Lake City Correctional Facility** (run by CoreCivic), which is also in Lake City. Confirm on the FDC Corrections Offender Network which institution holds the person.

## What Makes Columbia Different

- **It has two parts** — the main unit and the Columbia CI Annex.
- **It carries a close-management (restrictive-housing) role** alongside general population.
- **Close-management housing can mean more limited visiting,** so confirm the person's housing status and the current schedule before traveling.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
