## Overview

Graceville Correctional Facility is a **privately operated men's prison** near Graceville in Jackson County, in the Florida Panhandle. It holds people in Florida Department of Corrections custody under a contract monitored by the Department of Management Services, holds men in medium security, and has a capacity of about 1,884.

## What Makes Graceville Different

- **It is privately operated for FDC.** Management & Training Corporation (MTC) runs the facility; FDC has announced that the GEO Group takes over operation on July 1, 2026. Confirm the current operator and contact details before traveling.
- **It runs reentry-focused programs,** including adult basic education and GED, substance-abuse treatment, and vocational training in areas such as an Auburn K-9 program, barbering, commercial driving (CDL), and landscape management.
- **It is in Jackson County, which observes Central Time** — confirm the facility's time zone and current visiting hours before traveling.
- **Money is sent through the operator's deposit path,** which can differ from the FDC-standard vendor and may change with the operator transition; confirm the current method in [Sending Money](/states/florida/guides/money/) and with the facility.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
