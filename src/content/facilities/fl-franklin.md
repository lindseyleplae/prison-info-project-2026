## Overview

Franklin Correctional Institution is a **general-population men's prison** near Carrabelle in Franklin County, on the Florida Panhandle Gulf coast. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds men in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 1,039.

## What Makes Franklin Different

- **It is a general-population men's institution** on the Panhandle's Gulf coast.
- **It offers academic and vocational programs,** including a diesel-service-technician track and entrepreneurial and small-business training.
- **It runs faith-based and reentry programming,** including a faith-and-character dorm, a seminary, and parenting programming, along with a substance-abuse component.
- **The on-grounds Franklin Work Camp is temporarily closed,** while the main institution remains open.

## Getting There and Parking

The institution is near Carrabelle in Franklin County, on the Florida Panhandle Gulf coast, north of the town on Highway 67. Although it is in the Panhandle, Franklin County observes Eastern Time.

:::key-info

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
