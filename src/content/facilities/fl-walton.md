## Overview

Walton Correctional Institution is a **large general-population men's prison** near DeFuniak Springs in Walton County, in the Florida Panhandle. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds men in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 1,201. Because it is in the western Panhandle, the institution is on **Central Time**.

## What Makes Walton Different

- **It is a large general-population men's institution** in the Florida Panhandle.
- **It is on Central Time,** one hour behind most of the state — visiting hours run on Central Time.
- **It offers vocational programs,** including HVAC and residential carpentry and cabinetmaking.
- **It runs a broad reentry program slate,** including CDL readiness, employability, financial skills, a faith- and character-based dorm, and a veterans dorm; its work camp hosts a "PAWS for a Cause" dog-training program.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
