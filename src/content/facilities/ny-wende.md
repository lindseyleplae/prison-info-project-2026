## Overview

Wende Correctional Facility, in Alden, is a **maximum-security prison** for men in Western New York, east of Buffalo. It is a hub facility: it serves as a **men's reception/classification center** for Western New York, and it houses a **Regional Medical Unit (RMU)**, one of the state's inpatient medical units. Like all maximum-security facilities, visiting and movement are more controlled than at lower-security prisons.

## What Makes Wende Different

- **It is a maximum-security prison,** so visiting, movement, and property are more restricted.
- **It is a men's reception/classification center** for Western New York. Newly received men have limited visiting until they are classified.
- **It houses a Regional Medical Unit (RMU),** one of the state's inpatient medical units. **Visiting for an RMU patient is arranged separately** (see Visiting).
- **It has a Special Housing Unit (SHU),** where custody allows one non-legal visit per seven-day period.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Wende Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are on Wednesdays and weekends within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Visit size:** A maximum of 3 adults plus 1 child under age 5

**SHU custody:** A person in the Special Housing Unit is allowed one non-legal visit per seven-day period

**RMU patients:** Visiting for a patient in the Regional Medical Unit is handled separately, in a dedicated RMU visit room and generally arranged around the patient's medical-clearance status — confirm the specifics with the facility

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
