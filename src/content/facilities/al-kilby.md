## Overview

Kilby Correctional Facility, on Wares Ferry Road in Mt. Meigs just northeast of Montgomery, is **Alabama's statewide reception and classification center for men**. It **receives all new male state inmates except those sentenced to death and youthful offenders**, evaluates and classifies them, and assigns them to a permanent prison. Because of that role, a newly sentenced man may be held at Kilby only briefly before transfer — so the facility shown in the inmate search can change within weeks. Kilby is a **close-custody** facility (it also houses maximum- and medium-custody men) and operates **hospital and mental-health services**, along with dental and specialty care, that serve the broader system. It holds about **1,390 men**. The current facility opened in **December 1969**.

## What Makes Kilby Different

- **It is Alabama's reception and classification center for men,** receiving most newly sentenced men before they are assigned to a permanent prison.
- **A person may be here only briefly,** so confirm the person is still housed at Kilby before traveling.
- **It operates hospital and mental-health services** that serve the broader prison system.
- **It is close to Montgomery,** which makes it more reachable than Alabama's rural prisons.

## Visiting

The statewide ADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the no-tan dress code, ID, and the $30 vending limit — apply at Kilby. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Schedule:** ADOC does not post Kilby's visiting days or hours online; under the 2025 visitation rules (AR 303), all visits are pre-scheduled and approved

**Reception status:** A person being received may have limited visiting until classification is complete — confirm with the facility

**How to confirm:** Call the facility at (334) 215-6600, and check the inmate search to confirm the person is still here

**List:** The person must add the visitor to their approved list first (up to eight approved adults)
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Alabama](/states/alabama/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Wares Ferry Road in Mt. Meigs, just northeast of Montgomery.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 10–12 miles northeast of downtown Montgomery, off the U.S. 80 / Atlanta Highway corridor toward Mt. Meigs; reachable from Interstate 85

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is the practical option
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Montgomery, a short drive southwest, has the nearest and fullest range of hotels, restaurants, and services. The nearest hospital emergency rooms are in Montgomery — **Jackson Hospital** (closest to the Wares Ferry Road area) and **Baptist Medical Center South**.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since late 2025, ADOC routes personal letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site scanning center in San Antonio, Texas**, addressed with the person's name and **AIS number**; the person receives the mail as **images on a tablet**. **Legal mail and packages** still go to the facility. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/alabama/guides/mail/).

## A Note on History

The original Kilby Prison, which operated from the 1920s until 1970 on a nearby site, held Alabama's electric chair and was the state's execution site for decades. Executions in Alabama are now carried out at **Holman Correctional Facility**, not at the current Kilby facility, which replaced the original prison in 1969.
