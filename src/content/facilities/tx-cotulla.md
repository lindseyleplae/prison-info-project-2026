## Overview

The Cotulla Transfer Facility is a men's transfer unit in Cotulla, in La Salle County in South Texas, along I-35 between San Antonio and Laredo. TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds, opened in 1992, on a small footprint of roughly 36 acres.

As a transfer facility, its defining feature is short stays: many people are held here only briefly while awaiting assignment to a permanent unit.

## What Makes the Cotulla Unit Different

-   A transfer facility — most people pass through on the way to a permanent unit rather than serving a full term here, so stays are often short
-   Because stays are short, a person may be reassigned before a scheduled visit; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the unit a person is currently assigned to, and confirming it close to the visit date avoids a wasted trip
-   A small unit — TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds — on roughly 36 acres less than a mile east of Cotulla
-   Located in South Texas on the I-35 corridor between San Antonio and Laredo, away from a larger city

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Cotulla Transfer Facility. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visits are scheduled through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal, and a visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved Visitors List before a visit can be booked.

Because Cotulla is a short-stay transfer unit, a person may be moved to another unit before a scheduled visit takes place. Checking the TDCJ inmate locator for the current unit assignment shortly before traveling helps confirm the visit will be at this facility.

## Getting There and Parking

The Cotulla Transfer Facility is in La Salle County in South Texas, less than a mile east of Cotulla off FM 624. Cotulla sits on Interstate 35 between San Antonio to the north and Laredo to the south.

:::key-info
**From San Antonio:** I-35 South toward Laredo to the Cotulla exits, then the short FM 624 approach east of town; often about 1.5 hours

**From Laredo:** I-35 North toward San Antonio to the Cotulla exits, then FM 624; often about 1 hour

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle. Cotulla is directly on the I-35 corridor, which makes the highway approach straightforward, but the final stretch on FM 624 is rural.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Cotulla is a small city, with limited commercial services concentrated near the I-35 interchanges, where travelers will find gas, fast food, and a few chain motels. San Antonio, about 90 minutes north, is the nearest large service center for a wider range of lodging, groceries, and other needs before the drive south.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
