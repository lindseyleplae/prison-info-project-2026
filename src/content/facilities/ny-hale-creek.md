## Overview

Hale Creek Alcohol and Substance Abuse Correctional Treatment Center, in Johnstown, is a **medium-security treatment facility** for men. As an **Alcohol and Substance Abuse Correctional Treatment Center (ASACTC)**, its defining role is dedicated alcohol and substance abuse treatment, rather than general-population confinement. The "ASACTC" is part of the facility's official name.

## What Makes Hale Creek Different

- **It is an Alcohol and Substance Abuse Correctional Treatment Center (ASACTC),** a treatment-focused facility centered on alcohol and substance abuse treatment.
- **It is a medium-security facility** for men.
- **It is in Fulton County,** in the southern Adirondack foothills northwest of Albany.
- **Visiting days are commonly assigned by the last digit of the person's DIN,** so the days available may depend on the person's number.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Hale Creek. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; visiting days are commonly assigned by the last digit of the person's DIN — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The facility is in Johnstown, in Fulton County, in the southern Adirondack foothills northwest of Albany.

:::key-info

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Johnstown and the neighboring Gloversville area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Amsterdam area and the Capital District around Albany to the southeast. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Johnstown/Gloversville and Amsterdam areas.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
