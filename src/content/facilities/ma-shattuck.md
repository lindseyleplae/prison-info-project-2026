## Overview

The Lemuel Shattuck Hospital Correctional Unit is a secure Massachusetts DOC unit within **Lemuel Shattuck Hospital**, a state public-health hospital in the Jamaica Plain area of Boston. It provides hospital-level medical care for incarcerated men transferred from prisons across the state who need that level of care.

## What Makes the Shattuck Unit Different

- **It is a secure DOC unit inside a public hospital,** not a standalone prison.
- **It provides hospital-level medical care** for incarcerated men transferred from prisons across the state who need that level of care.
- **A person held here has been transferred for medical care** and may return to their assigned prison.

## Nearby Services

The unit is inside a hospital in Boston, which has gas, food, and lodging nearby. Because the unit is within Lemuel Shattuck Hospital, emergency care is at the hospital itself; other 24-hour emergency rooms operate elsewhere in Boston.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail goes to the prison — Massachusetts does not use an off-site mail vendor. Address it with the person's full name and commitment number and the facility's mailing address (above); all incoming mail is opened and inspected. **Legal mail** is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/massachusetts/guides/mail/).
