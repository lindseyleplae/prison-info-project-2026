## Overview

The Havins Unit is a TDCJ pre-release facility near Brownwood, in Brown County. TDCJ lists it at about 596 beds, and it opened in July 1994. As a pre-release unit, it holds men working toward release, and the population turns over more often than at a long-term general-population prison.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's pre-release role and its location in Central/West Texas, away from the major metro areas.

## What Makes the Havins Unit Different

-   A pre-release facility rather than a general-population prison, so stays are often shorter and a person can transfer out with little notice; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the current assignment
-   Carries a substance-abuse treatment mission, including an In-Prison Therapeutic Community (IPTC) program
-   Operates a faith-based dorm and the GO KIDS family-engagement program
-   Located near Brownwood in Central/West Texas, reached by road from the surrounding region rather than a nearby metro grid

## Visiting

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Havins. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule, so the type of visit can differ from one person to the next on the same weekend. Because this is a pre-release unit, visiting arrangements and any program-tied scheduling are best confirmed directly with the unit before traveling.

## Getting There and Parking

Havins is near Brownwood, in Brown County, in the Central/West Texas region. The unit is reached by road from the surrounding area, and a private vehicle is required.

:::key-info
**From the Dallas-Fort Worth area:** Southwest toward the Brownwood area; often about 2.5-3 hours

**From Austin:** Northwest toward the Brownwood area; often about 2.5-3 hours

**From San Angelo:** East toward Brownwood; often about 1.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Brownwood is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging. There is no fixed-route transit to the unit, so the trip is by car.

## Mail

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
