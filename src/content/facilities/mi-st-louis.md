## Overview

St. Louis Correctional Facility, on N. Croswell Road in St. Louis, is a **higher-security (Level IV) men's prison** in central Michigan on a **67-acre site**, with a **double-fence perimeter and gun towers**. It opened in **1999**. One of its housing units is an **Adaptive Skills Residential Program (ASRP)** unit, and the facility has on-site mini-clinics and telemedicine. It is one of two MDOC prisons in St. Louis; the other, Central Michigan, is a Secure Level I facility on Hubbard Street.

## What Makes St. Louis Different

- **It is a higher-security (Level IV) prison** on a 67-acre site, with a double-fence perimeter and gun towers.
- **One of its housing units is an Adaptive Skills Residential Program (ASRP) unit.**
- **It has on-site mini-clinics and telemedicine.**
- **It is one of two prisons in St. Louis** (the other, Central Michigan, is Secure Level I on Hubbard Street), so confirm which one holds the person.

## Visiting

The statewide MDOC rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at St. Louis. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Scheduling:** All visits are by appointment, through the ViaPath scheduler (midoc.gtlvisitme.com), 48 hours to 7 days ahead

**Approved list:** Each visitor must first be approved on the prisoner's visiting list (apply with form CAJ-103)

**Which facility:** Confirm the exact facility — St. Louis CF is the Level IV prison on Croswell Road, about 1.5 miles from the Secure Level I Central Michigan Correctional Facility on Hubbard Street; the security level, street name, and MDOC number or OTIS record are the cleanest discriminators
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Michigan](/states/michigan/guides/visiting/).

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

St. Louis and nearby Alma have the nearest gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **MyMichigan Medical Center Alma** in Alma, about 6 miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's name and **MDOC number**. Michigan **photocopies incoming mail in black and white and delivers the copy; the original is shredded**, so do not send originals you want kept. Color photos and messages can be sent through JPay. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/michigan/guides/mail/).
