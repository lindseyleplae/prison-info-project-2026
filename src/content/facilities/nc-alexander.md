## Overview

Alexander Correctional Institution is a state prison for men operated by the North Carolina Department of Adult Correction (NCDAC). It is in Taylorsville, the seat of Alexander County, in the foothills of western North Carolina. The facility houses men in close and minimum custody, with a total capacity of about 1,180. It includes a 252-bed minimum-security unit in addition to close-custody single-cell housing.

NCDAC assigns custody level based on factors that include sentence length, time remaining, and conduct, and a person's custody level can change during incarceration. The custody classes held at a given prison determine whether visits are contact or non-contact, so families confirm the arrangement that applies to the specific person before a first visit.

## What Makes Alexander Different

Alexander combines two custody levels on one campus. Most of the prison is close custody — the higher of North Carolina's two security levels for general-population men — with single-cell housing, while a separate 252-bed unit holds men in minimum custody. Because the two populations operate under different rules, the visiting format, program access, and movement that apply to a given person depend on which unit and custody class that person is in. Families confirm those details with the facility rather than assuming they are uniform across the prison.

## Visiting

The statewide NCDAC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Alexander Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** Before any visit, the incarcerated person mails a visiting application to each prospective visitor. Applicants 16 and older include a copy of a government photo ID; minors under 16 include a copy of a birth certificate. Completed forms go to Alexander Correctional Institution.
- **Scheduling:** By appointment only. Call or email the prison to arrange a visit — there is no statewide online scheduler. Confirm the facility's visitation status the day before.
- **Limits:** Generally one visit per week, up to two hours, with up to three approved visitors. Whether a visit is contact or non-contact depends on the person's custody class and disciplinary status.
- **Main line:** 828-848-5300
:::

## Getting There and Parking

Alexander Correctional Institution is at 633 Old Landfill Road in Taylorsville, in Alexander County in the foothills of western North Carolina. Taylorsville is northwest of Statesville and northeast of Hickory; the prison is reached from those cities by way of NC Highway 16 and NC Highway 90. The nearest commercial airport is in the Charlotte metropolitan area to the south.

Parking is on site. Visitors confirm current entry procedures, the visitor-processing location, and what may be brought onto the grounds with the facility before arriving, because items such as phones, smartwatches, cameras, and recording devices are not permitted inside.

## Nearby Services

Taylorsville has limited lodging and dining; a wider range is available in the Hickory and Statesville areas to the south and southeast. Emergency medical care in the region is concentrated in the Hickory area. Visitors traveling a long distance generally find the most options for fuel, food, and overnight stays along the Interstate 40 corridor through Hickory and Statesville.

## Mail

Incoming personal mail does not go to the prison. North Carolina sends personal mail to an off-site vendor, **TextBehind**, which opens and scans it and delivers a digital copy to the incarcerated person's tablet; original letters, cards, and packages are returned to the sender. Address personal mail with the person's full name and **OPUS number** and the full (unabbreviated) prison name to: TextBehind, P.O. Box 247, Phoenix, MD 21131 — and include the sender's full first and last name in the return address. **Legal mail** is exempt and goes directly to the facility. **Books and publications** must come from a publisher or online retailer, shipped to the prison's street address, not the TextBehind box. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/north-carolina/guides/mail/).
