## Overview

Central State Prison, on Fulton Mill Road in Macon, Bibb County, is a **medium-security state prison for men** in central Georgia. The facility holds general-population men and serves as a **Mental Health Level III** site, and it operates a Georgia Correctional Industries garment factory. Capacity is about **1,153**. The prison is in the city of Macon, which is distinct from Macon State Prison — a separate GDC facility in Oglethorpe, in Macon County.

## What Makes Central Different

- **It is in the city of Macon (Bibb County),** not to be confused with Macon State Prison in Oglethorpe (Macon County).
- **It has a Mental Health Level III mission** alongside its general population.
- **It operates a Correctional Industries garment factory** on site.
- **It is in central Georgia,** off the Interstate 75 corridor.

## Visiting

The statewide GDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Central State Prison. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** Saturday, Sunday, and state holidays — confirm the facility's current visiting hours

**Scheduling:** By appointment through GDC's online visitation portal; the visitor must first be approved on the person's list, and requests for a given week are accepted Monday through Wednesday
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Georgia](/states/georgia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Fulton Mill Road in Macon, off the Interstate 75 corridor in central Georgia.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Macon, Bibb County — reached from Interstate 75 through Macon to Fulton Mill Road

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Macon has the closest gas, food, and lodging, with a full range of services in the city. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are at the Macon-area hospitals, including **Atrium Health Navicent The Medical Center** in Macon.

## Mail

Personal mail goes to the facility, addressed with the person's full name and **GDC ID number**. Georgia is moving toward centralized mail handling, so confirm the current mailing address before sending. Books and magazines must come from a publisher or established retailer, and legal mail is opened only in the person's presence. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/georgia/guides/mail/).
