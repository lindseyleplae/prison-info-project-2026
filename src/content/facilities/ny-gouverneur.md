## Overview

Gouverneur Correctional Facility, in Gouverneur, is a **medium-security prison** for men in St. Lawrence County, in the North Country of northern New York. It has a Residential Rehabilitation Unit (RRU) and a Special Housing Unit (SHU), and visiting hours can differ depending on the unit a person is housed in.

## What Makes Gouverneur Different

- **It is a medium-security prison** for men.
- **It has a Residential Rehabilitation Unit (RRU),** which has used a separate weekend evening visiting window.
- **It has a Special Housing Unit (SHU),** where visiting is more limited.
- **It is in the North Country,** in St. Lawrence County — a long drive from downstate New York.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Gouverneur Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** General-population visits are during the facility's posted weekend daytime hours; the RRU has used a separate weekend evening window (about 5:00 PM–9:00 PM) — confirm the person's unit and the current schedule

**SHU:** A person in the Special Housing Unit is limited to one non-legal visit within a seven-day period

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Gouverneur, in St. Lawrence County, in the North Country — a long drive from downstate New York.

:::key-info

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Gouverneur and the surrounding St. Lawrence County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Watertown and Canton/Potsdam areas. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Gouverneur/Canton area, with larger hospitals in Watertown.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
