## Overview

The San Saba Unit is a small TDCJ prison — TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds — near San Saba in San Saba County, in the Texas Hill Country. It opened in June 1992, transferred to the Texas Youth Commission in February 1996, and came under TDCJ operation in September 2007.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's small size and its rural Hill Country location.

## What Makes the San Saba Unit Different

-   A small unit — TDCJ lists it at about 606 beds — holding general-population men at G1-G2 custody
-   Academic programming is provided through Central Texas College, alongside chaplaincy services, a faith-based dormitory, and the GO KIDS Initiative
-   Medical care is delivered partly through telemedicine, reflecting the unit's distance from major hospitals
-   Located in the rural Hill Country of central Texas, a long drive from the state's large metro areas

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at San Saba. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

San Saba holds general-population men at G1-G2 custody, so most visits use standard contact visiting. The format still follows the incarcerated person's classification, so housing or status changes can change how a visit is conducted on a given weekend.

The unit's small size works in a family's favor on movement time: with about 606 beds, the walk from housing to the visiting area is shorter than at a large unit, so the wait between check-in and the start of a visit tends to be brief.

## Getting There and Parking

San Saba is in a rural section of San Saba County, in the Texas Hill Country. The unit sits on South Wallace Creek Road, and services thin out quickly outside the town of San Saba.

:::key-info
**From Austin:** Highways northwest toward the Hill Country and the San Saba area, then local roads to the unit; often about 2-2.5 hours

**From Brownwood:** Routes southeast toward San Saba, then local roads to the unit; often about 60-75 minutes

**From Lampasas:** Highways northwest toward San Saba, then local roads to the unit; often about 45-60 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, and the rural Hill Country setting means the trip is by car. The town of San Saba is the nearest place for gas and food before the final approach.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

San Saba is a small town with limited commercial services. Brownwood to the northwest and the larger Austin area to the southeast are the nearest broader service centers for groceries, lodging, and a wider range of dining. Closer in, the town of San Saba offers basic gas and food before the final stretch to the unit.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
