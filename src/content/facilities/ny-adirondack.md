## Overview

Adirondack Correctional Facility, in Ray Brook, is a **medium-security prison** for men in northern New York. It sits in the Adirondack Mountains in Essex County, in the area between Saranac Lake and Lake Placid.

## What Makes Adirondack Different

- **It is a medium-security prison,** so visiting and movement are less restricted than at a maximum-security facility.
- **It is in a remote part of the Adirondack Mountains,** in the High Peaks region of northern New York.
- **It is between Saranac Lake and Lake Placid,** a long drive from most of the state's population centers.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Adirondack Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Ray Brook, in Essex County, in the Adirondack Mountains of northern New York.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Ray Brook, Essex County, reached from State Route 86 between Saranac Lake and Lake Placid; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Ray Brook and the nearby villages of Saranac Lake and Lake Placid have gas, food, and lodging, with more options across the surrounding Adirondack region. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Saranac Lake and Lake Placid area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
