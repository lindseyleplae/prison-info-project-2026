## Overview

Queensboro Correctional Facility, in Long Island City, is a **minimum-security reentry facility** for men in the borough of Queens — the only DOCCS prison located in New York City. It serves as a re-entry and temporary-release (work-release) facility for men who are minimum-security and approved for a continuous temporary-release program, with a focus on finalizing release plans, employment, and housing for those nearing release. Because most residents are on work release, the day-to-day pattern at the facility differs from a standard prison.

## What Makes Queensboro Different

- **It is the only DOCCS prison in New York City,** in Long Island City, Queens.
- **It is a minimum-security reentry and work-release facility,** not a reception center.
- **Most residents are on work release,** so in-facility visiting differs from a standard prison.
- **Visiting is afternoon-only,** so confirm the current days and hours before traveling.

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Queensboro Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's address. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
