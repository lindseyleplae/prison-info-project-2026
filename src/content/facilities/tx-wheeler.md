## Overview

The J.B. Wheeler Unit is a TDCJ prison near Plainview in Hale County, in the Texas Panhandle/South Plains region. TDCJ lists it at about 576 beds, and the unit opened in March 1995. Medical care is provided through Texas Tech University Health Sciences Center, which serves the western TDCJ region.

Two facts shape much about visiting here: the unit's mixed custody population and its location in a thinly populated part of the Panhandle.

## What Makes Wheeler Unit Different

-   Holds both youthful-offender (J1, J2) and general-population (G1, G2) custody, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Operates a scent-specific canine program
-   Medical care is provided through Texas Tech University Health Sciences Center
-   Shares the town of Plainview with the separately operated Formby State Jail; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, and the two facilities have different addresses and visitation lists

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Wheeler. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Wheeler's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because Plainview is also home to the Formby State Jail, confirming the exact unit on the TDCJ inmate locator before traveling avoids a trip to the wrong facility. The two units have separate addresses and separate visitation lists.

## Getting There and Parking

Wheeler is near Plainview in Hale County, in the Texas Panhandle/South Plains region. The unit is reached by private vehicle, and services thin out away from the Plainview area.

:::key-info
**From Lubbock:** I-27 North toward Plainview, then local roads to the unit; often about 45-60 minutes

**From Amarillo:** I-27 South toward Plainview, then local roads to the unit; often about 1.5 hours

**From Dallas-Fort Worth:** Westbound highways toward the Lubbock/Plainview corridor, then I-27 to Plainview; often about 5-5.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, so the trip is by car. Plainview is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, with more options available in Lubbock to the south.

The nearest hospital is in Plainview. Covenant Health Plainview operates an emergency room in the city; additional hospitals and urgent care are in Lubbock.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Plainview is the nearest service center for gas, food, groceries, and lodging, with chain properties listed in the city and a wider range of options in Lubbock to the south. Services thin out away from the Plainview area.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
