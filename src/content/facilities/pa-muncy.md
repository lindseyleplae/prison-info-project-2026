## Overview

SCI Muncy, on Route 405 in Clinton Township near Muncy, is **Pennsylvania's diagnostic and classification center for women** — the statewide intake point where all newly committed women are received, evaluated (medical, mental health, security level, and programming), and then assigned and transferred to a permanent State Correctional Institution. Because of that role, a woman may be at Muncy only briefly, and the prison shown in the state locator can change within the first weeks or months. It is a **medium/maximum-security** facility that also houses a general-population women's population, holding about **1,055 women** (capacity about 1,643). Opened in 1953, it is the oldest women's institution in the Pennsylvania system. Muncy is also the designated facility for any woman under a death sentence in Pennsylvania, though no women are currently under a death sentence in the state.

## What Makes Muncy Different

- **It is the women's intake and classification center,** so a newly committed woman usually begins here before transferring to a permanent prison.
- **Visiting is limited during the reception period,** so confirm the person's status and location before traveling.
- **It is the oldest women's institution in the state system,** opened in 1953.
- **It is the designated facility for any woman under a death sentence,** though no women are currently under a death sentence in Pennsylvania.

## Visiting

The statewide PADOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Muncy. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** All visits (in person and video) are reserved through the Inmate Visitation System (inmatevisitation.cor.pa.gov); the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Reception status:** A person in reception has limited visiting and will transfer to a permanent prison after classification

**Schedule:** Muncy sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Pennsylvania](/states/pennsylvania/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Route 405 in Clinton Township near Muncy, about 15 miles southeast of Williamsport.

:::key-info
**By car:** From Williamsport, about 15 miles southeast via Interstate 180 to the Muncy area, then Route 405 to the facility

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The town of Muncy and the nearby Williamsport area have gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **UPMC Muncy**, in the town of Muncy; **UPMC Williamsport**, about 15 miles away, is the nearest trauma center.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Pennsylvania routes incoming letters, cards, and photos to an **off-site Smart Communications ("MailGuard") center in Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person as photocopies; address them with the person's name and inmate number. **Legal mail** goes directly to the institution and is opened in the person's presence, and **books** must come from a publisher or approved distributor through the Security Processing Center. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/pennsylvania/guides/mail/).
