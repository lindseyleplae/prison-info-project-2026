## Overview

Marcy Correctional Facility, in Marcy, is a **medium-security prison** for men in Oneida County, near Utica. It is one of DOCCS' mediums with a **mental-health treatment mission**.

## What Makes Marcy Different

- **It has a mental-health mission,** with a **Residential/Regional Mental Health Unit associated with the Central New York Psychiatric Center.** A person may be at Marcy for mental-health care.
- **It is a medium-security prison,** so visiting and movement follow the standard medium-security rules.
- **It is in central New York,** in Oneida County near Utica.
- **It is a separate prison from the adjacent Mid-State Correctional Facility,** which is also in Marcy on the same campus area — confirm the exact facility before traveling.

## Visiting

The statewide DOCCS rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Marcy Correctional Facility. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be on the incarcerated person's approved visitor list before visiting

**Hours:** Visits are within the facility's posted hours; some visits require advance scheduling — confirm the current schedule

**Screening:** A body scanner is required for a full-contact visit (a medical exemption can be requested)
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in New York](/states/new-york/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Marcy, in Oneida County, near Utica in central New York.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Marcy, Oneida County, near Utica off Old River Road; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no direct public-transit service to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Marcy and the surrounding Oneida County area have gas, food, and lodging, with more in the Utica area to the southeast. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Utica area.

## Mail

New York does not use an off-site mail vendor — incoming personal mail is **opened and photocopied at the facility**, and the incarcerated person receives the **photocopies** (images may also reach their JPay tablet) rather than the original letters. Address it with the person's **name and DIN (Department Identification Number)** and the facility's incarcerated-mail address: **P.O. Box 3600, Marcy, NY 13403**. **Legal mail** goes to the facility and is handled under privileged-mail rules. **Books, publications, and packages** must be ordered from **approved vendors**. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/new-york/guides/mail/).
