## Overview

Trumbull Correctional Institution (TCI), in Leavittsburg, Trumbull County, in northeast Ohio, is a **men's prison** spanning **minimum to close security**. It holds about **1,500** men and opened in **1992**. It has an attached **minimum-security camp** (the Trumbull Correctional Camp), which is a sub-unit of the institution rather than a separate prison.

## What Makes TCI Different

- **It spans minimum to close security,** holding men across a range of custody levels.
- **It has an attached minimum-security camp,** the Trumbull Correctional Camp, which is a sub-unit of the institution rather than a separate prison.
- **It opened in 1992** and holds about 1,500 men.
- **It is in Leavittsburg, in northeast Ohio,** west of Warren.

## Visiting

The statewide ODRC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Trumbull Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** Visits are reserved in advance through ViaPath; the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** TCI sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Leavittsburg, west of Warren in northeast Ohio.

:::key-info
**By car:** Reached via State Route 5 and Interstate 80, west of Warren in northeast Ohio; a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Warren has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Trumbull Regional Medical Center** in Warren, about 8 miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
