## Overview

Florida State Prison, near Raiford in Bradford County, north-central Florida, is a **maximum-security prison for men**. It houses the state's **execution chamber** and **death-row cells**. Because custody levels and housing differ across the prison, the visiting arrangements that apply to a particular person depend on that person's unit.

## What Makes Florida State Prison Different

- **It is a maximum-security prison for men,** so visiting can be more restricted than at lower-custody institutions.
- **It houses the state execution chamber and death-row cells.**
- **Some housing is non-contact,** and the rules differ by unit, so confirm the person's unit before traveling.
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Visiting

The statewide FDC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Florida State Prison. The institution's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Approved list:** Every visitor must be approved on the person's visitor list before visiting (the person starts the application, Form DC6-111A, at their institution)

**By unit:** Visiting for men in death-row or maximum-custody housing may be non-contact and differs by unit — confirm the person's unit and the current rules

**Reservations:** Many Florida institutions require an advance visit reservation — confirm the current days, hours, and how to reserve
:::

The full visitor process is in [Visiting in Florida](/states/florida/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is near Raiford, reached via State Road 16 in north-central Florida, in the Raiford prison cluster.

:::key-info
**By car:** In Bradford County near Raiford, reached via State Road 16, between Lake City and Gainesville

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

The Raiford area has limited services; Lake City and Gainesville, each within driving distance, have the full range of gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in the Lake Butler, Lake City, and Gainesville areas.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
