## Overview

The McPherson Unit, in Newport, is **Arkansas's main state prison for women** and the **statewide women's reception and intake center** — every woman entering the ADC is processed here first. It holds women at **all custody levels**, including the state's **women's death row**, and women sentenced to **life or life without parole are assigned here permanently**. ADC lists a capacity of about **1,050**; it does not publish a current count. ADC also operates a second women's facility, the **Hawkins Unit** in Wrightsville, where some women serve their initial assignment, so McPherson is the main and intake women's prison but not the only one. McPherson sits in the same Newport complex as the men's **Grimes Unit** (a separate prison next door); both opened in 1998.

## What Makes McPherson Different

- **It is the statewide women's reception center,** so a newly sentenced woman is processed here first.
- **It holds women at all custody levels,** including the state's women's death row.
- **Women serving life sentences are assigned here permanently.**
- **It shares a complex with the men's Grimes Unit,** so confirm the person is at McPherson (302 Corrections Drive), not Grimes (300 Corrections Drive).

## Visiting

The statewide ADC rules above — the approved visitor list, the no-white dress code, ID, and pre-scheduled visits — apply at McPherson. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Days:** Saturdays and Sundays for routine visits; how many visits a month depends on the inmate's class

**Reception:** A newly admitted woman in intake may have limited visiting at first — confirm with the unit

**Scheduling:** Request a visit through the TeleGov online portal, or call the unit's Visitation Clerk at (870) 523-2639

**List:** The visitor must be approved on the inmate's list first (the inmate mails out the application form)
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Arkansas](/states/arkansas/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The unit is on Corrections Drive, off Arkansas Highway 384, about 4 miles northeast of Newport.

:::key-info
**By car:** From Little Rock, about 100 miles north on U.S. Highway 67 to Exit 83 (Highway 384), then about 2.5 miles; Jonesboro is roughly 40–45 miles to the northeast

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the unit; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Newport, about 4 miles away, has the nearest restaurants and lodging; larger services are in Jonesboro. The nearest hospital emergency room is **Unity Health – Harris Medical Center** in Newport, the closest 24-hour ER to the unit.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the unit. Since September 2025, ADC routes non-legal personal letters and photos to an **off-site scanning center in Tampa, Florida**, addressed with the person's name and **ADC number**; the person receives the mail electronically. **Legal mail, books, magazines, and packages** still go directly to the unit at 302 Corrections Drive. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arkansas/guides/mail/).

## A Note on History

McPherson opened in **1998** and was originally run under private contract before returning to state operation. From **2002 to 2007**, McPherson and the neighboring Grimes Unit were the subject of a **U.S. Department of Justice investigation** under the Civil Rights of Institutionalized Persons Act; Arkansas entered a settlement in 2004, and the case closed in 2007 after the facilities were accredited. This is documented in the sources cited above.
