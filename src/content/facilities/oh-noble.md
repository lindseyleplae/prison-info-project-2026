## Overview

Noble Correctional Institution (NCI), on McConnelsville Road in Caldwell, Noble County, is a **minimum- and medium-security men's prison** in southeastern Ohio. It holds about **2,500** men and opened in **1996**.

## What Makes NCI Different

- **It is a minimum- and medium-security prison** for men in southeastern Ohio.
- **It holds about 2,500 men.**
- **It opened in 1996.**

## Visiting

The statewide ODRC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Noble Correctional Institution. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**Reservations:** Visits are reserved in advance through ViaPath; the visitor must first be on the person's approved list

**Schedule:** NCI sets its own visiting days and hours — confirm them with the facility before traveling
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Ohio](/states/ohio/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is in Caldwell, in southeastern Ohio.

:::key-info
**By car:** Reached via Interstate 77 in southeastern Ohio; Cambridge is the nearest larger city, about 20 miles north

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the prison; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Cambridge has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Southeastern Ohio Regional Medical Center** in Cambridge, about 20 miles away.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
