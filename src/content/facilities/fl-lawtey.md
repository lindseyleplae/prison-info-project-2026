## Overview

Lawtey Correctional Institution is a **faith- and character-based men's prison** in Lawtey, in Bradford County, in north Florida. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections, holds men in mixed custody, and has a capacity of about 832. A satellite work camp in Gainesville is operated under the institution.

## What Makes Lawtey Different

- **It is one of the nation's earliest entirely faith- and character-based prisons** and the flagship faith- and character-based institution in Florida, with a large volunteer corps and extensive self-betterment and faith programming.
- **It offers post-secondary education** through the Ashland University Second Chance Pell program.
- **It runs vocational tracks,** including HVAC, plumbing, architectural drafting, NCCER core construction, and OSHA 10.
- **It operates a satellite work camp in Gainesville.**

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
