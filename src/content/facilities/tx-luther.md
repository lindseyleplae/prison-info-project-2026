## Overview

The Luther Unit is a general-population men's prison near Navasota in Grimes County, established in July 1982. TDCJ lists its capacity at about 1,316 beds, and it houses G1 through G3 custody plus outside trusty status.

The unit shares its grounds and a combined agricultural operation with the neighboring Wallace Pack Unit, a separate prison on the same property. Because the two units sit together, the most important step before traveling is confirming which unit a person is assigned to on the TDCJ inmate locator — Luther and Pack keep separate visitation lists and are distinct facilities.

## What Makes Luther Unit Different

-   Co-located with the Wallace Pack Unit, with which it shares a combined farming operation; the two are separate prisons, so the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to
-   Operates a working farm — beef cattle, field crops, swine, and a buffalo ranch — along with horse and canine units
-   Runs a stainless steel manufacturing plant on site
-   Houses a 12-bed assisted-living infirmary among its medical services

## Operations and Programs

The Luther Unit functions as a working prison farm. Agricultural operations include a cow-calf herd, field crops, swine finishing, and a buffalo ranch, supported by horse and canine units. The unit also operates a stainless steel manufacturing plant.

Medical services include ambulatory care, dental, and 24-hour mental health coverage, with a 12-bed assisted-living infirmary on site. Education and vocational programs include adult basic education, GED, special education, automotive, electrical trades, welding, and cognitive intervention. The unit also runs a faith-based dormitory, reentry planning, and chaplaincy services.

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Luther Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Because Luther shares its grounds with the Wallace Pack Unit, the unit on the visitation list must match the unit where the person is actually housed. The two prisons maintain separate visiting lists, so confirming the assignment on the TDCJ inmate locator before scheduling avoids arriving at the wrong unit.

The visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive status can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend.

## Getting There and Parking

The Luther Unit is near Navasota in Grimes County, between Houston to the southeast and Bryan-College Station to the northwest. The drive uses highways for most of the route, with a final approach on local roads.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** Northwest highways toward the Navasota area, then local roads to the unit; often about 1.5 hours

**From Bryan-College Station:** Southeast toward Navasota, then local roads; often about 30-45 minutes

**From Austin:** East toward the Brenham and Navasota area, then local roads; often about 2-2.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Navasota is the nearest town for gas, food, and groceries before the unit. Bryan-College Station, about a half hour northwest, has a wider range of hotels, restaurants, and medical services, and Houston is the larger service center to the southeast.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
