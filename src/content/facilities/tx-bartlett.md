## Overview

The Bartlett Innovation Unit is a TDCJ men's prison in Williamson County, in Central Texas between Austin and Temple. TDCJ lists it at about 1,049 beds. The building first opened in October 1995 as the privately operated Bartlett State Jail under CoreCivic (Corrections Corporation of America) and closed in June 2017 during a statewide population decline. TDCJ reopened it in October 2024 under direct state management as the Bartlett Innovation Unit.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's reentry-focused program model and its Central Texas location.

## What Makes the Bartlett Innovation Unit Different

-   TDCJ's flagship "Innovation Unit," organized around evidence-based rehabilitation and reentry rather than a single custody mission
-   Anchored by THRIVE (Truth, Healing, Restoration, Independence, Vision and Empowerment), a trauma-informed reentry program with career-skill development and post-release supervision
-   Vocational training developed with the Windham School District, including culinary arts, computer programming, veterinary technician, renewable energy, and electrical lineman tracks
-   A Veteran X peer-mentoring track for incarcerated veterans, alongside faith-based and peer-support housing tracks

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Bartlett. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

Visiting format here follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. Two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

Because the unit reopened recently and runs a program-driven model, families should confirm current visiting arrangements with the unit before traveling. The TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, which is the first thing to check before scheduling.

## Getting There and Parking

Bartlett sits in Central Texas between Austin and Temple. The town straddles the Williamson and Bell county line, and TDCJ assigns the unit to Williamson County.

:::key-info
**From Austin:** North on I-35 toward Georgetown, then state and farm-to-market roads east toward Bartlett; often about 60-75 minutes

**From Temple:** South and west connectors toward Bartlett; often about 30-40 minutes

**From the Dallas-Fort Worth area:** I-35 South toward the Temple area, then local roads into Bartlett; often about 2.5-3 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle. Plan for the final approach on local roads, where services thin out near the unit.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
