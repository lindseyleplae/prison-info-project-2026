## Overview

The Glossbrenner Unit is a state facility operated by TDCJ as a Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF). A SAFPF is an intensive, court-ordered in-prison treatment program for people whose offenses are tied to substance use; participants are placed there to complete structured treatment rather than to serve a standard general-population term. TDCJ lists the unit at about 612 beds. It opened in December 1994 and sits near San Diego in Duval County, in South Texas.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's treatment-program structure and its rural South Texas location.

## What Makes It Different

-   Run as a Substance Abuse Felony Punishment Facility (SAFPF) — an intensive substance-abuse treatment program, not a standard general-population prison
-   Houses an In-Prison Therapeutic Community (IPTC), where daily life and movement are organized around the treatment regimen
-   Includes a faith-based dorm and the GO KIDS family-focused programming
-   Because it is a treatment program, the daily schedule and visiting access can follow the program phase, so the experience may differ from a general-population unit

## Visiting

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at Glossbrenner. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
:::

As a treatment program, the Glossbrenner Unit can organize visiting differently than a general-population prison. Treatment programs often phase visiting to the stage of treatment, which means access may be limited early in the program and may change as a participant progresses. The posted weekend schedule is the starting point, but the program phase can determine whether, when, and how a visit happens — so families should confirm the current rules with the unit before traveling.

## Getting There and Parking

The Glossbrenner Unit is in a rural section of Duval County, off FM 1329 near San Diego in South Texas. The drive starts on highways but finishes on farm-to-market roads, and services thin out over the last stretch. A private vehicle is required.

:::key-info
**From Corpus Christi:** West toward Alice, then north and west toward San Diego and the FM 1329 approach; often about 1-1.5 hours

**From San Antonio:** South toward the Alice and San Diego area, then the final FM-road approach; often about 2-2.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route public transit to the unit, so the trip is by car.

## Mail

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
