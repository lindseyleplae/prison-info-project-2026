## Overview

The Sanders Estes Unit is a TDCJ pre-release facility for men, listed at about 1,040 beds. It sits near Venus in Johnson County, south of the Fort Worth and Dallas-Fort Worth area. TDCJ classifies it as a Pre-Release Facility holding G1 and G2 custody, and the unit now operates under direct state management after years of private operation.

Two facts shape visiting here: the pre-release mission, which means the population turns over as people approach parole or release, and the unit's location south of the metroplex.

## What Makes the Estes Unit Different

-   A pre-release unit for men, oriented toward reentry programming, including vocational training in building trades, electrical trades, food service and culinary arts, computer technology, and commercial housekeeping
-   Houses G1 and G2 custody, so visiting follows standard pre-release procedures rather than a high-security format
-   Now TDCJ state-operated after years under a private contractor; the official unit page lists it under direct state operation
-   Located near Venus, south of the Fort Worth/DFW area, reached by private vehicle

## Visiting

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Estes Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.

Because this is a pre-release unit, the population is generally made up of people within range of parole or release, and placements can change as people move toward release. A person shown at the Estes Unit on the locator at one point may be reassigned, so the current unit assignment is the thing to confirm before a visit. General-population pre-release housing typically uses standard contact visiting, and visits are arranged through TDCJ's statewide online portal.

## Getting There and Parking

The Estes Unit is near Venus in Johnson County, south of Fort Worth and the broader Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex. The trip is by private vehicle.

:::key-info
**From Fort Worth:** South toward the Venus area in Johnson County; often about 35-50 minutes

**From Dallas:** Southwest toward the Venus area; often about 50-70 minutes

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route transit to the unit, so the trip is by car. Venus is a small community, and the nearest larger service centers for gas, food, and lodging are in the surrounding Johnson County and southern Dallas-Fort Worth area.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).
