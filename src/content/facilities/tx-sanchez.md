## Overview

The Rogelio Sanchez State Jail is a men's state jail run by TDCJ in El Paso, in the far western corner of Texas. TDCJ lists it at about 1,100 beds. It opened in February 1996 and sits on the east side of El Paso off Interstate 10, near Loop 375.

State jails hold people sentenced to state-jail felonies and also serve a transfer function, so the population turns over and a person can move to another unit. Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's distance from the rest of Texas and the time zone it keeps.

## What Makes the Sanchez Unit Different

-   Located in El Paso, in the far western tip of Texas — a long drive from the rest of the state, and far enough that families traveling more than 300 miles may request a special visit under TDCJ's distance rule
-   Unlike almost all of TDCJ, El Paso observes **Mountain Time**, one hour behind the Central Time used across most of Texas; posted visiting hours and scheduler times should be confirmed as Mountain or Central before planning a trip
-   A men's state jail with mixed state-jail and general-population custody, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Rogelio Sanchez State Jail. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:00 p.m.

**Time zone:** El Paso is on Mountain Time, one hour behind most of Texas. Confirm whether posted hours and scheduler times are Mountain or Central before traveling.
:::

TDCJ does not publish unit-specific visiting hours for each facility, so visits run on the statewide schedule and are arranged through the TDCJ Online Visitation Portal. Because El Paso keeps Mountain Time while the rest of Texas keeps Central Time, a posted or scheduled start time can be read an hour off; families coming from Central-Time areas should verify the zone when they book.

The unit's custody mix is the other variable. State-jail and general-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

The Rogelio Sanchez State Jail is on the far east side of El Paso, in El Paso County, near the western corner of Texas. El Paso runs on Mountain Time, so build the time difference into any trip planned from elsewhere in Texas.

:::key-info
**From within El Paso:** The unit is off Interstate 10 east near Loop 375, on the city's far east side; a short drive from most of the El Paso metro

**From San Antonio:** Interstate 10 West across West Texas; often a full-day drive of roughly 8-9 hours, crossing into Mountain Time near the Pecos area

**From Dallas-Fort Worth:** West and southwest across the state toward El Paso; often roughly 9-10 hours, also crossing into Mountain Time

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

Because El Paso is far from most of Texas, many families fall under TDCJ's over-300-mile provision, which allows a request for a longer special visit; that process is handled through the unit and is covered in [Visiting in Texas](/states/texas/guides/visiting/).

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

El Paso is a large metro area, so gas, food, groceries, and lodging are widely available across the city, including near Interstate 10 on the east side closer to the unit. Hotel sites list a range of chain properties throughout El Paso.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
