## Overview

Mabel Bassett Correctional Center, on Kickapoo Road in McLoud, Pottawatomie County, is one of Oklahoma's two state prisons for women. Oklahoma DOC operates it as the statewide reception and assessment center for all incoming women, a general-population women's prison across all custody levels, and the women's medical and mental-health hub. It holds women from minimum through maximum security and runs the standard Oklahoma DOC programming — education, work assignments, and treatment — alongside its reception and health-care functions.

## What Makes Mabel Bassett Correctional Center Different

- **It is the only women's reception and assessment center in the state.** Every woman entering Oklahoma state custody is processed at Mabel Bassett first, with assessment typically taking about 10 to 30 days (the facility has roughly 102 reception beds), after which she either remains here or transfers to another facility.
- **It is the women's medical and mental-health hub.** Inpatient and specialty care for incarcerated women is centered here.
- **A woman under a death sentence is held here.** Mabel Bassett is the designated facility for a woman under a death sentence in Oklahoma; men's death row and the state execution chamber are at the Oklahoma State Penitentiary in McAlester. There is no execution chamber at Mabel Bassett.
- **It holds women across all custody levels,** minimum through maximum, in McLoud, Pottawatomie County.

## Visiting

The statewide Oklahoma DOC rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at Mabel Bassett Correctional Center. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
**List:** Every visitor must first be approved on the inmate's visiting list — submit a Visitor Application Form (online or by mail to the Visitation Unit, P.O. Box 11400, Oklahoma City, OK 73136-0400) and pass a background check (ages 18+); approval can take up to 90 days

**Scheduling:** Schedule through the statewide Visitation Unit at 405-768-3269; all visits must be scheduled in advance — no walk-ins — with requests received by Wednesday at 1 p.m. for that period

**Before traveling:** Confirm the person's current facility and visitation status before traveling
:::

Because Mabel Bassett is the women's reception and assessment center, a woman who has just arrived may still be in intake processing, and higher-security or restrictive housing may limit visits early on — confirm a woman's visitation status before traveling. The full approval process is in [Visiting in Oklahoma](/states/oklahoma/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Kickapoo Road in McLoud, Pottawatomie County, east of Oklahoma City.

:::key-info
**By car:** McLoud, Pottawatomie County, in central Oklahoma, roughly 30 miles east of Oklahoma City; access is from the Oklahoma City metro area via Interstate 40 and state roads

**By transit:** McLoud is a small community with no practical public transit to the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach the prison
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

McLoud and the nearby Shawnee and Oklahoma City areas have gas, food, and lodging. The nearest full-service hospital emergency rooms are in the **Shawnee and Oklahoma City areas**.

## Mail

Oklahoma DOC routes incoming **personal mail** through an off-site digital vendor. As of September 1, 2024, letters and photos are mailed to the Securus Digital Mail Center in Dallas, where they are scanned and delivered to the inmate as a digital color image on a Securus tablet or kiosk — the original is not delivered. Inmates without device access get a free black-and-white printout. Address personal mail exactly as:

> Inmate's Full Name + ODOC Number
> Securus Digital Mail Center - Okla. Dept. of Corrections
> P.O. Box 223566
> Dallas, TX 75222-3566

Illegible or incorrectly addressed mail may be returned or delayed.

**Legal mail and special correspondence** are *not* sent to the digital center — they go directly to the facility and are handled under the rules for privileged mail. **Publications** (magazines, newspapers, books) also go to the facility and must come directly from the publisher or an approved vendor. **Packages** cannot be sent to the digital mail center and must go to the facility.
