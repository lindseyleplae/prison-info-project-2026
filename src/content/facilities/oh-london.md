## Overview

London Correctional Institution (LoCI), in London, Madison County, central Ohio, is one of **Ohio's oldest prisons**. It is a **men's prison spanning minimum to close security** and holds about **2,500 men**. It opened in the **1920s**, beginning as the London Prison Farm.

## What Makes London Different

- **It is one of Ohio's oldest prisons,** opened in the 1920s; it began as the London Prison Farm.
- **It spans minimum to close security** and holds about 2,500 men.
- **It is a separate prison from the nearby Madison Correctional Institution,** also in London — confirm which one holds the person before traveling.
- **It is west of Columbus,** reached via Interstate 70 and State Route 56.

## Nearby Services

London has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is **Madison Health** in London.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Ohio routes incoming letters and cards to the off-site **ODRC Mail Processing Center** in Youngstown, where they are opened, scanned, and delivered to the person electronically on a ViaPath tablet; address them with the person's name and **ID number**. **Legal mail, publications** (publisher- or retailer-only books, magazines, and newspapers), **and packages** still go directly to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/ohio/guides/mail/).
