## Overview

The J. Aaron Hawkins Sr. Center for Women, in Wrightsville about 10 miles south of Little Rock, is **Arkansas's women's center** and the place where the state provides **care for pregnant incarcerated women** — prenatal and postnatal care, with deliveries at UAMS, through a maternal-health program. It is part of the **Wrightsville/Hawkins complex**, next to the men's Wrightsville Unit. The same campus is **physically divided**: a women's side and a separate, walled-off men's side, so families should confirm which side holds the person. ADC currently lists about 212 beds; capacity figures have varied, so confirm the current count.

## What Makes Hawkins Different

- **It is Arkansas's women's center** and the state's care site for **pregnant incarcerated women**.
- **The campus also holds a separate men's unit,** divided from the women's side by a wall, so it is mixed-gender on one site — confirm which side a person is on.
- **It is near Little Rock,** more accessible than the women's prison at Newport (McPherson).
- Arkansas has also been opening a separate Little Rock health unit for women (the Dr. Mary L. Parker-Reed Women's Health Unit), so where a pregnant or medically-fragile woman is held may change — confirm with the locator.

## Visiting

The statewide ADC rules above — the approved visitor list, the no-white dress code, ID, and pre-scheduled visits — apply at Hawkins.

:::key-info
**Days:** Saturdays and Sundays for routine visits; how many visits a month depends on the inmate's class

**Scheduling:** Request a visit through the TeleGov online portal, or call the unit's Visitation Clerk

**Which side:** The campus has a women's side and a separate men's side — confirm which one holds the person, and the visiting day, before traveling

**Hours:** ADC does not post the unit's specific visiting hours online — call (501) 897-2256 to confirm
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in Arkansas](/states/arkansas/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The center is in Wrightsville, off Highway 365 south of Little Rock, next to the Wrightsville Unit.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 10 miles south of Little Rock; reachable from Interstate 530 and U.S. 365

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the unit; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Little Rock, about 10 miles north, has the fullest range of hotels, restaurants, and services. The nearest 24-hour emergency rooms are in Little Rock — **Baptist Health Medical Center–Little Rock** and **UAMS Medical Center** (UAMS is also where Hawkins's pregnant residents deliver).

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the unit. Since September 2025, ADC routes non-legal personal letters and photos to an **off-site scanning center in Tampa, Florida**, addressed with the person's name and **ADC number**; the person receives the mail electronically. **Legal mail, books, and magazines** still go directly to the unit at the Wrightsville address. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/arkansas/guides/mail/).
