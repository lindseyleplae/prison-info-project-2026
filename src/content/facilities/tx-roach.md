## Overview

The T. L. Roach Unit is a TDCJ prison in the Texas Panhandle, listed by TDCJ at about 1,384 beds on a site of roughly 1,651 acres. It sits in Childress County near Childress, in the far-northwest corner of the state.

Two facts shape almost everything about visiting here: the unit's remote Panhandle location and its mixed custody population.

## What Makes the Roach Unit Different

-   Houses the Special Alternative Incarceration Program (SAIP), a boot-camp program for men
-   Serves as the Region V (Panhandle) maintenance headquarters and operates a soap and detergent factory and distribution center, along with scent-specific and human-remains-detection canine programs
-   Houses a G1, G2, and G4 custody mix, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule
-   Sits in the far-northwest Texas Panhandle, a long drive from the major metros, which is the central visiting factor for most families

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Roach Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

## Getting There and Parking

The Roach Unit is in a rural section of Childress County, in the Texas Panhandle, off FM 164 about two miles southwest of Highway 287. The remote Panhandle location is the defining travel factor: the unit is a long drive from the major metros.

:::key-info
**From Amarillo:** Southeast on Highway 287 toward Childress, then the FM 164 approach; often about 2 hours

**From Dallas-Fort Worth:** Northwest on Highway 287 toward Childress, then the FM 164 approach; often about 4-4.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

The distance from both Dallas-Fort Worth and Amarillo is the main planning consideration. There is no fixed-route public transit to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle, and families traveling from either metro often plan around the drive time.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Childress is the nearest service town for gas, food, groceries, and lodging before the unit, and commercial options thin out on the final farm-to-market approach. Families traveling from a distance generally plan fuel and meal stops in Childress.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
