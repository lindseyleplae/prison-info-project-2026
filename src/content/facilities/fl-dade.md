## Overview

Dade Correctional Institution is a **men's state prison** in Florida City, Miami-Dade County, in the Miami-Dade area of South Florida. It is operated by the Florida Department of Corrections and holds men across **minimum, medium, and close custody**, with a major mental-health mission.

## What Makes Dade Different

- **It has a major mental-health mission.** Dade operates the **Transitional Care Unit (TCU)**, described as the largest inpatient mental-health unit in the Florida prison system.
- **A person may be placed there for psychiatric care.** Because of the mental-health mission, treatment or medical status can affect when and how a visit happens — confirm the current schedule before traveling.
- **It is distinct from nearby facilities.** Dade CI (men) is a separate institution from the neighboring **Homestead Correctional Institution** (women) in the same area, and from the **Miami-Dade county jail system** (Miami-Dade Corrections and Rehabilitation).
- **The institution shown in the offender search can change** if the person is transferred.

## Mail

Florida does not deliver original personal mail to the prison. Routine personal letters and photos go to an off-site **Smart Communications** processing center, where they are **opened and scanned** and delivered electronically — the person reads them on a tablet or kiosk, and the mailroom may print copies. Address routine mail with the person's **full name and DC number**, to: **Smart Communications, P.O. Box 23608, Tampa, FL 33623**. Confirm the current address and format on FDC's website before sending. **Legal mail and privileged correspondence go directly to the institution**, not to the scanning center. **Publications** must come from an approved publisher or retailer. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/florida/guides/mail/).
