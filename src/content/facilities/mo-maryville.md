## Overview

Maryville Treatment Center, on U.S. Highway 136 in Maryville, is a **minimum-security substance-use treatment center for men** in northwestern Missouri. It is one of the smaller institutions in the Missouri DOC and operates a structured substance-use treatment program alongside its housing units. Because it is a treatment facility, placement is tied to program assignment, and people are transferred as their treatment and classification change.

## What Makes Maryville Different

- **It is a minimum-security substance-use treatment center** for men in northwestern Missouri.
- **It is one of the smaller institutions in the Missouri DOC,** in Nodaway County near the Iowa line.
- **Placement is tied to the treatment program,** so people are transferred as their treatment and classification change.

## Getting There and Parking

The facility is on U.S. Highway 136 in Maryville, in Nodaway County in northwestern Missouri, near the Iowa line.

:::key-info
**By car:** Maryville is in northwestern Missouri, reached via U.S. 71 and U.S. 136; it is a long drive from most of the state, and a private vehicle is required

**By transit:** There is no practical public transit to the facility; a private vehicle is required
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Maryville has gas, food, and lodging. The nearest 24-hour emergency room is at **Mosaic Medical Center–Maryville**, in Maryville.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since 2022, Missouri routes incoming letters and photos to an **off-site Securus digital-mail center in Tampa, Florida**, where they are scanned and delivered to the person's **tablet**; address them to **[person's name and DOC ID#], C/O Digital Mail Center–Missouri DOC, P.O. Box 25678, Tampa, FL 33622-5678**. **Legal mail** still goes directly to the prison, and **books** come publisher-direct to the facility. Full rules are in [Mail & Packages](/states/missouri/guides/mail/).
