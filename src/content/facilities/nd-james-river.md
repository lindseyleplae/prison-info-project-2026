## Overview

James River Correctional Center is a state prison for men operated by the North Dakota Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation (ND DOCR). It is in Jamestown, the seat of Stutsman County, in southeastern North Dakota. The facility houses men in medium and minimum custody.

ND DOCR assigns custody level based on factors that include sentence length, time remaining, and conduct, and a resident's custody level can change during incarceration. The custody class and housing unit held at a given prison affect visiting arrangements, so families confirm the arrangement that applies to the specific person before a first visit.

## What Makes the James River Correctional Center Different

James River Correctional Center opened in 1998 and is located on the grounds of the North Dakota State Hospital in Jamestown. It holds men in medium and minimum custody. The facility includes the James River Minimum Unit (JRMU) as a sub-unit; JRMU is part of James River Correctional Center rather than a separate prison, and it shares the facility's address and main phone line. Newly sentenced men enter the North Dakota system at the North Dakota State Penitentiary in Bismarck, where reception and orientation take place, before classification and any assignment to James River.

## Visiting

The statewide ND DOCR rules above — the approved visitor list, the dress code, ID, and item limits — apply at the James River Correctional Center. The facility's own arrangements:

:::key-info
- **Approved list first:** Before any visit, each visitor must apply and be approved through the ND DOCR visitor application. The application is electronic or a printable PDF, and processing can take up to 15 business days once the facility receives it. Visitor names are checked with the North Dakota Bureau of Criminal Investigation, North Dakota Parole and Probation, and local law enforcement.
- **Scheduling:** Visiting days and hours are set by the facility — North Dakota does not use a statewide visit scheduler. Confirm the current schedule directly with the prison before traveling.
- **ID:** Official photo identification is required for everyone entering the facility except those under 16 who are supervised by a parent or guardian.
- **Main line:** 701-253-3660
:::

## Getting There and Parking

James River Correctional Center is at 2521 Circle Drive in Jamestown, in Stutsman County in southeastern North Dakota. Jamestown is along Interstate 94 between Bismarck to the west and Fargo to the east. The facility is on the North Dakota State Hospital grounds. The nearest large commercial airports are in the Bismarck and Fargo areas.

Parking is on site. Visitors confirm current entry procedures, the visitor-processing location, and what may be brought onto the grounds with the facility before arriving, because electronic devices and personal items are generally not permitted inside — visitors may typically bring only identification, a vehicle key, and a small amount of coins or small bills for vending machines.

## Nearby Services

Jamestown has lodging, dining, and fuel, with additional options along the Interstate 94 corridor and a wider range in the Bismarck and Fargo areas. Emergency medical care is available locally, with larger hospitals in the Bismarck and Fargo areas. Visitors traveling a long distance generally find the most options for fuel, food, and overnight stays along Interstate 94.

## Mail

North Dakota DOCR routes incoming **personal mail** through an off-site scanning vendor. Letters, cards, photos, and drawings sent to a resident are delivered to the Securus Digital Mail Center, where they are opened and scanned; the resident receives a digital copy on their Securus tablet (the "player") rather than the original paper. Address personal mail with the resident's full committed name and ID number at:

> Resident's Full Name and ID Number
> James River Correctional Center
> 2521 Circle Drive
> Jamestown, ND 58401

**Legal mail, court and agency correspondence, documents requiring a signature, and visitation applications** are not scanned — they go directly to the facility. **Publications** must be new and shipped directly from an approved publisher or vendor. Confirm the current mailing address and any package rules with the facility before sending anything, because mail-handling vendors and addresses change.
