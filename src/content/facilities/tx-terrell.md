## Overview

The C.T. Terrell Unit is a TDCJ prison in Brazoria County, near Rosharon, about an hour south of Houston. TDCJ lists it at roughly 1,603 beds, divided between a main unit and a smaller Trusty Camp. It opened in 1983 and operates a large agricultural program on the surrounding farmland.

The unit shares the Rosharon farm area with two other TDCJ prisons — the Ramsey and Stringfellow Units — so its name and location are easy to confuse with theirs. The TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, which determines both the drive and the visitation list.

One naming point matters before any trip. The current C.T. Terrell Unit in Rosharon is a separate, distinct prison from an earlier facility that carried the "Terrell Unit" name and was renamed the Allan B. Polunsky Unit in 2001. They are not the same place. Families should confirm the exact unit on the TDCJ offender search before traveling.

## What Makes the Terrell Unit Different

-   Part of the Rosharon prison-farm cluster, where the Terrell, Ramsey, and Stringfellow Units sit on nearby farmland and their names are easy to confuse; the TDCJ inmate locator shows the exact unit a person is assigned to, and a nearby cluster unit is a different drive and a different visitation list
-   Carries a Type I geriatric mission, so part of the population is older or in long-term care, which can affect housing assignment and visiting format
-   Operates a large agricultural program on the surrounding land, along with a faith-based dorm and CHANGES/Pre-Release programming
-   Houses a G1-G3 custody mix plus Outside Trusty, so the visiting format follows the incarcerated person's classification rather than one unit-wide rule

## Visiting Hours and Procedures

The statewide rules above — approval, ID, prohibited items, and dress code — apply at the Terrell Unit. What follows is specific to this unit.

:::key-info
**Visiting days and hours:**
- Saturday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
- Sunday: 8:00 a.m. - 5:30 p.m.
:::

The unit's custody mix is the main reason one posted schedule does not mean one visiting experience. General-population housing typically uses standard contact visiting, while more restrictive housing can mean different room assignments or non-contact procedures on the same weekend. The format follows the incarcerated person's classification, so two families arriving the same morning may have different experiences.

The geriatric mission adds another variable. Some people housed here are in long-term or specialized care, and visits in those settings can follow different procedures than a standard general-population visit. Confirming the housing and visit format with the unit ahead of the trip avoids surprises at check-in.

## Getting There and Parking

The Terrell Unit is in a rural section of Brazoria County, south of Houston, on the farmland around Rosharon. The drive starts on highways and finishes on farm-to-market roads, and services thin out over the last stretch.

:::key-info
**From Houston:** South on Highway 288 toward the Rosharon area, then the final FM-road approach; often about 1 hour

**From Galveston:** West and southwest connectors toward the Rosharon area, then local farm-to-market roads; often about 1-1.5 hours

**Parking:** Visitor parking is available at the unit.

**Entry:** Check-in is at the unit's visitor processing point, where ID review and standard TDCJ screening happen before movement into the visiting area.
:::

There is no fixed-route bus to the unit, so the trip is by private vehicle. Rosharon is a small community with limited commercial services; the larger suburbs south of Houston — including Pearland and Angleton — are the nearest places for gas, food, and lodging before the final approach.

## Phones, Mail, and Video

Contact has a sequence to it. A phone number must be on the incarcerated person's approved contact list before calls can connect, and after intake or a transfer that approval can take time to process. Calls are placed by the incarcerated person on TDCJ's Securus phone system; a prison cannot be called into. Account setup, rates, and video options are covered in [Phone & Video Calls](/states/texas/guides/phone-video/).

## Nearby Services

Rosharon has limited commercial services. Pearland and the southern Houston suburbs are the nearest service centers for gas, food, groceries, and lodging before the prison-farm area, and options thin out significantly on the final farm-to-market approach.

For support beyond the unit itself, the Texas Inmate Families Association is a statewide nonprofit focused on education, advocacy, and prison-system navigation resources for families with someone in TDCJ custody.
