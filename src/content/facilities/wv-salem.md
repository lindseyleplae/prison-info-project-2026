## Overview

Salem Correctional Center, near Salem in Harrison County, is a **medium-security men's prison** holding about **340 men** in custody levels 1-3 (FY2024). People placed here are generally **within a few years of parole or discharge**, so it functions largely as a step-down and pre-release prison. It runs a **residential substance-abuse treatment (RSAT)** unit and supervised outside work crews. Salem also operates a **classification and intake unit**: since 2024 it has received men awaiting placement in the prison system and parole violators awaiting revocation hearings, which means a person may be held here **temporarily during intake** before transferring to another prison. The site opened in 1899 as the West Virginia Industrial Home for Girls, later became the Industrial Home for Youth, and was converted to an adult prison in **2013**. West Virginia has no death penalty, so there is no death row.

## What Makes Salem Different

- **It is a pre-release-oriented prison,** holding men who are generally within a few years of parole or discharge.
- **It runs a residential substance-abuse treatment (RSAT) unit** and supervised outside work crews.
- **It has a classification and intake unit,** so a person may be held here briefly during intake, or while awaiting a parole-revocation hearing, before moving to another facility.
- **It occupies the former West Virginia Industrial Home for Youth,** an adult prison since 2013.

## Visiting

The statewide WV DCR rules above — the approved list, the dress code, ID, and search rules — apply at Salem.

:::key-info
**Days and hours:** WVDCR does not post Salem's specific schedule; statewide, prison visits are Saturday and Sunday, 8:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m., with the facility setting its own times — call (304) 782-2371 to confirm

**How it works:** Visits are scheduled in advance by the incarcerated person, not walk-in; the visitor must be on the approved list first

**A person in the intake/classification unit** may not be eligible for regular visiting until classified — confirm eligibility by phone
:::

The full approval process is in [Visiting in West Virginia](/states/west-virginia/guides/visiting/).

## Getting There and Parking

The prison is on Industrial Boulevard near Salem, west of Clarksburg in north-central West Virginia.

:::key-info
**By car:** About 13 miles west of Clarksburg on U.S. Route 50; from Interstate 79, take U.S. 50 west toward Salem

**By transit:** There is no public transit to the facility; a private vehicle is the practical way to reach it
:::

Distances are approximate, based on map routing. Visitor parking is on site.

## Nearby Services

Clarksburg and Bridgeport, to the east, have the nearest cluster of food, gas, and lodging. The nearest 24/7 emergency room is **WVU Medicine United Hospital Center** in Bridgeport, the region's main hospital.

## Mail

Personal mail does **not** go to the prison. Since 2023, West Virginia routes personal letters to an **off-site scanning center in Maryland**, addressed with the person's name and **OID number**; the person receives the mail as **images on a tablet**, and photos must be sent through the tablet rather than mailed. **Legal mail, books, and magazines** go to the facility. Full addressing details are in [Mail & Packages](/states/west-virginia/guides/mail/).
