# Content Style Guide
## California Prison Family Resource Guide

This guide defines the standards for all content on this site. Follow these rules to maintain consistency as the site grows.

---

## Site Identity

### How We Describe the Site
Focus on **what the site does**, not who made it:

> "This site gathers information that families of incarcerated people need and presents it in one place, in plain language."

**Don't say:**
- ~~"Created by a family member..."~~
- ~~"Made by someone who's been through this..."~~
- Personal backstories or credentials

**Always include:**
- Not affiliated with CDCR or any government agency
- Information only, not legal advice

---

## Voice & Tone

### Our Voice
- **Factual and direct** — State information clearly without hedging
- **Supportive but not preachy** — Acknowledge challenges without being emotional
- **Practical** — Focus on what people need to do, not how they should feel
- **Impersonal** — The site speaks, not a person

### What We Sound Like
> "Mail the completed form to the Visiting Sergeant at the facility where the incarcerated person is housed."

> "All visitors go through the same entry process. The steps below apply to everyone."

### What We Don't Sound Like
> ~~"This part is hard. Give yourself grace on the drive home."~~

> ~~"It's okay to feel frustrated. You're not alone."~~

---

## Language Rules

### Referring to Incarcerated People

**Never overuse "loved one."** Vary the language:

| Use These | In This Context |
|-----------|-----------------|
| they/them | General references, continuing a thought |
| the incarcerated person | When clarity is needed, formal contexts |
| the person you're visiting | Visiting-specific content |
| the person you're writing to | Mail-specific content |
| them | After establishing who you're talking about |

**Example of good variation:**
> "Ask them to send you the form—they need to sign it first. The incarcerated person's signature confirms they want you on their visiting list."

### Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| loved one (overuse) | Repetitive, assumes relationship | Vary as shown above |
| hard, difficult | Emotional framing | Describe the actual challenge |
| overwhelming | Emotional | "complex," "has many steps" |
| frustrating | Emotional | State the fact without the feeling |
| give yourself grace | Therapy speak | Remove entirely |
| it's okay to feel... | Therapy speak | Remove entirely |
| you're not alone | Therapy speak | State facts about others in same situation |

### Therapy Speak to Avoid

Remove or reframe these patterns:
- "This is hard" → Just explain the thing
- "It's okay to [feel/be]..." → Don't tell people how to feel
- "Give yourself grace" → Remove
- "You're not alone" → "Thousands of families go through this process"
- "Take care of yourself" → Remove unless specifically relevant

## Content Rules

### What We Include
- Factual information from official sources
- Step-by-step procedures
- Rules and requirements
- Contact information and resources
- Tips based on community knowledge (clearly framed)

### What We Don't Include
- Legal advice (we provide information, not advice)
- Parenting advice (no "how to talk to your kids" or "don't punish them")
- Emotional support content (no "this part is hard")
- Personal opinions on the prison system
- Encouragement to break rules or work around the system

### Disclaimers

**Every guide page must have a disclaimer.** Use this standard language:

> ⚠️ DISCLAIMER
>
> This guide is for informational purposes only and is not legal advice. Information comes from publicly available sources, including official CDCR materials and community resources, and may be outdated or inaccurate. Policies change frequently. Always verify current rules with CDCR directly.

When we build the website, this will be a site-wide component that appears automatically.

## Formatting Standards

### Callout Boxes

Use colored boxes for specific purposes:

| Box Type | Color | Use For |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Warning (⚠️) | Orange | Important cautions, disclaimers |
| Tip (💡) | Green | Helpful suggestions |
| Info (ℹ️) | Blue | General information, standard procedures |
| Facility-specific (📍) | Red | "Check your facility page" callouts |
| Link (🔗) | Purple | External resources |
| Time-sensitive (⏱️) | Yellow | Deadlines, processing times |

### Box Titles

**Do:**
- STANDARD PROCEDURE
- MAILROOM DISCRETION
- HOW REJECTIONS WORK
- CHECK BEFORE YOU GO

**Don't:**
- THIS IS HARD
- THE FRUSTRATING REALITY
- A NOTE FIRST (vague)

### Headings

Use clear, descriptive headings:
- **Good:** "Documentation for Minors," "Rules for Children During Visits"
- **Bad:** "A Note About Kids," "Some Things to Consider"

## State-Specific Content

### Current: California (CDCR)
- Use "California Guide" in subtitles
- Reference CDCR specifically
- Include CDCR hotline numbers

### Future States
When expanding to other states:
- Create parallel structure (e.g., "Texas Guide for TDCJ State Prisons")
- Research that state's specific terminology
- Find equivalent resources and hotlines
- DO NOT copy California content and just change the state name

---

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any page, verify:

- [ ] Disclaimer is present (or will be added site-wide)
- [ ] No overuse of "loved one" — language is varied
- [ ] No therapy speak (hard, overwhelming, grace, etc.)
- [ ] No parenting advice
- [ ] No legal advice
- [ ] Sources are cited or noted
- [ ] Contact info/hotlines are current
- [ ] Formatting is consistent with other pages

---

## Updates & Maintenance

### When to Update Content
- When policies change (check CDCR site monthly)
- When vendors change (e.g., phone provider switches)
- When users report errors
- When new facilities open or close

### How to Note Updates
- Add "Last updated: [Month Year]" to page footer
- Keep a changelog for major updates
